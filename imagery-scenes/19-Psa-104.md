# Psalm 104
1: בָּרֲכִ֥י נַפְשִׁ֗י אֶת־ יְה֫וָ֥ה יְהוָ֣ה אֱ֭לֹהַי גָּדַ֣לְתָּ מְּאֹ֑ד ה֭וֹד וְהָדָ֣ר לָבָֽשְׁתָּ׃
2: עֹֽטֶה־ א֭וֹר כַּשַּׂלְמָ֑ה נוֹטֶ֥ה שָׁ֝מַ֗יִם כַּיְרִיעָֽה׃
3: הַ֥מְקָרֶֽה בַמַּ֗יִם עֲֽלִיּ֫וֹתָ֥יו הַשָּׂם־ עָבִ֥ים רְכוּב֑וֹ הַֽ֝מְהַלֵּ֗ךְ עַל־ כַּנְפֵי־ רֽוּחַ׃
4: עֹשֶׂ֣ה מַלְאָכָ֣יו רוּח֑וֹת מְ֝שָׁרְתָ֗יו אֵ֣שׁ לֹהֵֽט׃
5: יָֽסַד־ אֶ֭רֶץ עַל־ מְכוֹנֶ֑יהָ בַּל־ תִּ֝מּ֗וֹט עוֹלָ֥ם וָעֶֽד׃
6: תְּ֭הוֹם כַּלְּב֣וּשׁ כִּסִּית֑וֹ עַל־ הָ֝רִ֗ים יַֽעַמְדוּ־ מָֽיִם׃
7: מִן־ גַּעֲרָ֣תְךָ֣ יְנוּס֑וּן מִן־ ק֥וֹל רַֽ֝עַמְךָ֗ יֵחָפֵזֽוּן׃
8: יַעֲל֣וּ הָ֭רִים יֵרְד֣וּ בְקָע֑וֹת אֶל־ מְ֝ק֗וֹם זֶ֤ה ׀ יָסַ֬דְתָּ לָהֶֽם׃
9: גְּֽבוּל־ שַׂ֭מְתָּ בַּל־ יֽ͏ַעֲבֹר֑וּן בַּל־ יְ֝שׁוּב֗וּן לְכַסּ֥וֹת הָאָֽרֶץ׃
10: הַֽמְשַׁלֵּ֣חַ מַ֭עְיָנִים בַּנְּחָלִ֑ים בֵּ֥ין הָ֝רִ֗ים יְהַלֵּכֽוּן׃
11: יַ֭שְׁקוּ כָּל־ חַיְת֣וֹ שָׂדָ֑י יִשְׁבְּר֖וּ פְרָאִ֣ים צְמָאָֽם׃
12: עֲ֭לֵיהֶם עוֹף־ הַשָּׁמַ֣יִם יִשְׁכּ֑וֹן מִבֵּ֥ין עֳ֝פָאיִ֗ם יִתְּנוּ־ קֽוֹל׃
13: מַשְׁקֶ֣ה הָ֭רִים מֵעֲלִיּוֹתָ֑יו מִפְּרִ֥י מַ֝עֲשֶׂ֗יךָ תִּשְׂבַּ֥ע הָאָֽרֶץ׃
14: מַצְמִ֤יחַ חָצִ֨יר ׀ לַבְּהֵמָ֗ה וְ֭עֵשֶׂב לַעֲבֹדַ֣ת הָאָדָ֑ם לְה֥וֹצִיא לֶ֝֗חֶם מִן־ הָאָֽרֶץ׃
15: וְיַ֤יִן ׀ יְשַׂמַּ֬ח לְֽבַב־ אֱנ֗וֹשׁ לְהַצְהִ֣יל פָּנִ֣ים מִשָּׁ֑מֶן וְ֝לֶ֗חֶם לְֽבַב־ אֱנ֥וֹשׁ יִסְעָֽד׃
16: יִ֭שְׂבְּעוּ עֲצֵ֣י יְהוָ֑ה אַֽרְזֵ֥י לְ֝בָנ֗וֹן אֲשֶׁ֣ר נָטָֽע׃
17: אֲשֶׁר־ שָׁ֭ם צִפֳּרִ֣ים יְקַנֵּ֑נוּ חֲ֝סִידָ֗ה בְּרוֹשִׁ֥ים בֵּיתָֽהּ׃
18: הָרִ֣ים הַ֭גְּבֹהִים לַיְּעֵלִ֑ים סְ֝לָעִ֗ים מַחְסֶ֥ה לַֽשְׁפַנִּֽים׃
19: עָשָׂ֣ה יָ֭רֵחַ לְמוֹעֲדִ֑ים שֶׁ֝֗מֶשׁ יָדַ֥ע מְבוֹאֽוֹ׃
20: תָּֽשֶׁת־ חֹ֭שֶׁךְ וִ֣יהִי לָ֑יְלָה בּֽוֹ־ תִ֝רְמֹ֗שׂ כָּל־ חַיְתוֹ־ יָֽעַר׃
21: הַ֭כְּפִירִים שֹׁאֲגִ֣ים לַטָּ֑רֶף וּלְבַקֵּ֖שׁ מֵאֵ֣ל אָכְלָֽם׃
22: תִּזְרַ֣ח הַ֭שֶּׁמֶשׁ יֵאָסֵפ֑וּן וְאֶל־ מְ֝עוֹנֹתָ֗ם יִרְבָּצֽוּן׃
23: יֵצֵ֣א אָדָ֣ם לְפָעֳל֑וֹ וְֽלַעֲבֹ֖דָת֣וֹ עֲדֵי־ עָֽרֶב׃
24: מָֽה־ רַבּ֬וּ מַעֲשֶׂ֨יךָ ׀ יְֽהוָ֗ה כֻּ֭לָּם בְּחָכְמָ֣ה עָשִׂ֑יתָ מָלְאָ֥ה הָ֝אָ֗רֶץ קִנְיָנֶֽךָ׃
25: זֶ֤ה ׀ הַיָּ֥ם גָּדוֹל֮ וּרְחַ֪ב יָ֫דָ֥יִם שָֽׁם־ רֶ֭מֶשׂ וְאֵ֣ין מִסְפָּ֑ר חַיּ֥וֹת קְ֝טַנּ֗וֹת עִם־ גְּדֹלֽוֹת׃
26: שָׁ֭ם אֳנִיּ֣וֹת יְהַלֵּכ֑וּן לִ֝וְיָתָ֗ן זֶֽה־ יָצַ֥רְתָּ לְשַֽׂחֶק־ בּֽוֹ׃
27: כֻּ֭לָּם אֵלֶ֣יךָ יְשַׂבֵּר֑וּן לָתֵ֖ת אָכְלָ֣ם בְּעִתּֽוֹ׃
28: תִּתֵּ֣ן לָ֭הֶם יִלְקֹט֑וּן תִּפְתַּ֥ח יָֽ֝דְךָ֗ יִשְׂבְּע֥וּן טֽוֹב׃
29: תַּסְתִּ֥יר פָּנֶיךָ֮ יִֽבָּהֵ֫ל֥וּן תֹּסֵ֣ף ר֭וּחָם יִגְוָע֑וּן וְֽאֶל־ עֲפָרָ֥ם יְשׁוּבֽוּן׃
30: תְּשַׁלַּ֣ח ר֭וּחֲךָ יִבָּרֵא֑וּן וּ֝תְחַדֵּ֗שׁ פְּנֵ֣י אֲדָמָֽה׃
31: יְהִ֤י כְב֣וֹד יְהוָ֣ה לְעוֹלָ֑ם יִשְׂמַ֖ח יְהוָ֣ה בְּמַעֲשָֽׂיו׃
32: הַמַּבִּ֣יט לָ֭אָרֶץ וַתִּרְעָ֑ד יִגַּ֖ע בֶּהָרִ֣ים וְֽיֶעֱשָֽׁנוּ׃
33: אָשִׁ֣ירָה לַיהוָ֣ה בְּחַיָּ֑י אֲזַמְּרָ֖ה לֵאלֹהַ֣י בְּעוֹדִֽי׃
34: יֶעֱרַ֣ב עָלָ֣יו שִׂיחִ֑י אָ֝נֹכִ֗י אֶשְׂמַ֥ח בַּיהוָֽה׃
35: יִתַּ֤מּוּ חַטָּאִ֨ים ׀ מִן־ הָאָ֡רֶץ וּרְשָׁעִ֤ים ׀ ע֤וֹד אֵינָ֗ם בָּרֲכִ֣י נַ֭פְשִׁי אֶת־ יְהוָ֗ה הַֽלְלוּ־ יָֽהּ׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 104

## Scene 1: Praise for the Creator

> "Praise the Lord, my soul. Lord my God, you are very great; you are clothed with splendor and majesty." - Psalm 104:1 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist begins by addressing his soul to praise the Lord, acknowledging His greatness and majesty.
- Imagery:
  - The imagery of God being clothed with splendor and majesty reflects His glory and sovereignty.

## Scene 2: God's Divine Work in Creation

> "He wraps himself in light as with a garment; he stretches out the heavens like a tent and lays the beams of his upper chambers on their waters." - Psalm 104:2-3a (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist describes how God envelops Himself in light and stretches out the heavens like a tent. He also speaks of the pillars that support the upper chambers of God's abode.
- Imagery:
  - The image of God wrapping Himself in light demonstrates His radiant splendor and holiness. The stretching out of the heavens as a tent emphasizes God's power and dominion over the cosmos. The mention of the beams and upper chambers suggests the architectural grandeur of God's dwelling place.

## Scene 3: The Elements of Creation

> "He makes the clouds his chariot and rides on the wings of the wind. He makes winds his messengers, flames of fire his servants." - Psalm 104:3b-4 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist continues to extol God's works in creation, specifically highlighting the clouds, wind, and fire.
- Imagery:
  - The imagery of God using clouds as His chariot and riding on the wings of the wind symbolizes His majesty and control over nature. The mention of winds as messengers and flames of fire as servants further emphasizes God's dominion over the elements.

## Scene 4: The Foundations of the Earth

> "He set the earth on its foundations; it can never be moved. You covered it with the watery depths as with a garment; the waters stood above the mountains." - Psalm 104:5-6 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges God as the one who established the foundations of the earth and covered it with the waters.
- Imagery:
  - The image of God setting the earth on its unshakable foundations depicts His stability and order in creation. The metaphor of the earth covered by the watery depths like a garment emphasizes the power and vastness of God's creation.

## Scene 5: God's Control of the Waters

> "But at your rebuke the waters fled, at the sound of your thunder, they took to flight; they flowed over the mountains, they went down into the valleys, to the place you assigned for them." - Psalm 104:7-8 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist describes how God exercises His authority over the waters, causing them to retreat and flow according to His command.
- Imagery:
  - The imagery of God rebuking the waters and causing them to flee speaks of His power and control over the forces of nature. The depiction of water flowing over mountains and into valleys illustrates the extent of God's sovereign control and His ordained boundaries for the seas.

## Scene 6: God's Provision for All Creatures

> "You set a boundary they cannot cross; never again will they cover the earth. He makes springs pour water into the ravines; it flows between the mountains. They give water to all the beasts of the field; the wild donkeys quench their thirst." - Psalm 104:9-11 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges that God has set a permanent boundary for the waters. He also describes how God provides water from springs, flowing between the mountains, which sustains the animals of the field.
- Imagery:
  - The image of God setting a boundary for the waters symbolizes His control and order in creation. The depiction of springs pouring water into ravines and the water providing for the thirst of the wild animals highlights God's provision and care for all living creatures.

## Scene 7: Birds and Their Habitat

> "The birds of the sky nest by the waters; they sing among the branches." - Psalm 104:12 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist observes the habitat of birds and their joyful songs near the waters.
- Imagery:
  - The imagery of birds nesting by the waters evokes a sense of serenity and harmony in nature. The mention of birds singing among the branches emphasizes the beauty and vitality of creation.

## Scene 8: God's Provision for Animals

> "He waters the mountains from his upper chambers; the land is satisfied by the fruit of his work. He makes grass grow for the cattle, and plants for people to cultivate— bringing forth food from the earth: wine that gladdens human hearts, oil to make their faces shine, and bread that sustains their hearts." - Psalm 104:13-15 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist recognizes God's provision for both animals and humans, including water for the mountains, grass for cattle, and crops for cultivation.
- Imagery:
  - The image of God watering the mountains from His upper chambers depicts His involvement in every aspect of creation. The mention of grass, plants, wine, oil, and bread as gifts from God symbolizes His generosity and care for the nourishment and well-being of His creation.

## Scene 9: Trees and Their Significance

> "The trees of the Lord are well watered, the cedars of Lebanon that he planted. There the birds make their nests; the stork has its home in the junipers. The high mountains belong to the wild goats; the crags are a refuge for the hyrax." - Psalm 104:16-18 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist observes the lushness and benefits of the Lord's trees, specifically mentioning the cedars of Lebanon. He also highlights the habitat of birds, the mountains as dwelling places for wild goats, and the crags as a refuge for hyraxes.
- Imagery:
  - The imagery of well-watered trees symbolizes abundance and vitality. The mention of birds making nests and different animals finding shelter highlights the diverse and interconnected ecosystem that God has created.

## Scene 10: The Moon and the Seasons

> "He made the moon to mark the seasons, and the sun knows when to go down. You bring darkness, it becomes night, and all the beasts of the forest prowl." - Psalm 104:19-20 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges God's role in appointing the moon to mark the seasons and the sun's set time. He also notes the activities of the nocturnal animals during the night.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the moon marking the seasons signifies God's order and regularity in the cycles of nature. The reference to darkness becoming night and the activities of the beasts of the forest highlights the rhythm and balance in the natural world.

## Scene 11: God's Provision for Predators and Prey

> "The lions roar for their prey and seek their food from God. The sun rises, and they steal away; they return and lie down in their dens. Then people go out to their work, to their labor until evening." - Psalm 104:21-23 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist observes the behavior of lions seeking their food from God, as well as the sunrise and sunset. He also notes that while lions rest, humans engage in daily work.
- Imagery:
  - The imagery of lions roaring for their prey and seeking food from God illustrates the predator-prey dynamic in nature. The mention of the sun rising and setting depicts the cycle of day and night, which governs the activities of both humans and animals.

## Scene 12: The Magnificence of God's Works

> "How many are your works, Lord! In wisdom you made them all; the earth is full of your creatures." - Psalm 104:24 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist reflects on the multitude of God's works and acknowledges His wisdom in creating them all.
- Imagery:
  - The image of God's abundant works symbolizes His creativity and the sheer variety of life on earth. The acknowledgment of God's wisdom emphasizes His intelligence and purpose in designing and sustaining His creation.

## Scene 13: The Sea and Its Inhabitants

> "There is the sea, vast and spacious, teeming with creatures beyond number— living things both large and small. There the ships go to and fro, and Leviathan, which you formed to frolic there." - Psalm 104:25-26 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist marvels at the vastness of the sea and the multitude of creatures that inhabit it. He also mentions the ships that navigate the waters and Leviathan, a sea creature formed by God.
- Imagery:
  - The imagery of the sea as vast and spacious reveals the expansiveness and mystery of the marine world. The mention of creatures both large and small underscores the diversity and abundance of life in the oceans. The reference to ships evokes a sense of exploration and human interaction with the sea, while Leviathan represents the awe-inspiring and untamed creatures of the deep.

## Scene 14: God's Providential Care for All

> "All creatures look to you to give them their food at the proper time. When you give it to them, they gather it up; when you open your hand, they are satisfied with good things." - Psalm 104:27-28 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges that all creatures depend on God for their sustenance, and they find satisfaction in the provisions He gives.
- Imagery:
  - The image of all creatures looking to God for food emphasizes His role as the provider and sustainer of all living beings. The depiction of God opening His hand represents His generous and abundant provision for His creation.

## Scene 15: God's Life-Giving Spirit

> "When you hide your face, they are terrified; when you take away their breath, they die and return to the dust. When you send your Spirit, they are created, and you renew the face of the ground." - Psalm 104:29-30 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist recognizes that when God hides His face or withdraws His breath, life ceases. However, when God sends His Spirit, He renews and creates life.
- Imagery:
  - The imagery of God hiding His face and taking away breath symbolizes His role as the ultimate source of life. The mention of God sending His Spirit depicts His ongoing creative power and ability to breathe new life into the world.

## Scene 16: God's Glory Forever

> "May the glory of the Lord endure forever; may the Lord rejoice in his works— he who looks at the earth, and it trembles, who touches the mountains, and they smoke." - Psalm 104:31-32 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist concludes by expressing the desire for the Lord's glory to endure forever and for Him to find joy in His works. He also acknowledges God's power to bring about trembling and smoke from the earth and mountains.
- Imagery:
  - The imagery of God's enduring glory emphasizes His eternal nature and the lasting impact of His works. The depiction of the earth trembling and mountains smoking highlights His awesome power and ability to bring about both creation and destruction.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict the eudaimonic significance of God's creation and providence. They illustrate God's sovereignty, power, and wisdom in establishing and sustaining the natural world. The imagery evokes a sense of awe, beauty, and harmony in the Creator's works. It reminds believers of God's abundant provision, care, and ongoing creative power. Ultimately, Psalm 104 invites readers to praise and honor the Lord for His magnificent and life-sustaining works.
