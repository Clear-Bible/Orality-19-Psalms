# Psalm 50
1: מִזְמ֗וֹר לְאָ֫סָ֥ף אֵ֤ל ׀ אֱֽלֹהִ֡ים יְֽהוָ֗ה דִּבֶּ֥ר וַיִּקְרָא־ אָ֑רֶץ מִמִּזְרַח־ שֶׁ֝֗מֶשׁ עַד־ מְבֹאֽוֹ׃
2: מִצִּיּ֥וֹן מִכְלַל־ יֹ֗פִי אֱלֹהִ֥ים הוֹפִֽיעַ׃
3: יָ֤בֹ֥א אֱלֹהֵ֗ינוּ וְֽאַל־ יֶ֫חֱרַ֥שׁ אֵשׁ־ לְפָנָ֥יו תֹּאכֵ֑ל וּ֝סְבִיבָ֗יו נִשְׂעֲרָ֥ה מְאֹֽד׃
4: יִקְרָ֣א אֶל־ הַשָּׁמַ֣יִם מֵעָ֑ל וְאֶל־ הָ֝אָ֗רֶץ לָדִ֥ין עַמּֽוֹ׃
5: אִסְפוּ־ לִ֥י חֲסִידָ֑י כֹּרְתֵ֖י בְרִיתִ֣י עֲלֵי־ זָֽבַח׃
6: וַיַּגִּ֣ידוּ שָׁמַ֣יִם צִדְק֑וֹ כִּֽי־ אֱלֹהִ֓ים ׀ שֹׁפֵ֖ט ה֣וּא סֶֽלָה׃
7: שִׁמְעָ֤ה עַמִּ֨י ׀ וַאֲדַבֵּ֗רָה יִ֭שְׂרָאֵל וְאָעִ֣ידָה בָּ֑ךְ אֱלֹהִ֖ים אֱלֹהֶ֣יךָ אָנֹֽכִי׃
8: לֹ֣א עַל־ זְ֭בָחֶיךָ אוֹכִיחֶ֑ךָ וְעוֹלֹתֶ֖יךָ לְנֶגְדִּ֣י תָמִֽיד׃
9: לֹא־ אֶקַּ֣ח מִבֵּיתְךָ֣ פָ֑ר מִ֝מִּכְלְאֹתֶ֗יךָ עַתּוּדִֽים׃
10: כִּי־ לִ֥י כָל־ חַיְתוֹ־ יָ֑עַר בְּ֝הֵמ֗וֹת בְּהַרְרֵי־ אָֽלֶף׃
11: יָ֭דַעְתִּי כָּל־ ע֣וֹף הָרִ֑ים וְזִ֥יז שָׂ֝דַ֗י עִמָּדִֽי׃
12: אִם־ אֶ֭רְעַב לֹא־ אֹ֣מַר לָ֑ךְ כִּי־ לִ֥י תֵ֝בֵ֗ל וּמְלֹאָֽהּ׃
13: הַֽ֭אוֹכַל בְּשַׂ֣ר אַבִּירִ֑ים וְדַ֖ם עַתּוּדִ֣ים אֶשְׁתֶּֽה׃
14: זְבַ֣ח לֵאלֹהִ֣ים תּוֹדָ֑ה וְשַׁלֵּ֖ם לְעֶלְי֣וֹן נְדָרֶֽיךָ׃
15: וּ֭קְרָאֵנִי בְּי֣וֹם צָרָ֑ה אֲ֝חַלֶּצְךָ֗ וּֽתְכַבְּדֵֽנִי׃
16: וְלָ֤רָשָׁ֨ע ׀ אָ֘מַ֤ר אֱלֹהִ֗ים מַה־ לְּ֭ךָ לְסַפֵּ֣ר חֻקָּ֑י וַתִּשָּׂ֖א בְרִיתִ֣י עֲלֵי־ פִֽיךָ׃
17: וְ֭אַתָּה שָׂנֵ֣אתָ מוּסָ֑ר וַתַּשְׁלֵ֖ךְ דְּבָרַ֣י אַחֲרֶֽיךָ׃
18: אִם־ רָאִ֣יתָ גַ֭נָּב וַתִּ֣רֶץ עִמּ֑וֹ וְעִ֖ם מְנָאֲפִ֣ים חֶלְקֶֽךָ׃
19: פִּ֭יךָ שָׁלַ֣חְתָּ בְרָעָ֑ה וּ֝לְשׁוֹנְךָ֗ תַּצְמִ֥יד מִרְמָֽה׃
20: תֵּ֭שֵׁב בְּאָחִ֣יךָ תְדַבֵּ֑ר בְּבֶֽן־ אִ֝מְּךָ֗ תִּתֶּן־ דֹּֽפִי׃
21: אֵ֤לֶּה עָשִׂ֨יתָ ׀ וְֽהֶחֱרַ֗שְׁתִּי דִּמִּ֗יתָ הֱֽיוֹת־ אֶֽהְיֶ֥ה כָמ֑וֹךָ אוֹכִיחֲךָ֖ וְאֶֽעֶרְכָ֣ה לְעֵינֶֽיךָ׃
22: בִּֽינוּ־ נָ֣א זֹ֭את שֹׁכְחֵ֣י אֱל֑וֹהַּ פֶּן־ אֶ֝טְרֹ֗ף וְאֵ֣ין מַצִּֽיל׃
23: זֹבֵ֥חַ תּוֹדָ֗ה יְֽכַ֫בְּדָ֥נְנִי וְשָׂ֥ם דֶּ֑רֶךְ אַ֝רְאֶ֗נּוּ בְּיֵ֣שַׁע אֱלֹהִֽים׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 50

## Scene 1: The Mighty God Summoning His People

> "The Mighty One, God, the LORD, speaks and summons the earth from the rising of the sun to where it sets. From Zion, perfect in beauty, God shines forth." - Psalm 50:1-2 (WLC/NIV)

- Scene:
  - The Mighty God summons the earth, from the east where the sun rises to the west where it sets.
  - God's splendor shines forth from Zion, radiating beauty.

- Imagery:
  - God's voice resounds throughout the earth, calling all creation to attention.
  - God's splendor is likened to the rising sun, illuminating the earth with its brilliance.
  - Zion, the place of God's dwelling, is described as perfect in beauty, serving as the source of God's radiant presence.

## Scene 2: God Gathering His Faithful People

> "Our God comes and will not be silent; a fire devours before him, and around him a tempest rages. He summons the heavens above, and the earth, that he may judge his people." - Psalm 50:3-4 (WLC/NIV)

- Scene:
  - God approaches with overwhelming presence, as evidenced by a consuming fire and a raging storm.
  - God gathers heaven and earth, preparing to judge His people.

- Imagery:
  - God's arrival is depicted as a dynamic event, with a blazing fire and a turbulent storm accompanying His presence.
  - The gathering of the heavens and the earth symbolizes the comprehensive scope of God's authority and dominion.
  - The purpose of the gathering is to initiate judgment, signaling God's role as the righteous judge of His people.

## Scene 3: God's Address to His People

> "Gather to me this consecrated people, who made a covenant with me by sacrifice." - Psalm 50:5 (WLC/NIV)

- Scene:
  - God calls for His consecrated people, those who have made a covenant with Him through sacrifice, to gather.

- Imagery:
  - The people are described as consecrated, set apart for God's purposes and holy service.
  - The covenant with God is emphasized, signifying a committed relationship based on sacrificial devotion.

## Scene 4: The Heavenly Witness to God's Righteousness

> "And the heavens declare his righteousness, for God himself is judge." - Psalm 50:6 (WLC/NIV)

- Scene:
  - The heavens bear witness to God's righteousness, recognizing His role as the ultimate judge.

- Imagery:
  - The heavens are personified as witnesses, testifying to God's righteousness.
  - God's position as the judge implies His perfect understanding of truth and justice.

## Scene 5: God's Rebuke of the Wicked

> "Hear, my people, and I will speak, O Israel, and I will testify against you: I am God, your God. I do not rebuke you for your sacrifices or your burnt offerings, which are ever before me. I have no need of a bull from your stall or of goats from your pens, for every animal of the forest is mine, and the cattle on a thousand hills." - Psalm 50:7-10 (WLC/NIV)

- Scene:
  - God speaks and rebukes His people, Israel, addressing their misplaced reliance on sacrifices.
  - God emphasizes His lack of need for their offerings, as all creation already belongs to Him.

- Imagery:
  - God's address to His people emphasizes His unique relationship with them as their God.
  - The ineffectiveness of sacrifices is highlighted, suggesting that true worship requires sincere hearts and obedience, not merely outward rituals.
  - The imagery of ownership over all creatures conveys God's abundance and sovereignty.

## Scene 6: God's Ownership of All Creation

> "Every creature of the forest is mine, and the cattle on a thousand hills. I know every bird in the mountains, and the insects in the fields are mine." - Psalm 50:11-12 (WLC/NIV)

- Scene:
  - God asserts His ownership over all creatures, from the forest to the mountains, from birds to insects.

- Imagery:
  - The expansive range of God's ownership is depicted, encompassing all creatures in various habitats.
  - The intimate knowledge of every creature underscores God's intimate care and concern for His creation.

## Scene 7: God's Reliance on Humanity's Thanksgiving and Sacrifice

> "If I were hungry I would not tell you, for the world is mine, and all that is in it. Do I eat the flesh of bulls or drink the blood of goats? Sacrifice thank offerings to God, fulfill your vows to the Most High." - Psalm 50:12-14 (WLC/NIV)

- Scene:
  - God affirms His self-sufficiency, expressing His lack of need for food offerings from His people.
  - Instead, God desires their thanksgiving and faithfulness in fulfilling their vows.

- Imagery:
  - God's declaration of ownership extends to the entire world and its contents, emphasizing His self-sufficiency.
  - The rhetorical questions regarding consuming sacrifices underscore the inefficacy of external rituals alone.
  - Thanksgiving and faithfulness are presented as the desired responses, implying a deeper connection and gratitude towards God.

## Scene 8: The Call to Seek God's Presence

> "And call on me in the day of trouble; I will deliver you, and you will honor me." - Psalm 50:15 (WLC/NIV)

- Scene:
  - God invites His people to call upon Him in times of trouble, promising deliverance and honor.

- Imagery:
  - Seeking God's presence and calling upon Him symbolize dependence, trust, and acknowledgment of His sovereignty.
  - Deliverance is depicted as an act of divine intervention, leading to the resulting honor and praise to God.

## Scene 9: God's Rebuke of the Wicked and Their Hypocrisy

> "But to the wicked person, God says: 'What right have you to recite my laws or take my covenant on your lips? You hate my instruction and cast my words behind you. When you see a thief, you join with him; you throw in your lot with adulterers. You use your mouth for evil and harness your tongue to deceit. You sit and testify against your brother and slander your own mother’s son. When you did these things and I kept silent, you thought I was exactly like you. But I now arraign you and set my accusations before you.'" - Psalm 50:16-21 (WLC/NIV)

- Scene:
  - God addresses the wicked, reproaching them for their hypocrisy, lawlessness, and slanderous ways.
  - God reveals that their actions have not gone unnoticed, and a day of judgment is imminent.

- Imagery:
  - The wicked are characterized by their false piety, outwardly professing God's laws while disregarding them in their actions.
  - Their participation in theft and adultery alludes to their lack of moral integrity and commitment to godly principles.
  - The imagery of deceitful speech, false testimony, and slander highlights their malicious intent and harmful words.
  - The wicked's distorted perception of God's silence implies their misunderstanding of His patience, assuming His approval of their sinful ways.
  - God's accusation against the wicked emphasizes the impending judgment and the exposure of their wrongdoing.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict the mighty God summoning His people with His radiant presence, gathering them for judgment. The heavens bear witness to God's righteousness, while God rebukes the wicked for their misplaced reliance on sacrifices and their hypocritical practices. God asserts His ownership over all creation and calls His people to offer thanksgiving and fulfill their vows, seeking His presence in times of trouble. Divine deliverance is promised to the faithful, while the wicked are admonished for their hypocrisy and unrighteousness, facing the impending judgment of God. The imagery in these scenes conveys the eudaimonic significance of fear of the Lord, sincere worship, gratitude, obedience, trust, and acknowledgment of God's sovereignty and righteousness.
