# Psalm 91
1: יֹ֭שֵׁב בְּסֵ֣תֶר עֶלְי֑וֹן בְּצֵ֥ל שַׁ֝דַּ֗י יִתְלוֹנָֽן׃
2: אֹמַ֗ר לַֽ֭יהוָה מַחְסִ֣י וּמְצוּדָתִ֑י אֱ֝לֹהַ֗י אֶבְטַח־ בּֽוֹ׃
3: כִּ֤י ה֣וּא יַ֭צִּֽילְךָ מִפַּ֥ח יָק֗וּשׁ מִדֶּ֥בֶר הַוּֽוֹת׃
4: בְּאֶבְרָת֨וֹ ׀ יָ֣סֶךְ לָ֭ךְ וְתַֽחַת־ כְּנָפָ֣יו תֶּחְסֶ֑ה צִנָּ֖ה וְֽסֹחֵרָ֣ה אֲמִתּֽוֹ׃
5: לֹא־ תִ֭ירָא מִפַּ֣חַד לָ֑יְלָה מֵ֝חֵ֗ץ יָע֥וּף יוֹמָֽם׃
6: מִ֭דֶּבֶר בָּאֹ֣פֶל יַהֲלֹ֑ךְ מִ֝קֶּ֗טֶב יָשׁ֥וּד צָהֳרָֽיִם׃
7: יִפֹּ֤ל מִצִּדְּךָ֨ ׀ אֶ֗לֶף וּרְבָבָ֥ה מִימִינֶ֑ךָ אֵ֝לֶ֗יךָ לֹ֣א יִגָּֽשׁ׃
8: רַ֭ק בְּעֵינֶ֣יךָ תַבִּ֑יט וְשִׁלֻּמַ֖ת רְשָׁעִ֣ים תִּרְאֶֽה׃
9: כִּֽי־ אַתָּ֣ה יְהוָ֣ה מַחְסִ֑י עֶ֝לְי֗וֹן שַׂ֣מְתָּ מְעוֹנֶֽךָ׃
10: לֹֽא־ תְאֻנֶּ֣ה אֵלֶ֣יךָ רָעָ֑ה וְ֝נֶ֗גַע לֹא־ יִקְרַ֥ב בְּאָהֳלֶֽךָ׃
11: כִּ֣י מַ֭לְאָכָיו יְצַוֶּה־ לָּ֑ךְ לִ֝שְׁמָרְךָ֗ בְּכָל־ דְּרָכֶֽיךָ׃
12: עַל־ כַּפַּ֥יִם יִשָּׂא֑וּנְךָ פֶּן־ תִּגֹּ֖ף בָּאֶ֣בֶן רַגְלֶֽךָ׃
13: עַל־ שַׁ֣חַל וָפֶ֣תֶן תִּדְרֹ֑ךְ תִּרְמֹ֖ס כְּפִ֣יר וְתַנִּֽין׃
14: כִּ֤י בִ֣י חָ֭שַׁק וַאֲפַלְּטֵ֑הוּ אֲ֝שַׂגְּבֵ֗הוּ כִּֽי־ יָדַ֥ע שְׁמִֽי׃
15: יִקְרָאֵ֨נִי ׀ וְֽאֶעֱנֵ֗הוּ עִמּֽוֹ־ אָנֹכִ֥י בְצָרָ֑ה אֲ֝חַלְּצֵ֗הוּ וַֽאֲכַבְּדֵֽהוּ׃
16: אֹ֣רֶךְ יָ֭מִים אַשְׂבִּיעֵ֑הוּ וְ֝אַרְאֵ֗הוּ בִּֽישׁוּעָתִֽי׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 91

## Scene 1: The Shelter of the Almighty

> "Whoever dwells in the shelter of the Most High will rest in the shadow of the Almighty." - Psalm 91:1 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist describes a person seeking shelter in the presence of the Most High.
- Imagery:
  - Dwelling in the shelter of the Most High reflects a sense of security and protection.
  - Resting in the shadow of the Almighty suggests finding solace and peace amidst the challenges of life.

## Scene 2: The Refuge under God's Wings

> "I will say of the Lord, 'He is my refuge and my fortress, my God, in whom I trust.'" - Psalm 91:2 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist declares their trust in the Lord as their refuge and fortress.
- Imagery:
  - Finding refuge under God's wings symbolizes finding safety and protection like a bird under its parent's wings.
  - God being a fortress implies an impenetrable stronghold, a place of ultimate security.

## Scene 3: Deliverance from Deadly Traps

> "Surely he will save you from the fowler's snare and from the deadly pestilence." - Psalm 91:3 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist expresses confidence in God's deliverance from traps and dangers.
- Imagery:
  - God's saving from the fowler's snare portrays rescue from hidden traps and snares set by the enemy.
  - Deliverance from deadly pestilence signifies protection from life-threatening diseases or calamities.

## Scene 4: God's Faithfulness as a Shield

> "He will cover you with his feathers, and under his wings you will find refuge; his faithfulness will be your shield and rampart." - Psalm 91:4 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist emphasizes God's faithfulness as a protective shield and rampart.
- Imagery:
  - God covering with His feathers and providing refuge under His wings represents His nurturing and caring presence.
  - God's faithfulness being a shield and rampart implies unwavering protection and defense against all harm.

## Scene 5: Fearlessness in the Face of Danger

> "You will not fear the terror of night, nor the arrow that flies by day." - Psalm 91:5 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist declares the absence of fear in the presence of danger.
- Imagery:
  - Not fearing the terror of the night reveals a sense of security and confidence even in the darkness.
  - Not fearing the arrow that flies by day signifies courage and trust in God's protection amidst visible threats.

## Scene 6: Protection from Plagues

> "Nor the pestilence that stalks in the darkness, nor the plague that destroys at midday." - Psalm 91:6 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist affirms God's safeguarding from plagues and calamities.
- Imagery:
  - God protecting from the pestilence that stalks in the darkness represents His ability to shield from hidden dangers.
  - God rescuing from the plague that destroys at midday shows His power over even the most devastating and unexpected disasters.

## Scene 7: Security under God's Wings

> "A thousand may fall at your side, ten thousand at your right hand, but it will not come near you." - Psalm 91:7 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges the protection of God even in the midst of chaos and destruction.
- Imagery:
  - Despite the multitude falling around, God ensures that harm will not come near, illustrating His watchful care and divine intervention.

## Scene 8: Witnessing the Destruction of the Wicked

> "You will only observe with your eyes and see the punishment of the wicked." - Psalm 91:8 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist describes the privilege of witnessing the downfall of the wicked.
- Imagery:
  - Observing with one's eyes and seeing the punishment of the wicked signifies the vindication of the righteous and the justice of God.

## Scene 9: God's Protection from Harm

> "If you say, 'The Lord is my refuge,' and you make the Most High your dwelling..." - Psalm 91:9 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist encourages taking refuge in God and making Him the dwelling place.
- Imagery:
  - Making the Lord one's refuge and dwelling affirms the constant protection and provision God offers to those who seek Him.

## Scene 10: Angelic Guard and Protection

> "For he will command his angels concerning you to guard you in all your ways." - Psalm 91:11 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist proclaims God's command for angels to protect and guard.
- Imagery:
  - God commanding His angels to guard reflects divine intervention and supernatural protection in every aspect of life.

## Scene 11: Assurance in Overcoming Dangerous Situations

> "You will tread on the lion and the cobra; you will trample the great lion and the serpent." - Psalm 91:13 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist assures victory over dangerous creatures.
- Imagery:
  - Treading on the lion and the cobra signifies triumph over powerful and threatening enemies.
  - Trampling the great lion and the serpent symbolizes domination over dangerous and deceptive forces.

## Scene 12: God's Faithfulness and Salvation

> "Because he loves me," says the Lord, "I will rescue him; I will protect him, for he acknowledges my name." - Psalm 91:14 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist highlights God's faithfulness and salvation for those who love Him and acknowledge His name.
- Imagery:
  - God's rescue and protection stem from His love, showing the depth of His care for His people.

## Scene 13: Answered Prayer and Abundant Life

> "He will call on me, and I will answer him; I will be with him in trouble, I will deliver him and honor him." - Psalm 91:15 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist depicts the assurance of answered prayers and intimate companionship with God.
- Imagery:
  - God answering when called upon portrays His attentiveness and willingness to provide assistance in times of trouble.
  - His promise to be with in trouble and deliver honors the relationship between the Psalmist and God.

## Scene 14: Long Life and Divine Salvation

> "With long life I will satisfy him and show him my salvation." - Psalm 91:16 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist declares the granting of long life and divine salvation.
- Imagery:
  - Satisfaction with long life speaks of a fulfilled and blessed existence.
  - Seeing God's salvation reflects experiencing the fullness of His deliverance and redemption.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict the believer's unshakable trust in God, finding refuge and protection in His presence. They highlight the eudaimonic significance of seeking shelter under God's wings, experiencing His faithfulness as a shield, and the freedom from fear in the face of danger. The imagery of divine deliverance from deadly traps, protection from plagues, witnessing the destruction of the wicked, and the assurance of answered prayers emphasizes God's constant care and intervention in the lives of His people. Ultimately, the Psalm portrays a sense of security, peace, and abundant life that comes from dwelling in the presence of the Most High.
