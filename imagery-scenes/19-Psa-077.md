# Psalm 77
1: לַמְנַצֵּ֥חַ עַֽל־ לְאָסָ֥ף מִזְמֽוֹר׃
2: קוֹלִ֣י אֶל־ אֱלֹהִ֣ים וְאֶצְעָ֑קָה קוֹלִ֥י אֶל־ אֱ֝לֹהִ֗ים וְהַאֲזִ֥ין אֵלָֽי׃
3: בְּי֥וֹם צָרָתִי֮ אֲדֹנָ֪י דָּ֫רָ֥שְׁתִּי יָדִ֤י ׀ לַ֣יְלָה נִ֭גְּרָה וְלֹ֣א תָפ֑וּג מֵאֲנָ֖ה הִנָּחֵ֣ם נַפְשִֽׁי׃
4: אֶזְכְּרָ֣ה אֱלֹהִ֣ים וְאֶֽהֱמָיָ֑ה אָשִׂ֓יחָה ׀ וְתִתְעַטֵּ֖ף רוּחִ֣י סֶֽלָה׃
5: אָ֭חַזְתָּ שְׁמֻר֣וֹת עֵינָ֑י נִ֝פְעַ֗מְתִּי וְלֹ֣א אֲדַבֵּֽר׃
6: חִשַּׁ֣בְתִּי יָמִ֣ים מִקֶּ֑דֶם שְׁ֝נ֗וֹת עוֹלָמִֽים׃
7: אֶֽזְכְּרָ֥ה נְגִינָתִ֗י בַּ֫לָּ֥יְלָה עִם־ לְבָבִ֥י אָשִׂ֑יחָה וַיְחַפֵּ֥שׂ רוּחִֽי׃
8: הַֽ֭לְעוֹלָמִים יִזְנַ֥ח ׀ אֲדֹנָ֑י וְלֹֽא־ יֹסִ֖יף לִרְצ֣וֹת עֽוֹד׃
9: הֶאָפֵ֣ס לָנֶ֣צַח חַסְדּ֑וֹ גָּ֥מַר אֹ֝֗מֶר לְדֹ֣ר וָדֹֽר׃
10: הֲשָׁכַ֣ח חַנּ֣וֹת אֵ֑ל אִם־ קָפַ֥ץ בְּ֝אַ֗ף רַחֲמָ֥יו סֶֽלָה׃
11: וָ֭אֹמַר חַלּ֣וֹתִי הִ֑יא שְׁ֝נ֗וֹת יְמִ֣ין עֶלְיֽוֹן׃
12: מַֽעַלְלֵי־ יָ֑הּ כִּֽי־ אֶזְכְּרָ֖ה מִקֶּ֣דֶם פִּלְאֶֽךָ׃
13: וְהָגִ֥יתִי בְכָל־ פָּעֳלֶ֑ךָ וּֽבַעֲלִ֖ילוֹתֶ֣יךָ אָשִֽׂיחָה׃
14: אֱ֭לֹהִים בַּקֹּ֣דֶשׁ דַּרְכֶּ֑ךָ מִי־ אֵ֥ל גָּ֝ד֗וֹל כֵּֽאלֹהִֽים׃
15: אַתָּ֣ה הָ֭אֵל עֹ֣שֵׂה פֶ֑לֶא הוֹדַ֖עְתָּ בָעַמִּ֣ים עֻזֶּֽךָ׃
16: גָּאַ֣לְתָּ בִּזְר֣וֹעַ עַמֶּ֑ךָ בְּנֵי־ יַעֲקֹ֖ב וְיוֹסֵ֣ף סֶֽלָה׃
17: רָ֘א֤וּךָ מַּ֨יִם ׀ אֱֽלֹהִ֗ים רָא֣וּךָ מַּ֣יִם יָחִ֑ילוּ אַ֝֗ף יִרְגְּז֥וּ תְהֹמֽוֹת׃
18: זֹ֤רְמוּ מַ֨יִם ׀ עָב֗וֹת ק֭וֹל נָתְנ֣וּ שְׁחָקִ֑ים אַף־ חֲ֝צָצֶ֗יךָ יִתְהַלָּֽכוּ׃
19: ק֤וֹל רַעַמְךָ֨ ׀ בַּגַּלְגַּ֗ל הֵאִ֣ירוּ בְרָקִ֣ים תֵּבֵ֑ל רָגְזָ֖ה וַתִּרְעַ֣שׁ הָאָֽרֶץ׃
20: בַּיָּ֤ם דַּרְכֶּ֗ךָ בְּמַ֣יִם רַבִּ֑ים וְ֝עִקְּבוֹתֶ֗יךָ לֹ֣א נֹדָֽעוּ׃
21: נָחִ֣יתָ כַצֹּ֣אן עַמֶּ֑ךָ בְּֽיַד־ מֹשֶׁ֥ה וְאַהֲרֹֽן׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 77

## Scene 1: The Distressed Cry of the Psalmist

> I cried out to God for help; I cried out to God to hear me.
> - Psalm 77:1 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist is in distress and crying out to God for help.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's cry represents his deep longing for God's intervention and deliverance in his difficult situation.

## Scene 2: Remembering God's Faithfulness

> When I was in distress, I sought the Lord; at night I stretched out untiring hands, and I would not be comforted.
> - Psalm 77:2 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist recalls a time of distress where he earnestly sought the Lord's help.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's untiring hands stretching out at night symbolize his desperate and persistent seeking of comfort and relief from God.

## Scene 3: Frustration and Disquietude

> I remembered you, God, and I groaned; I meditated, and my spirit grew faint.
> - Psalm 77:3 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist remembers God and expresses his grief and frustration.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's groaning and faint spirit demonstrate his inner turmoil and disquietude in the face of his distress.

## Scene 4: Sleeplessness and Questioning

> You kept my eyes from closing; I was too troubled to speak.
> - Psalm 77:4 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist describes his sleeplessness and inability to speak due to his troubled state.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's eyes being kept from closing and his inability to speak signify his restless and agitated state of mind, unable to find peace or articulate his thoughts.

## Scene 5: Reflecting on God's Faithfulness

> I thought about ancient times; I considered years long past.
> - Psalm 77:5 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist reflects on the past and considers the faithfulness of God throughout history.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's thoughts on ancient times and years long past evoke a sense of looking back and reminiscing on God's faithfulness and deliverance in the past.

## Scene 6: Questions of God's Rejection

> At night I remember my music; I meditate in my heart, and my spirit ponders.

> Will the Lord reject forever? Will he never show his favor again?
> - Psalm 77:6-7 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist remembers his former joy and expresses doubts about God's favor.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's remembrance of his music and meditation in his heart reflects his longing for the joy and intimacy with God that he once experienced. His questions about God's rejection and favor highlight his doubts and uncertainties.

## Scene 7: Remembering God's Character and Works

> Has his unfailing love vanished forever? Has his promise failed for all time?

> Has God forgotten to be merciful? Has he in anger withheld his compassion?
> - Psalm 77:8-9 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist questions whether God's character and promises have changed.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's questions about God's unfailing love, promise, mercy, and compassion reveal his fear and concern that God's attributes may have disappeared or been withheld.

## Scene 8: Wrestling with Doubt

> Then I thought, "To this I will appeal: the years when the Most High stretched out his right hand.
> - Psalm 77:10 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist seeks reassurance by recalling the times when God displayed His power.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's appeal to the years when the Most High stretched out His right hand signifies his effort to find comfort and faith by remembering God's mighty acts and deliverance in the past.

## Scene 9: Reflecting on God's Mighty Deeds

> I will remember the deeds of the Lord; yes, I will remember your miracles of long ago.
> - Psalm 77:11 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist determines to remember and recount the acts of the Lord.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's resolve to remember the deeds and miracles of the Lord indicates his decision to focus on God's past works and the reassurance they bring.

## Scene 10: The Power and Holiness of God

> I will consider all your works and meditate on all your mighty deeds.
> - Psalm 77:12 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist commits to reflecting on God's works and mighty deeds.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's intention to consider and meditate on God's works and mighty deeds emphasizes the importance of recognizing God's power and holiness in bringing about deliverance.

## Scene 11: God's Way in the Sanctuary

> Your ways, God, are holy. What god is as great as our God?
> - Psalm 77:13 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges that God's ways are holy and unmatched.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's exaltation of God's holiness and greatness signifies the recognition that God's ways are perfect, highlighting the unmatched greatness of the one true God.

## Scene 12: God's Miraculous Acts

> You are the God who performs miracles; you display your power among the peoples.
> - Psalm 77:14 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges God's ability to perform miracles and display His power.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's proclamation of God's miraculous acts and display of power emphasizes the awe-inspiring nature of God's works, particularly in the sight of the people.

## Scene 13: God's Redemption of His People

> With your mighty arm you redeemed your people, the descendants of Jacob and Joseph.
> - Psalm 77:15 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist recognizes God's redemption of His people, the descendants of Jacob and Joseph.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's reference to God's mighty arm and the redemption of the people of Jacob and Joseph symbolizes God's strength and faithfulness in delivering His chosen ones.

## Scene 14: Waters Parting Before God

> The waters saw you, God, the waters saw you and writhed; the very depths were convulsed.
> - Psalm 77:16 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist describes the awe-inspiring event of God's intervention in parting waters.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's depiction of the waters seeing God and writhing, and the depths convulsing, portrays the power and authority of God over creation, particularly in the miraculous parting of the waters.

## Scene 15: God in the Clouds and Thunder

> The clouds poured down water, the heavens resounded with thunder; your arrows flashed back and forth.
> - Psalm 77:17 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist describes the manifestation of God's presence in the clouds and the thunder.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's imagery of the clouds pouring down water, the heavens resounding with thunder, and arrows flashing back and forth portrays the majestic and awe-inspiring nature of God's presence and intervention in the world.

## Scene 16: Thunder and Lightning in the Whirlwind

> Your thunder was heard in the whirlwind, your lightning lit up the world; the earth trembled and quaked.
> - Psalm 77:18 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist depicts the impact of God's thunder and lightning in a powerful whirlwind.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's portrayal of God's thunder being heard in the whirlwind, His lightning lighting up the world, and the earth trembling and quaking conveys the overwhelming power and magnitude of God's presence.

## Scene 17: God's Guiding Hand

> Your path led through the sea, your way through the mighty waters, though your footprints were not seen.
> - Psalm 77:19 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist recognizes God's leading and guidance through the sea and mighty waters.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's reference to God's path through the sea and mighty waters and the absence of visible footprints indicates that God's ways often surpass human understanding, emphasizing His sovereignty and guidance.

## Scene 18: God's Shepherding Care

> You led your people like a flock by the hand of Moses and Aaron.
> - Psalm 77:20 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges God's shepherding care and leadership of His people.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's imagery of God leading His people like a flock by the hand of Moses and Aaron signifies God's provision, protection, and guidance, likening His care to that of a shepherd.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict the psalmist's distress and longing for God's intervention. The psalmist wrestles with doubt and questions God's favor, but ultimately finds comfort and reassurance by remembering God's character, mighty deeds, and past deliverances. The imagery of God's power, holiness, miraculous acts, and shepherding care highlights His unmatched greatness and faithfulness. Through reflecting on these images, the psalmist is able to find solace and strengthen his faith in the midst of his troubles.
