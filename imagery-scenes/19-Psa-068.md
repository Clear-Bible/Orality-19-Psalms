# Psalm 68
1: לַמְנַצֵּ֥חַ לְדָוִ֗ד מִזְמ֥וֹר שִֽׁיר׃
2: יָק֣וּם אֱ֭לֹהִים יָפ֣וּצוּ אוֹיְבָ֑יו וְיָנ֥וּסוּ מְ֝שַׂנְאָ֗יו מִפָּנָֽיו׃
3: כְּהִנְדֹּ֥ף עָשָׁ֗ן תִּ֫נְדֹּ֥ף כְּהִמֵּ֣ס דּ֭וֹנַג מִפְּנֵי־ אֵ֑שׁ יֹאבְד֥וּ רְ֝שָׁעִ֗ים מִפְּנֵ֥י אֱלֹהִֽים׃
4: וְֽצַדִּיקִ֗ים יִשְׂמְח֣וּ יַֽ֭עַלְצוּ לִפְנֵ֥י אֱלֹהִ֗ים וְיָשִׂ֥ישׂוּ בְשִׂמְחָֽה׃
5: שִׁ֤ירוּ ׀ לֵֽאלֹהִים֮ זַמְּר֪וּ שְׁ֫מ֥וֹ סֹ֡לּוּ לָרֹכֵ֣ב בָּ֭עֲרָבוֹת בְּיָ֥הּ שְׁמ֗וֹ וְעִלְז֥וּ לְפָנָֽיו׃
6: אֲבִ֣י יְ֭תוֹמִים וְדַיַּ֣ן אַלְמָנ֑וֹת אֱ֝לֹהִ֗ים בִּמְע֥וֹן קָדְשֽׁוֹ׃
7: אֱלֹהִ֤ים ׀ מ֘וֹשִׁ֤יב יְחִידִ֨ים ׀ בַּ֗יְתָה מוֹצִ֣יא אֲ֭סִירִים בַּכּוֹשָׁר֑וֹת אַ֥ךְ ס֝וֹרֲרִ֗ים שָׁכְנ֥וּ צְחִיחָֽה׃
8: אֱֽלֹהִ֗ים בְּ֭צֵאתְךָ לִפְנֵ֣י עַמֶּ֑ךָ בְּצַעְדְּךָ֖ בִֽישִׁימ֣וֹן סֶֽלָה׃
9: אֶ֤רֶץ רָעָ֨שָׁה ׀ אַף־ שָׁמַ֣יִם נָטְפוּ֮ מִפְּנֵ֪י אֱלֹ֫הִ֥ים זֶ֥ה סִינַ֑י מִפְּנֵ֥י אֱ֝לֹהִ֗ים אֱלֹהֵ֥י יִשְׂרָאֵֽל׃
10: גֶּ֣שֶׁם נְ֭דָבוֹת תָּנִ֣יף אֱלֹהִ֑ים נַחֲלָתְךָ֥ וְ֝נִלְאָ֗ה אַתָּ֥ה כֽוֹנַנְתּֽ͏ָהּ׃
11: חַיָּתְךָ֥ יָֽשְׁבוּ־ בָ֑הּ תָּ֤כִ֥ין בְּטוֹבָתְךָ֖ לֶעָנִ֣י אֱלֹהִֽים׃
12: אֲדֹנָ֥י יִתֶּן־ אֹ֑מֶר הַֽ֝מְבַשְּׂר֗וֹת צָבָ֥א רָֽב׃
13: מַלְכֵ֣י צְ֭בָאוֹת יִדֹּד֣וּן יִדֹּד֑וּן וּנְוַת בַּ֝֗יִת תְּחַלֵּ֥ק שָׁלָֽל׃
14: אִֽם־ תִּשְׁכְּבוּן֮ בֵּ֪ין שְׁפַ֫תָּ֥יִם כַּנְפֵ֣י י֭וֹנָה נֶחְפָּ֣ה בַכֶּ֑סֶף וְ֝אֶבְרוֹתֶ֗יהָ בִּֽירַקְרַ֥ק חָרֽוּץ׃
15: בְּפָ֘רֵ֤שׂ שַׁדַּ֓י מְלָ֘כִ֤ים בָּ֗הּ תַּשְׁלֵ֥ג בְּצַלְמֽוֹן׃
16: הַר־ אֱ֭לֹהִים הַר־ בָּשָׁ֑ן הַ֥ר גַּ֝בְנֻנִּ֗ים הַר־ בָּשָֽׁן׃
17: לָ֤מָּה ׀ תְּֽרַצְּדוּן֮ הָרִ֪ים גַּבְנֻ֫נִּ֥ים הָהָ֗ר חָמַ֣ד אֱלֹהִ֣ים לְשִׁבְתּ֑וֹ אַף־ יְ֝הוָ֗ה יִשְׁכֹּ֥ן לָנֶֽצַח׃
18: רֶ֤כֶב אֱלֹהִ֗ים רִבֹּתַ֣יִם אַלְפֵ֣י שִׁנְאָ֑ן אֲדֹנָ֥י בָ֝֗ם סִינַ֥י בַּקֹּֽדֶשׁ׃
19: עָ֘לִ֤יתָ לַמָּר֨וֹם ׀ שָׁ֘בִ֤יתָ שֶּׁ֗בִי לָקַ֣חְתָּ מַ֭תָּנוֹת בָּאָדָ֑ם וְאַ֥ף ס֝וֹרְרִ֗ים לִשְׁכֹּ֤ן ׀ יָ֬הּ אֱלֹהִֽים׃
20: בָּ֤ר֣וּךְ אֲדֹנָי֮ י֤וֹם ׀ י֥וֹם יַֽעֲמָס־ לָ֗נוּ הָ֘אֵ֤ל יְֽשׁוּעָתֵ֬נוּ סֶֽלָה׃
21: הָ֤אֵ֣ל ׀ לָנוּ֮ אֵ֤ל לְֽמוֹשָׁ֫ע֥וֹת וְלֵיהוִ֥ה אֲדֹנָ֑י לַ֝מָּ֗וֶת תּוֹצָאֽוֹת׃
22: אַךְ־ אֱלֹהִ֗ים יִמְחַץ֮ רֹ֤אשׁ אֹ֫יְבָ֥יו קָדְקֹ֥ד שֵׂעָ֑ר מִ֝תְהַלֵּ֗ךְ בַּאֲשָׁמָֽיו׃
23: אָמַ֣ר אֲ֭דֹנָי מִבָּשָׁ֣ן אָשִׁ֑יב אָ֝שִׁ֗יב מִֽמְּצֻל֥וֹת יָֽם׃
24: לְמַ֤עַן ׀ תִּֽמְחַ֥ץ רַגְלְךָ֗ בְּ֫דָ֥ם לְשׁ֥וֹן כְּלָבֶ֑יךָ מֵאֹיְבִ֥ים מִנֵּֽהוּ׃
25: רָא֣וּ הֲלִיכוֹתֶ֣יךָ אֱלֹהִ֑ים הֲלִ֘יכ֤וֹת אֵלִ֖י מַלְכִּ֣י בַקֹּֽדֶשׁ׃
26: קִדְּמ֣וּ שָׁ֭רִים אַחַ֣ר נֹגְנִ֑ים בְּת֥וֹךְ עֲ֝לָמ֗וֹת תּוֹפֵפֽוֹת׃
27: בְּֽ֭מַקְהֵלוֹת בָּרְכ֣וּ אֱלֹהִ֑ים יְ֝הוָ֗ה מִמְּק֥וֹר יִשְׂרָאֵֽל׃
28: שָׁ֤ם בִּנְיָמִ֨ן ׀ צָעִ֡יר רֹדֵ֗ם שָׂרֵ֣י יְ֭הוּדָה רִגְמָתָ֑ם שָׂרֵ֥י זְ֝בֻל֗וּן שָׂרֵ֥י נַפְתָּלִֽי׃
29: צִוָּ֥ה אֱלֹהֶ֗יךָ עֻ֫זֶּ֥ךָ עוּזָּ֥ה אֱלֹהִ֑ים ז֝֗וּ פָּעַ֥לְתָּ לָּֽנוּ׃
30: מֵֽ֭הֵיכָלֶךָ עַל־ יְרוּשָׁלָ֑͏ִם לְךָ֤ יוֹבִ֖ילוּ מְלָכִ֣ים שָֽׁי׃
31: גְּעַ֨ר חַיַּ֪ת קָנֶ֡ה עֲדַ֤ת אַבִּירִ֨ים ׀ בְּעֶגְלֵ֬י עַמִּ֗ים מִתְרַפֵּ֥ס בְּרַצֵּי־ כָ֑סֶף בִּזַּ֥ר עַ֝מִּ֗ים קְרָב֥וֹת יֶחְפָּֽצוּ׃
32: יֶאֱתָ֣יוּ חַ֭שְׁמַנִּים מִנִּ֣י מִצְרָ֑יִם כּ֥וּשׁ תָּרִ֥יץ יָ֝דָ֗יו לֵאלֹהִֽים׃
33: מַמְלְכ֣וֹת הָ֭אָרֶץ שִׁ֣ירוּ לֵאלֹהִ֑ים זַמְּר֖וּ אֲדֹנָ֣י סֶֽלָה׃
34: לָ֭רֹכֵב בִּשְׁמֵ֣י שְׁמֵי־ קֶ֑דֶם הֵ֥ן יִתֵּ֥ן בְּ֝קוֹלוֹ ק֣וֹל עֹֽז׃
35: תְּנ֥וּ עֹ֗ז לֵֽאלֹ֫הִ֥ים עַֽל־ יִשְׂרָאֵ֥ל גַּאֲוָת֑וֹ וְ֝עֻזּ֗וֹ בַּשְּׁחָקִֽים׃
36: נ֤וֹרָ֥א אֱלֹהִ֗ים מִֽמִּקְדָּ֫שֶׁ֥יךָ אֵ֤ל יִשְׂרָאֵ֗ל ה֤וּא נֹתֵ֨ן ׀ עֹ֖ז וְתַעֲצֻמ֥וֹת לָעָ֗ם בָּר֥וּךְ אֱלֹהִֽים׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 68

## Scene 1: God's Victorious Procession

> "May God arise, may his enemies be scattered; may his foes flee before him." - Psalm 68:1 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - God is depicted as rising up to assert His power and authority.
  - His enemies and foes are scattered and fleeing before Him.

- Imagery:
  - God's rise implies His sovereignty and superiority over all.
  - The scattering of enemies symbolizes God's triumph over opposition.
  - The fleeing of foes portrays the fear and inability to stand against God's might.

## Scene 2: God's Deliverance of the Righteous

> "As smoke is blown away by the wind, may you blow them away; as wax melts before the fire, may the wicked perish before God." - Psalm 68:2 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - God is portrayed as blowing away the wicked like smoke blown away by the wind.
  - The wicked are depicted as melting before God like wax melts before the fire.

- Imagery:
  - Blowing away the wicked symbolizes God's ability to remove them effortlessly.
  - The melting of the wicked represents their complete destruction and annihilation.
  
## Scene 3: God's Provision and Protection for the Weak

> "But may the righteous be glad and rejoice before God; may they be happy and joyful." - Psalm 68:3 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The righteous are called to rejoice and be glad before God.

- Imagery:
  - The righteous are depicted as experiencing joy and happiness in the presence of God.
  
## Scene 4: God's Care for the Orphans and Widows

> "Sing to God, sing in praise of his name, extol him who rides on the clouds; rejoice before him—his name is the Lord. A father to the fatherless, a defender of widows, is God in his holy dwelling." - Psalm 68:4-5 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - God is praised for His caring nature towards orphans and widows.
  - He is acknowledged as a father to the fatherless and a defender of widows.

- Imagery:
  - God is portrayed as riding on the clouds, signifying His majesty and power.
  - His care for orphans and widows emphasizes His loving and protective character.
  
## Scene 5: God's Manifestation at Mount Sinai

> "God sets the lonely in families, he leads out the prisoners with singing; but the rebellious live in a sun-scorched land." - Psalm 68:6 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - God is depicted as placing the lonely in families and leading prisoners out with singing.
  - The rebellious are described as living in a sun-scorched land.

- Imagery:
  - Placing the lonely in families signifies God's restoration of broken relationships and provision of community.
  - Leading prisoners out with singing portrays God's deliverance and freedom.
  - The sun-scorched land represents the harsh consequences faced by those who rebel against God's ways.
  
## Scene 6: God's March of Victory

> "When you, God, went out before your people, when you marched through the wilderness, the earth shook, the heavens poured down rain, before God, the One of Sinai, before God, the God of Israel." - Psalm 68:7-8 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist recalls God's march before His people through the wilderness.
  - The earth shook and the heavens poured down rain in the presence of God.

- Imagery:
  - God's march through the wilderness symbolizes His guidance and protection of His people.
  - The shaking of the earth and the pouring down of rain represent God's power and authority.
  
## Scene 7: God's Abundant Provision

> "You gave abundant showers, O God; you refreshed your weary inheritance." - Psalm 68:9 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - God is acknowledged for providing abundant showers and refreshing His weary inheritance.

- Imagery:
  - The abundance of showers denotes God's provision and prosperity.
  - The refreshing of the weary inheritance signifies restoration and renewal.

## Scene 8: God's Unstoppable Victory

> "Your people settled in it, and from your bounty, God, you provided for the poor." - Psalm 68:10 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - God's people have settled in the land, and God is praised for providing for the poor.

- Imagery:
  - God's provision for the poor demonstrates His care and compassion.
  
## Scene 9: God's Defeat of the Enemy

> "The Lord announces the word, and the women who proclaim it are a mighty throng: 'Kings and armies flee in haste; the women at home divide the plunder.'" - Psalm 68:11-12 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The Lord's victory over the enemy is proclaimed with the participation of a mighty throng.
  - Kings and armies are seen fleeing in haste, and the women at home divide the plunder.

- Imagery:
  - The Lord's announcement signifies His decisive authority and power.
  - The fleeing of kings and armies portrays their defeat in the face of God's might.
  - The women dividing the plunder illustrates the spoils of victory being shared among those left at home.

## Scene 10: God's Dwelling on Mount Zion

> "Even while you sleep among the sheep pens, the wings of my dove are sheathed with silver, its feathers with shining gold." - Psalm 68:13 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - God is described as dwelling on Mount Zion while the psalmist affirms the beauty of a dove with silver wings and golden feathers.

- Imagery:
  - God's dwelling on Mount Zion suggests His presence and reign in His holy city.
  - The dove with silver wings and golden feathers symbolizes purity, beauty, and divine favor.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict God's victorious procession, His deliverance of the righteous, His provision and protection for the weak, His care for orphans and widows, His manifestation at Mount Sinai, His march of victory, His abundant provision, His unstoppable victory, His defeat of the enemy, and His dwelling on Mount Zion. Each image conveys the eudaimonic significance of God's power, protection, provision, restoration, and care for His people. The Psalm evokes a sense of awe and gratitude towards God for His mighty deeds and loving kindness.
