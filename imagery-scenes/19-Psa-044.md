# Psalm 44
1: לַמְנַצֵּ֬חַ לִבְנֵי־ קֹ֬רַח מַשְׂכִּֽיל׃
2: אֱלֹהִ֤ים ׀ בְּאָזְנֵ֬ינוּ שָׁמַ֗עְנוּ אֲבוֹתֵ֥ינוּ סִפְּרוּ־ לָ֑נוּ פֹּ֥עַל פָּעַ֥לְתָּ בִֽ֝ימֵיהֶ֗ם בִּ֣ימֵי קֶֽדֶם׃
3: אַתָּ֤ה ׀ יָדְךָ֡ גּוֹיִ֣ם ה֭וֹרַשְׁתָּ וַתִּטָּעֵ֑ם תָּרַ֥ע לְ֝אֻמִּ֗ים וַֽתְּשַׁלְּחֵֽם׃
4: כִּ֤י לֹ֪א בְחַרְבָּ֡ם יָ֥רְשׁוּ אָ֗רֶץ וּזְרוֹעָם֮ לֹא־ הוֹשִׁ֪יעָ֫ה לָּ֥מוֹ כִּֽי־ יְמִֽינְךָ֣ וּ֭זְרוֹעֲךָ וְא֥וֹר פָּנֶ֗יךָ כִּ֣י רְצִיתָֽם׃
5: אַתָּה־ ה֣וּא מַלְכִּ֣י אֱלֹהִ֑ים צַ֝וֵּ֗ה יְשׁוּע֥וֹת יַעֲקֹֽב׃
6: בְּ֭ךָ צָרֵ֣ינוּ נְנַגֵּ֑חַ בְּ֝שִׁמְךָ֗ נָב֥וּס קָמֵֽינוּ׃
7: כִּ֤י לֹ֣א בְקַשְׁתִּ֣י אֶבְטָ֑ח וְ֝חַרְבִּ֗י לֹ֣א תוֹשִׁיעֵֽנִי׃
8: כִּ֣י ה֭וֹשַׁעְתָּנוּ מִצָּרֵ֑ינוּ וּמְשַׂנְאֵ֥ינוּ הֱבִישֽׁוֹתָ׃
9: בֵּֽ֭אלֹהִים הִלַּלְ֣נוּ כָל־ הַיּ֑וֹם וְשִׁמְךָ֓ ׀ לְעוֹלָ֖ם נוֹדֶ֣ה סֶֽלָה׃
10: אַף־ זָ֭נַחְתָּ וַתַּכְלִימֵ֑נוּ וְלֹא־ תֵ֝צֵ֗א בְּצִבְאוֹתֵֽינוּ׃
11: תְּשִׁיבֵ֣נוּ אָ֭חוֹר מִנִּי־ צָ֑ר וּ֝מְשַׂנְאֵ֗ינוּ שָׁ֣סוּ לָֽמוֹ׃
12: תִּ֭תְּנֵנוּ כְּצֹ֣אן מַאֲכָ֑ל וּ֝בַגּוֹיִ֗ם זֵרִיתָֽנוּ׃
13: תִּמְכֹּֽר־ עַמְּךָ֥ בְלֹא־ ה֑וֹן וְלֹ֥א־ רִ֝בִּ֗יתָ בִּמְחִירֵיהֶֽם׃
14: תְּשִׂימֵ֣נוּ חֶ֭רְפָּה לִשְׁכֵנֵ֑ינוּ לַ֥עַג וָ֝קֶ֗לֶס לִסְבִיבוֹתֵֽינוּ׃
15: תְּשִׂימֵ֣נוּ מָ֭שָׁל בַּגּוֹיִ֑ם מְנֽוֹד־ רֹ֝֗אשׁ בַּל־ אֻמִּֽים׃
16: כָּל־ הַ֭יּוֹם כְּלִמָּתִ֣י נֶגְדִּ֑י וּבֹ֖שֶׁת פָּנַ֣י כִּסָּֽתְנִי׃
17: מִ֭קּוֹל מְחָרֵ֣ף וּמְגַדֵּ֑ף מִפְּנֵ֥י א֝וֹיֵ֗ב וּמִתְנַקֵּֽם׃
18: כָּל־ זֹ֣את בָּ֭אַתְנוּ וְלֹ֣א שְׁכַחֲנ֑וּךָ וְלֹֽא־ שִׁ֝קַּ֗רְנוּ בִּבְרִיתֶֽךָ׃
19: לֹא־ נָס֣וֹג אָח֣וֹר לִבֵּ֑נוּ וַתֵּ֥ט אֲשֻׁרֵ֗ינוּ מִנִּ֥י אָרְחֶֽךָ׃
20: כִּ֣י דִ֭כִּיתָנוּ בִּמְק֣וֹם תַּנִּ֑ים וַתְּכַ֖ס עָלֵ֣ינוּ בְצַלְמָֽוֶת׃
21: אִם־ שָׁ֭כַחְנוּ שֵׁ֣ם אֱלֹהֵ֑ינוּ וַנִּפְרֹ֥שׂ כַּ֝פֵּ֗ינוּ לְאֵ֣ל זָֽר׃
22: הֲלֹ֣א אֱ֭לֹהִים יַֽחֲקָר־ זֹ֑את כִּֽי־ ה֥וּא יֹ֝דֵ֗עַ תַּעֲלֻמ֥וֹת לֵֽב׃
23: כִּֽי־ עָ֭לֶיךָ הֹרַ֣גְנוּ כָל־ הַיּ֑וֹם נֶ֝חְשַׁ֗בְנוּ כְּצֹ֣אן טִבְחָֽה׃
24: ע֤וּרָה ׀ לָ֖מָּה תִישַׁ֥ן ׀ אֲדֹנָ֑י הָ֝קִ֗יצָה אַל־ תִּזְנַ֥ח לָנֶֽצַח׃
25: לָֽמָּה־ פָנֶ֥יךָ תַסְתִּ֑יר תִּשְׁכַּ֖ח עָנְיֵ֣נוּ וְֽלַחֲצֵֽנוּ׃
26: כִּ֤י שָׁ֣חָה לֶעָפָ֣ר נַפְשֵׁ֑נוּ דָּבְקָ֖ה לָאָ֣רֶץ בִּטְנֵֽנוּ׃
27: ק֭וּמָֽה עֶזְרָ֣תָה לָּ֑נוּ וּ֝פְדֵ֗נוּ לְמַ֣עַן חַסְדֶּֽךָ׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 44

## Scene 1: Acknowledging God's Past Deliverance

> We have heard it with our ears, O God; our ancestors have told us what you did in their days, in days long ago.

- Scene:
  - The psalmist recalls the stories passed down from their ancestors about God's past deliverance. 
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist hears with their ears the accounts of God's deeds from their ancestors.
  - The psalmist emphasizes the ancient nature of these stories, highlighting their significance in days long ago.

## Scene 2: God's Presence in Battle

> With your hand you drove out the nations and planted our ancestors; you crushed the peoples and made our ancestors flourish.

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges God's active presence in battle, driving out the nations before their ancestors.
- Imagery:
  - God's hand is depicted as a symbol of power and intervention, driving out the nations.
  - The planting of ancestors symbolizes God's establishment of their people in the land.
  - The crushing of peoples represents God's defeat of their enemies, allowing their ancestors to flourish.

## Scene 3: Trust in God's Salvation

> It was not by their sword that they won the land, nor did their arm bring them victory; it was your right hand, your arm, and the light of your face, for you loved them.

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges that their ancestors' victory was not achieved by their own strength, but through God's intervention and love.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist contrasts the reliance on human weapons (sword and arm) with the power of God's right hand and arm.
  - The light of God's face symbolizes His favor and guidance.
  - The psalmist emphasizes God's love for their ancestors, highlighting the source of their victory.

## Scene 4: Boasting in God's Name

> You are my King and my God, who decrees victories for Jacob. Through you we push back our enemies; through your name we trample our foes.

- Scene:
  - The psalmist declares their allegiance to God as their King and acknowledges His role in granting victories to their people.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist identifies God as their King and God, emphasizing His authority and sovereignty.
  - Pushing back enemies and trampling foes represent the subjugation and defeat of their adversaries through God's power.
  - The psalmist highlights the use of God's name in these victories, emphasizing the authority and power attributed to His name.

## Scene 5: Rejection and Humiliation

> I put no trust in my bow, my sword does not bring me victory; but you give us victory over our enemies, you put our adversaries to shame.

- Scene:
  - The psalmist rejects relying on human weapons and attributes victory to God alone, acknowledging His ability to shame their adversaries.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist contrasts their lack of trust in their own weapons (bow and sword) with the certainty of victory given by God.
  - The giving of victory and the putting of adversaries to shame highlight God's power in reversing the fortunes of their enemies.

## Scene 6: Constant Devotion to God

> In God we make our boast all day long, and we will praise your name forever.

- Scene:
  - The psalmist expresses their unwavering devotion to God, boasting in Him and committing to praise His name forever.
- Imagery:
  - Boasting in God emphasizes the psalmist's confidence and trust in Him.
  - The commitment to praise God's name forever highlights their enduring worship and devotion.

## Scene 7: Rejection and Disgrace

> But now you have rejected and humbled us; you no longer go out with our armies.

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges a shift in their circumstances, experiencing rejection, humiliation, and the absence of God's presence in battle.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist expresses a sense of abandonment through God's rejection and the absence of His presence with their armies.

## Scene 8: Persecution and Defeat

> You made us retreat before the enemy, and our adversaries have plundered us. You gave us up to be devoured like sheep and have scattered us among the nations.

- Scene:
  - The psalmist describes their experience of persecution, retreat, defeat, and scattering among the nations.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist portrays the enemy triumphing over them, plundering and devouring them like defenseless sheep.
  - The scattering among the nations represents the loss of their identity and territory, suffering the consequences of defeat.

## Scene 9: God's People Despised and Ashamed

> You sold your people for a pittance, gaining nothing from their sale. You have made us a reproach to our neighbors, the scorn and derision of those around us.

- Scene:
  - The psalmist laments the devaluation and scorn their people experience, as well as the reproach they face from their neighbors.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist portrays God selling His people for a pittance, highlighting the perceived lack of value their enemies place on them.
  - The reproach from their neighbors symbolizes the shame and disgrace they endure, becoming objects of scorn and derision.

## Scene 10: Remembering God's Covenant

> You have made us a byword among the nations; the peoples shake their heads at us. I live in disgrace all day long, and my face is covered with shame because of the enemy, who is bent on revenge.

- Scene:
  - The psalmist bemoans the mockery and disgrace they endure among the nations, feeling the weight of shame and the enemy's desire for revenge.
- Imagery:
  - Being a byword among the nations emphasizes the widespread derision they face, becoming a symbol of humiliation and mockery.
  - The shaking of heads and living in disgrace symbolize the public humiliation and ongoing shame they experience.
  - The enemy's desire for revenge highlights the threat they pose, creating a sense of constant fear and vulnerability.

## Scene 11: Plea for God's Intervention and Mercy

> All this came upon us, though we had not forgotten you; we had not been false to your covenant.

- Scene:
  - The psalmist appeals to God, acknowledging their faithfulness to Him and questioning why they have suffered despite their commitment.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist declares their steadfastness in not forgetting God and remaining faithful to His covenant.
  - The psalmist raises the question of why they have faced such hardship despite their faithfulness, highlighting the unjust nature of their suffering.

## Scene 12: Crushed and Brought Low

> Our hearts had not turned back; our feet had not strayed from your path. But you crushed us and made us a haunt for jackals; you covered us over with deep darkness. 

- Scene:
  - The psalmist affirms their dedication to God's path, yet laments their crushing and the resulting desolation they experience.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist emphasizes their unwavering commitment to God, remaining faithful to His path.
  - The crushing and becoming a haunt for jackals symbolize the severe affliction and desolation they endure.
  - The deep darkness represents the emotional and spiritual darkness that surrounds them, intensifying their feelings of despair.

## Scene 13: Cry for Redemption and Deliverance

> If we had forgotten the name of our God or spread out our hands to a foreign god, would not God have discovered it, since he knows the secrets of the heart?

- Scene:
  - The psalmist appeals to God's knowledge of their hearts, asserting their loyalty and innocence in times of distress.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist presents a hypothetical situation where they would have turned away from God or embraced foreign gods, asserting their loyalty and devotion in contrast.
  - God's knowledge of the secrets of the heart underscores the psalmist's innocence and loyalty, placing trust in God's understanding of their true allegiance.

## Scene 14: Slaughtered Like Sheep

> Yet for your sake we face death all day long; we are considered as sheep to be slaughtered.

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges their constant facing of death and being seen as expendable, likening themselves to sheep destined for slaughter.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's attribution of death to God's sake highlights their loyalty and willingness to endure suffering for His purposes.
  - The comparison to sheep being slaughtered emphasizes their vulnerability and powerlessness in the face of persecution and death.

## Scene 15: Wake Up, O Lord!

> Awake, Lord! Why do you sleep? Rouse yourself! Do not reject us forever. Why do you hide your face and forget our misery and oppression?

- Scene:
  - The psalmist pleads for God's active intervention, questioning His perceived inactivity and absence in their distress.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist implores God to wake up, illustrating their belief in His ability to intervene and bring deliverance.
  - God's perceived sleep and hiding of His face symbolize His apparent disinterest or distance, intensifying the psalmist's desperation and longing for His presence and help.
  - The mention of misery and oppression reiterates the psalmist's state of suffering and their expectation that God will address it.

## Scene 16: Arise for Our Help

> We are brought down to the dust; our bodies cling to the ground. Rise up and help us; rescue us because of your unfailing love.

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges their weakened state and implores God to rise up and rescue them, appealing to His unfailing love.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist describes their state of being brought down to the dust and their bodies clinging to the ground, emphasizing their helplessness and vulnerability.
  - The call for God to rise up suggests that He has the power to raise them from their lowly state and provide assistance.
  - The plea for rescue based on God's unfailing love expresses their trust in His compassion and faithful care.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict a journey of faith and lament. The psalmist starts by acknowledging God's past deliverance and the reliance of their ancestors on His intervention. They boast in God's name, acknowledging His authority and power. However, the psalmist then describes a shift in their circumstances, experiencing rejection, humiliation, and defeat. They express their unwavering devotion to God and confusion over their suffering despite their faithfulness. The psalmist pleads for God's intervention, questioning His apparent inactivity and absence. They long for redemption and deliverance, appealing to God's knowledge of their hearts and His unfailing love. The imagery throughout the psalm conveys the eudaimonic significance of trust, devotion, and dependence on God in the face of adversity, as well as the longing for His intervention and restoration.
