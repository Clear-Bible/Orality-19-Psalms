# Psalm 116
1: אָ֭הַבְתִּי כִּֽי־ יִשְׁמַ֥ע ׀ יְהוָ֑ה אֶת־ ק֝וֹלִ֗י תַּחֲנוּנָֽי׃
2: כִּֽי־ הִטָּ֣ה אָזְנ֣וֹ לִ֑י וּבְיָמַ֥י אֶקְרָֽא׃
3: אֲפָפ֤וּנִי ׀ חֶבְלֵי־ מָ֗וֶת וּמְצָרֵ֣י שְׁא֣וֹל מְצָא֑וּנִי צָרָ֖ה וְיָג֣וֹן אֶמְצָֽא׃
4: וּבְשֵֽׁם־ יְהוָ֥ה אֶקְרָ֑א אָנָּ֥ה יְ֝הוָ֗ה מַלְּטָ֥ה נַפְשִֽׁי׃
5: חַנּ֣וּן יְהֹוָ֣ה וְצַדִּ֑יק וֵ֖אלֹהֵ֣ינוּ מְרַחֵֽם׃
6: שֹׁמֵ֣ר פְּתָאיִ֣ם יְהֹוָ֑ה דַּ֝לּוֹתִ֗י וְלִ֣י יְהוֹשִֽׁיעַ׃
7: שׁוּבִ֣י נַ֭פְשִׁי לִמְנוּחָ֑יְכִי כִּֽי־ יְ֝הוָ֗ה גָּמַ֥ל עָלָֽיְכִי׃
8: כִּ֤י חִלַּ֥צְתָּ נַפְשִׁ֗י מִ֫מָּ֥וֶת אֶת־ עֵינִ֥י מִן־ דִּמְעָ֑ה אֶת־ רַגְלִ֥י מִדֶּֽחִי׃
9: אֶ֭תְהַלֵּךְ לִפְנֵ֣י יְהוָ֑ה בְּ֝אַרְצ֗וֹת הַֽחַיִּֽים׃
10: הֶ֭אֱמַנְתִּי כִּ֣י אֲדַבֵּ֑ר אֲ֝נִ֗י עָנִ֥יתִי מְאֹֽד׃
11: אֲ֭נִי אָמַ֣רְתִּי בְחָפְזִ֑י כָּֽל־ הָאָדָ֥ם כֹּזֵֽב׃
12: מָֽה־ אָשִׁ֥יב לַיהוָ֑ה כָּֽל־ תַּגְמוּל֥וֹהִי עָלָֽי׃
13: כּוֹס־ יְשׁוּע֥וֹת אֶשָּׂ֑א וּבְשֵׁ֖ם יְהוָ֣ה אֶקְרָֽא׃
14: נְ֭דָרַי לַיהוָ֣ה אֲשַׁלֵּ֑ם נֶגְדָה־ נָּ֝֗א לְכָל־ עַמּֽוֹ׃
15: יָ֭קָר בְּעֵינֵ֣י יְהוָ֑ה הַ֝מָּ֗וְתָה לַחֲסִידָֽיו׃
16: אֽ͏ָנָּ֣ה יְהוָה֮ כִּֽי־ אֲנִ֪י עַ֫בְדֶּ֥ךָ אֲֽנִי־ עַ֭בְדְּךָ בֶּן־ אֲמָתֶ֑ךָ פִּ֝תַּ֗חְתָּ לְמוֹסֵרָֽי׃
17: לְֽךָ־ אֶ֭זְבַּח זֶ֣בַח תּוֹדָ֑ה וּבְשֵׁ֖ם יְהוָ֣ה אֶקְרָֽא׃
18: נְ֭דָרַי לַיהוָ֣ה אֲשַׁלֵּ֑ם נֶגְדָה־ נָּ֝֗א לְכָל־ עַמּֽוֹ׃
19: בְּחַצְר֤וֹת ׀ בֵּ֤ית יְהוָ֗ה בְּֽת֘וֹכֵ֤כִי יְֽרוּשָׁלִָ֗ם הַֽלְלוּ־ יָֽהּ׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 116

## Scene 1: Thankful Deliverance

> "I love the Lord, for he heard my voice; he heard my cry for mercy. Because he turned his ear to me, I will call on him as long as I live." - Psalm 116:1-2 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist declaring their love for God because He has heard their voice and cry for mercy.
- Imagery:
  - God turning His ear towards the psalmist, symbolizing His attentive and compassionate nature.
  - The psalmist's cry for mercy being heard by God, representing God's willingness to listen and help in times of need.

## Scene 2: Tangled in Death's Cords

> "The cords of death entangled me, the anguish of the grave came over me; I was overcome by distress and sorrow." - Psalm 116:3 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist describing a moment of great distress and sorrow, feeling trapped and entangled in the cords of death.
- Imagery:
  - The cords of death symbolizing the imminent threat and power of death.
  - The anguish of the grave representing the overwhelming fear and despair that comes with the prospect of death.
  - The psalmist being overcome by distress and sorrow, highlighting the emotional and psychological toll of their situation.

## Scene 3: The Name of the Lord

> "Then I called on the name of the Lord: 'Lord, save me!'" - Psalm 116:4 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist calling on the name of the Lord and pleading for salvation.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist verbally invoking the name of the Lord, signifying their recognition of God's sovereignty and power.
  - The plea for salvation emphasizing the psalmist's belief in God's ability to deliver them from their troubles.

## Scene 4: The Lord is Gracious and Righteous

> "The Lord is gracious and righteous; our God is full of compassion." - Psalm 116:5 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Acknowledgment of God's character as gracious, righteous, and compassionate.
- Imagery:
  - The Lord being described as gracious and righteous, highlighting His kind and just nature.
  - God being full of compassion, signifying His deep empathy and concern for His people.

## Scene 5: The Lord Preserves the Simple-hearted

> "The Lord protects the unwary; when I was brought low, he saved me." - Psalm 116:6 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Recognition of God's protection and salvation for the simple-hearted and vulnerable.
- Imagery:
  - The Lord protecting the unwary, indicating His role as a guardian and shield for those who are vulnerable.
  - God saving the psalmist when they were brought low, illustrating His ability to lift up those who are in distress.

## Scene 6: Return to Your Rest, O Soul

> "Return to your rest, my soul, for the Lord has been good to you." - Psalm 116:7 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist encouraging their own soul to find rest and peace in God's goodness.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist speaking to their own soul, portraying a sense of internal struggle and the need for comfort.
  - The call to return to rest signifies the psalmist's desire for inner calm and a reminder of God's faithfulness.

## Scene 7: The Psalmist's Deliverance

> "For you, Lord, have delivered me from death, my eyes from tears, my feet from stumbling." - Psalm 116:8 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Gratitude expressed for being delivered from death, tears, and stumbling.
- Imagery:
  - God delivering the psalmist from death, representing His power over life and death.
  - The psalmist's eyes being delivered from tears, symbolizing consolation and relief from sorrow and pain.
  - God protecting the psalmist's feet from stumbling, portraying His guidance and support in navigating life's challenges.

## Scene 8: Walking in the Land of the Living

> "that I may walk before the Lord in the land of the living." - Psalm 116:9 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Aspiration to live a life of faithfulness and obedience before the Lord.
- Imagery:
  - Walking before the Lord, indicating a desire to live in His presence and according to His ways.
  - The land of the living representing a life filled with blessings, joy, and meaningful experiences.

## Scene 9: Trusting in the Lord's Faithfulness

> "I trusted in the Lord when I said, 'I am greatly afflicted.'" - Psalm 116:10 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Testimony of trust in the Lord during times of affliction.
- Imagery:
  - Trusting in the Lord, illustrating the psalmist's unwavering belief in God's faithfulness and reliability.
  - The acknowledgement of being greatly afflicted, emphasizing the psalmist's vulnerability and dependence on God.

## Scene 10: Precious in the Sight of the Lord

> "In my alarm I said, 'Everyone is a liar.'" - Psalm 116:11 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Expressing distrust in others and recognizing the trustworthiness of God.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist feeling alarmed, suggesting a state of distress and confusion caused by the deceitful actions of others.
  - The declaration that everyone is a liar, highlighting the recognition that human beings may not always be reliable or truthful.
  - Implication that God, in contrast, is trustworthy and faithful.

## Scene 11: Offering a Cup of Salvation

> "What shall I return to the Lord for all his goodness to me? I will lift up the cup of salvation and call on the name of the Lord." - Psalm 116:12-13 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - A response of gratitude for God's goodness and a commitment to worship Him.
- Imagery:
  - Questioning what can be given to the Lord in return for His goodness, illustrating a recognition of His abundant blessings.
  - Lifting up the cup of salvation, symbolizing the act of offering worship and praise to God.
  - The call to invoke the name of the Lord, representing an act of reverence and devotion.

## Scene 12: Fulfilling Vows to God

> "I will fulfill my vows to the Lord in the presence of all his people." - Psalm 116:14 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - A determination to fulfill promises made to God.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist committing to fulfill their vows, indicating a sense of loyalty and obedience to God.
  - The presence of all His people representing a public declaration and accountability in honoring those vows.

## Scene 13: Precious in the Eyes of the Lord

> "Precious in the sight of the Lord is the death of his faithful servants." - Psalm 116:15 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Acknowledgment of the value God places on the lives and deaths of His faithful servants.
- Imagery:
  - The death of His faithful servants being precious in God's sight, signifying the deep significance and honor He bestows upon their lives and their ultimate transition to eternity with Him.

## Scene 14: Grateful Praise and Ongoing Commitment

> "Truly I am your servant, Lord; I serve you just as my mother did; you have freed me from my chains. I will sacrifice a thank offering to you and call on the name of the Lord." - Psalm 116:16-17 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Profound declaration of servanthood to the Lord, gratitude for deliverance, and continued worship.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist claiming to be a servant of the Lord, signifying their devotion and commitment to His will.
  - Mention of their mother serving the Lord, highlighting a generational legacy of faithfulness and worship.
  - God freeing the psalmist from chains, depicting liberation from various forms of bondage and oppression.
  - Promising to sacrifice a thank offering, representing an act of worship and gratitude towards God.
  - The continued calling on the name of the Lord, emphasizing ongoing reliance on His presence and power.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict the psalmist's journey from distress and despair to a place of profound gratitude and faithfulness. Through vivid imagery, the psalmist expresses their recognition of God's compassionate nature, His deliverance from adversity, and the eudaimonic significance of trusting in Him. The psalmist portrays God as a protector, a guide, and a source of rest and salvation. The scenes also highlight the psalmist's response of worship and commitment, acknowledging the preciousness of their relationship with God and their desire to live a life that honors Him.
