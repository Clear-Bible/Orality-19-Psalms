# Psalm 103
1: לְדָוִ֨ד ׀ בָּרֲכִ֣י נַ֭פְשִׁי אֶת־ יְהוָ֑ה וְכָל־ קְ֝רָבַ֗י אֶת־ שֵׁ֥ם קָדְשֽׁוֹ׃
2: בָּרֲכִ֣י נַ֭פְשִׁי אֶת־ יְהוָ֑ה וְאַל־ תִּ֝שְׁכְּחִ֗י כָּל־ גְּמוּלָֽיו׃
3: הַסֹּלֵ֥חַ לְכָל־ עֲוֺנֵ֑כִי הָ֝רֹפֵ֗א לְכָל־ תַּחֲלֻאָֽיְכִי׃
4: הַגּוֹאֵ֣ל מִשַּׁ֣חַת חַיָּ֑יְכִי הַֽ֝מְעַטְּרֵ֗כִי חֶ֣סֶד וְרַחֲמִֽים׃
5: הַמַּשְׂבִּ֣יַע בַּטּ֣וֹב עֶדְיֵ֑ךְ תִּתְחַדֵּ֖שׁ כַּנֶּ֣שֶׁר נְעוּרָֽיְכִי׃
6: עֹשֵׂ֣ה צְדָק֣וֹת יְהוָ֑ה וּ֝מִשְׁפָּטִ֗ים לְכָל־ עֲשׁוּקִֽים׃
7: יוֹדִ֣יעַ דְּרָכָ֣יו לְמֹשֶׁ֑ה לִבְנֵ֥י יִ֝שְׂרָאֵ֗ל עֲלִילֽוֹתָיו׃
8: רַח֣וּם וְחַנּ֣וּן יְהוָ֑ה אֶ֖רֶךְ אַפַּ֣יִם וְרַב־ חָֽסֶד׃
9: לֹֽא־ לָנֶ֥צַח יָרִ֑יב וְלֹ֖א לְעוֹלָ֣ם יִטּֽוֹר׃
10: לֹ֣א כַ֭חֲטָאֵינוּ עָ֣שָׂה לָ֑נוּ וְלֹ֥א כַ֝עֲוֺנֹתֵ֗ינוּ גָּמַ֥ל עָלֵֽינוּ׃
11: כִּ֤י כִגְבֹ֣הַּ שָׁ֭מַיִם עַל־ הָאָ֑רֶץ גָּבַ֥ר חַ֝סְדּ֗וֹ עַל־ יְרֵאָֽיו׃
12: כִּרְחֹ֣ק מִ֭זְרָח מִֽמַּֽעֲרָ֑ב הִֽרְחִ֥יק מִ֝מֶּ֗נּוּ אֶת־ פְּשָׁעֵֽינוּ׃
13: כְּרַחֵ֣ם אָ֭ב עַל־ בָּנִ֑ים רִחַ֥ם יְ֝הוָ֗ה עַל־ יְרֵאָֽיו׃
14: כִּי־ ה֖וּא יָדַ֣ע יִצְרֵ֑נוּ זָ֝כ֗וּר כִּי־ עָפָ֥ר אֲנָֽחְנוּ׃
15: אֱ֭נוֹשׁ כֶּחָצִ֣יר יָמָ֑יו כְּצִ֥יץ הַ֝שָּׂדֶ֗ה כֵּ֣ן יָצִֽיץ׃
16: כִּ֤י ר֣וּחַ עָֽבְרָה־ בּ֣וֹ וְאֵינֶ֑נּוּ וְלֹא־ יַכִּירֶ֖נּוּ ע֣וֹד מְקוֹמֽוֹ׃
17: וְחֶ֤סֶד יְהוָ֨ה ׀ מֵעוֹלָ֣ם וְעַד־ ע֭וֹלָם עַל־ יְרֵאָ֑יו וְ֝צִדְקָת֗וֹ לִבְנֵ֥י בָנִֽים׃
18: לְשֹׁמְרֵ֥י בְרִית֑וֹ וּלְזֹכְרֵ֥י פִ֝קֻּדָ֗יו לַעֲשׂוֹתָֽם׃
19: יְֽהוָ֗ה בַּ֭שָּׁמַיִם הֵכִ֣ין כִּסְא֑וֹ וּ֝מַלְכוּת֗וֹ בַּכֹּ֥ל מָשָֽׁלָה׃
20: בָּרֲכ֥וּ יְהוָ֗ה מַלְאָ֫כָ֥יו גִּבֹּ֣רֵי כֹ֭חַ עֹשֵׂ֣י דְבָר֑וֹ לִ֝שְׁמֹ֗עַ בְּק֣וֹל דְּבָרֽוֹ׃
21: בָּרֲכ֣וּ יְ֭הוָה כָּל־ צְבָאָ֑יו מְ֝שָׁרְתָ֗יו עֹשֵׂ֥י רְצוֹנֽוֹ׃
22: בָּרֲכ֤וּ יְהוָ֨ה ׀ כָּֽל־ מַעֲשָׂ֗יו בְּכָל־ מְקֹמ֥וֹת מֶמְשַׁלְתּ֑וֹ בָּרֲכִ֥י נַ֝פְשִׁ֗י אֶת־ יְהוָֽה׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 103

## Scene 1: Praise for God's Benefits

> "Praise the Lord, my soul; all my inmost being, praise his holy name. Praise the Lord, my soul, and forget not all his benefits." - Psalm 103:1-2 (NIV)

- Scene: The psalmist calls upon his soul to praise the Lord and remember all of His benefits.
- Imagery:
  - Soul praising: The psalmist urges his soul, representing the innermost being, to praise the Lord, indicating the depth and sincerity of the worship.
  - Remembering benefits: The psalmist encourages himself to recall and acknowledge the many blessings and favors bestowed upon him by God, highlighting an attitude of gratitude.

## Scene 2: Forgiveness of Sins

> "who forgives all your sins and heals all your diseases," - Psalm 103:3 (NIV)

- Scene: God is the one who forgives sins and heals diseases.
- Imagery:
  - Forgiveness of sins: The psalmist portrays God as the forgiver of sins, emphasizing His role as a compassionate and merciful God who absolves his people from the burden of guilt.
  - Healing of diseases: The psalmist likens God's ability to heal diseases to His forgiveness, suggesting that God's healing power extends beyond physical ailments to our spiritual well-being.

## Scene 3: Redemption and Renewal

> "who redeems your life from the pit and crowns you with love and compassion," - Psalm 103:4 (NIV)

- Scene: God is the redeemer of our lives, lifting us out of despair and adorning us with love and compassion.
- Imagery:
  - Redemption from the pit: The psalmist uses the metaphor of being rescued from a pit to depict God's deliverance from dire circumstances, symbolizing His ability to offer salvation and hope.
  - Crowning with love and compassion: The psalmist portrays God as adorning His people with a crown, signifying His love and compassion, which are bestowed upon them as a mark of honor, dignity, and care.

## Scene 4: Satisfying Renewal

> "who satisfies your desires with good things so that your youth is renewed like the eagle's." - Psalm 103:5 (NIV)

- Scene: God satisfies the desires of His people, renewing their vitality and strength like that of an eagle.
- Imagery:
  - Satisfying desires: The psalmist portrays God as the satisfier of desires, emphasizing His ability to fulfill the longings and needs of His people with good things, bringing satisfaction and contentment.
  - Renewal like an eagle's youth: The psalmist uses the symbol of an eagle's renewed strength and vitality to depict the rejuvenating power of God, suggesting that He revitalizes His people, providing them with renewed energy and vigor.

## Scene 5: Righteousness and Justice

> "The Lord works righteousness and justice for all the oppressed." - Psalm 103:6 (NIV)

- Scene: The Lord executes righteousness and justice for those who are oppressed.
- Imagery:
  - Working righteousness and justice: The psalmist conveys God's active involvement in administering righteousness and justice, highlighting His commitment to upholding what is right and fair in the face of oppression.
  - Oppressed: The psalmist acknowledges the existence of the oppressed, reflecting the harsh realities of life and the need for divine intervention to bring about justice and liberation.

## Scene 6: God's Ways and His Mercy

> "He made known his ways to Moses, his deeds to the people of Israel: The Lord is compassionate and gracious, slow to anger, abounding in love." - Psalm 103:7-8 (NIV)

- Scene: God reveals His ways to Moses and His deeds to the people of Israel, demonstrating His compassionate and gracious nature.
- Imagery:
  - Revealing His ways: The psalmist depicts God's act of making His ways known to Moses, signifying His desire to intimately relate to His chosen people, providing understanding of His character, directives, and purposes.
  - Compassionate and gracious: The psalmist emphasizes God's compassionate and gracious attributes, portraying Him as slow to anger and abounding in love, highlighting His merciful nature and patience with His people.

## Scene 7: Divine Forgiveness

> "He will not always accuse, nor will he harbor his anger forever; he does not treat us as our sins deserve or repay us according to our iniquities." - Psalm 103:9-10 (NIV)

- Scene: God's forgiveness is demonstrated by His refusal to harbor anger forever and His mercy in not treating us as our sins deserve.
- Imagery:
  - Not always accusing: The psalmist expresses God's willingness to relent from accusing and condemning His people, suggesting His relenting nature and willingness to forgive.
  - Not treating us as our sins deserve: The psalmist portrays God's mercy in not dealing with His people according to the full weight of their sins, illustrating His willingness to show compassion and leniency.

## Scene 8: Divine Love and Compassion

> "For as high as the heavens are above the earth, so great is his love for those who fear him; as far as the east is from the west, so far has he removed our transgressions from us." - Psalm 103:11-12 (NIV)

- Scene: God's love and compassion surpass the distance between the heavens and the earth, and He removes our transgressions from us.
- Imagery:
  - Greatness of His love: The psalmist employs the imagery of the vast expanse between the heavens and the earth to illustrate the immeasurable nature of God's love for those who fear Him, emphasizing its boundless and unlimited dimensions.
  - Removing transgressions: The psalmist uses the metaphor of removing transgressions as the removal of a vast distance, indicating God's ability to mercifully and completely separate His people from the guilt and consequences of their transgressions.

## Scene 9: Fatherly Compassion

> "As a father has compassion on his children, so the Lord has compassion on those who fear him; for he knows how we are formed, he remembers that we are dust." - Psalm 103:13-14 (NIV)

- Scene: Just as a father has compassion on his children, God shows compassion to those who fear Him, understanding their frailty and humanity.
- Imagery:
  - Fatherly compassion: The psalmist compares God's compassion to that of a father's compassion for his children, signifying God's tender care, protection, and deep love for His people.
  - Understanding our frailty: The psalmist highlights God's knowledge of human nature and condition, recognizing that He is intimately aware of our weaknesses and limitations.

## Scene 10: Temporary Nature of Life

> "As for mortals, their days are like grass, they flourish like a flower of the field; the wind blows over it and it is gone, and its place remembers it no more." - Psalm 103:15-16 (NIV)

- Scene: The temporary nature of human life is likened to grass and flowers that wither away.
- Imagery:
  - Grass and flowers: The psalmist describes the fleeting nature of human life by comparing it to the transitory existence of grass and flowers in the field, highlighting the brevity and fragility of our earthly lives.
  - Wind blowing it away: The psalmist depicts the transient nature of life by illustrating how easily it can be swept away like the wind blowing over grass and flowers, emphasizing the ephemeral nature of human existence.

## Scene 11: Everlasting Love and Righteousness

> "But from everlasting to everlasting the Lord's love is with those who fear him, and his righteousness with their children's children—with those who keep his covenant and remember to obey his precepts." - Psalm 103:17-18 (NIV)

- Scene: The Lord's love and righteousness are everlasting and extend to future generations who fear Him and keep His covenant.
- Imagery:
  - From everlasting to everlasting: The psalmist emphasizes the eternal nature of God's love, indicating that it has no beginning or end, lasting for all time, and serving as an assurance of His faithfulness and devotion to His people.
  - Righteousness with future generations: The psalmist highlights the continuity of God's righteousness, demonstrating that it extends not only to the present generation but also to the offspring of those who fear Him, symbolizing His commitment to bless and guide future generations who remain faithful to Him.

## Scene 12: Heavenly Rule and Praise

> "The Lord has established his throne in heaven, and his kingdom rules over all. Praise the Lord, you his angels, you mighty ones who do his bidding, who obey his word." - Psalm 103:19-20 (NIV)

- Scene: The Lord rules over all from His throne in heaven, and His angelic hosts praise Him and carry out His commands.
- Imagery:
  - Throne in heaven: The psalmist depicts the Lord as having established His throne in the heavenly realm, symbolizing His sovereignty, authority, and power over all creation.
  - Angelic praise and obedience: The psalmist portrays the angels as mighty ones who praise the Lord and faithfully carry out His commands, illustrating their role as servants and messengers of God's divine will.

## Scene 13: Universal Praise for God's Works

> "Praise the Lord, all his heavenly hosts, you his servants who do his will. Praise the Lord, all his works everywhere in his dominion. Praise the Lord, my soul." - Psalm 103:21-22 (NIV)

- Scene: All the heavenly hosts, servants, and works of the Lord are called upon to praise Him.
- Imagery:
  - Heavenly hosts and servants: The psalmist calls upon the angelic beings and servants of the Lord to join in praising Him, acknowledging their role as worshipers and participants in carrying out His divine will.
  - Works everywhere in His dominion: The psalmist extends the call for praise to all of God's works scattered throughout His dominion, encompassing all of creation, both seen and unseen.
  
## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict God's goodness, love, compassion, forgiveness, righteousness, justice, and sovereignty. They emphasize His benevolent nature and care for His people, highlighting His ability to redeem, renew, and provide for them. The imagery in each scene conveys the eudaimonic meaning of trust, gratitude, awe, and praise toward God, inspiring believers to acknowledge and appreciate the manifold blessings and attributes of the Lord in their lives.
