# Psalm 22
1: לַ֭מְנַצֵּחַ עַל־ אַיֶּ֥לֶת הַשַּׁ֗חַר מִזְמ֥וֹר לְדָוִֽד׃
2: אֵלִ֣י אֵ֭לִי לָמָ֣ה עֲזַבְתָּ֑נִי רָח֥וֹק מִֽ֝ישׁוּעָתִ֗י דִּבְרֵ֥י שַׁאֲגָתִֽי׃
3: אֱֽלֹהַ֗י אֶקְרָ֣א י֭וֹמָם וְלֹ֣א תַעֲנֶ֑ה וְ֝לַ֗יְלָה וְֽלֹא־ דֽוּמִיָּ֥ה לִֽי׃
4: וְאַתָּ֥ה קָד֑וֹשׁ י֝וֹשֵׁ֗ב תְּהִלּ֥וֹת יִשְׂרָאֵֽל׃
5: בְּ֭ךָ בָּטְח֣וּ אֲבֹתֵ֑ינוּ בָּ֝טְח֗וּ וַֽתְּפַלְּטֵֽמוֹ׃
6: אֵלֶ֣יךָ זָעֲק֣וּ וְנִמְלָ֑טוּ בְּךָ֖ בָטְח֣וּ וְלֹא־ בֽוֹשׁוּ׃
7: וְאָנֹכִ֣י תוֹלַ֣עַת וְלֹא־ אִ֑ישׁ חֶרְפַּ֥ת אָ֝דָ֗ם וּבְז֥וּי עָֽם׃
8: כָּל־ רֹ֭אַי יַלְעִ֣גוּ לִ֑י יַפְטִ֥ירוּ בְ֝שָׂפָ֗ה יָנִ֥יעוּ רֹֽאשׁ׃
9: גֹּ֣ל אֶל־ יְהוָ֣ה יְפַלְּטֵ֑הוּ יַ֝צִּילֵ֗הוּ כִּ֘י חָ֥פֵֽץ בּֽוֹ׃
10: כִּֽי־ אַתָּ֣ה גֹחִ֣י מִבָּ֑טֶן מַ֝בְטִיחִ֗י עַל־ שְׁדֵ֥י אִמִּֽי׃
11: עָ֭לֶיךָ הָשְׁלַ֣כְתִּי מֵרָ֑חֶם מִבֶּ֥טֶן אִ֝מִּ֗י אֵ֣לִי אָֽתָּה׃
12: אַל־ תִּרְחַ֣ק מִ֭מֶּנִּי כִּי־ צָרָ֣ה קְרוֹבָ֑ה כִּי־ אֵ֥ין עוֹזֵֽר׃
13: סְ֭בָבוּנִי פָּרִ֣ים רַבִּ֑ים אַבִּירֵ֖י בָשָׁ֣ן כִּתְּרֽוּנִי׃
14: פָּצ֣וּ עָלַ֣י פִּיהֶ֑ם אַ֝רְיֵ֗ה טֹרֵ֥ף וְשֹׁאֵֽג׃
15: כַּמַּ֥יִם נִשְׁפַּכְתִּי֮ וְהִתְפָּֽרְד֗וּ כָּֽל־ עַצְמ֫וֹתָ֥י הָיָ֣ה לִ֭בִּי כַּדּוֹנָ֑ג נָ֝מֵ֗ס בְּת֣וֹךְ מֵעָֽי׃
16: יָ֘בֵ֤שׁ כַּחֶ֨רֶשׂ ׀ כֹּחִ֗י וּ֭לְשׁוֹנִי מֻדְבָּ֣ק מַלְקוֹחָ֑י וְֽלַעֲפַר־ מָ֥וֶת תִּשְׁפְּתֵֽנִי׃
17: כִּ֥י סְבָב֗וּנִי כְּלָ֫בִ֥ים עֲדַ֣ת מְ֭רֵעִים הִקִּיפ֑וּנִי כָּ֝אֲרִ֗י יָדַ֥י וְרַגְלָֽי׃
18: אֲסַפֵּ֥ר כָּל־ עַצְמוֹתָ֑י הֵ֥מָּה יַ֝בִּ֗יטוּ יִרְאוּ־ בִֽי׃
19: יְחַלְּק֣וּ בְגָדַ֣י לָהֶ֑ם וְעַל־ לְ֝בוּשִׁ֗י יַפִּ֥ילוּ גוֹרָֽל׃
20: וְאַתָּ֣ה יְ֭הוָה אַל־ תִּרְחָ֑ק אֱ֝יָלוּתִ֗י לְעֶזְרָ֥תִי חֽוּשָׁה׃
21: הַצִּ֣ילָה מֵחֶ֣רֶב נַפְשִׁ֑י מִיַּד־ כֶּ֝֗לֶב יְחִידָתִֽי׃
22: ה֭וֹשִׁיעֵנִי מִפִּ֣י אַרְיֵ֑ה וּמִקַּרְנֵ֖י רֵמִ֣ים עֲנִיתָֽנִי׃
23: אֲסַפְּרָ֣ה שִׁמְךָ֣ לְאֶחָ֑י בְּת֖וֹךְ קָהָ֣ל אֲהַלְלֶֽךָּ׃
24: יִרְאֵ֤י יְהוָ֨ה ׀ הַֽלְל֗וּהוּ כָּל־ זֶ֣רַע יַעֲקֹ֣ב כַּבְּד֑וּהוּ וְג֥וּרוּ מִ֝מֶּ֗נּוּ כָּל־ זֶ֥רַע יִשְׂרָאֵֽל׃
25: כִּ֤י לֹֽא־ בָזָ֨ה וְלֹ֪א שִׁקַּ֡ץ עֱנ֬וּת עָנִ֗י וְלֹא־ הִסְתִּ֣יר פָּנָ֣יו מִמֶּ֑נּוּ וּֽבְשַׁוְּע֖וֹ אֵלָ֣יו שָׁמֵֽעַ׃
26: מֵ֥אִתְּךָ֗ תְֽהִלָּ֫תִ֥י בְּקָהָ֥ל רָ֑ב נְדָרַ֥י אֲ֝שַׁלֵּ֗ם נֶ֣גֶד יְרֵאָֽיו׃
27: יֹאכְל֬וּ עֲנָוִ֨ים ׀ וְיִשְׂבָּ֗עוּ יְהַֽלְל֣וּ יְ֭הוָה דֹּ֣רְשָׁ֑יו יְחִ֖י לְבַבְכֶ֣ם לָעַֽד׃
28: יִזְכְּר֤וּ ׀ וְיָשֻׁ֣בוּ אֶל־ יְ֭הוָה כָּל־ אַפְסֵי־ אָ֑רֶץ וְיִֽשְׁתַּחֲו֥וּ לְ֝פָנֶ֗יךָ כָּֽל־ מִשְׁפְּח֥וֹת גּוֹיִֽם׃
29: כִּ֣י לַ֭יהוָה הַמְּלוּכָ֑ה וּ֝מֹשֵׁ֗ל בַּגּוֹיִֽם׃
30: אָכְל֬וּ וַיִּֽשְׁתַּחֲוּ֨וּ ׀ כָּֽל־ דִּשְׁנֵי־ אֶ֗רֶץ לְפָנָ֣יו יִ֭כְרְעוּ כָּל־ יוֹרְדֵ֣י עָפָ֑ר וְ֝נַפְשׁ֗וֹ לֹ֣א חִיָּֽה׃
31: זֶ֥רַע יַֽעַבְדֶ֑נּוּ יְסֻפַּ֖ר לַֽאדֹנָ֣י לַדּֽוֹר׃
32: יָ֭בֹאוּ וְיַגִּ֣ידוּ צִדְקָת֑וֹ לְעַ֥ם נ֝וֹלָ֗ד כִּ֣י עָשָֽׂה׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 22

## Scene 1: Despair and Invocation for Help

> "My God, my God, why have you forsaken me? Why are you so far from saving me, so far from my cries of anguish? My God, I cry out by day, but you do not answer, by night, but I find no rest." - Psalm 22:1-2 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist is in a state of despair, feeling abandoned by God.
  - They cry out to God day and night, but receive no answer and find no rest.

- Imagery:
  - Abandonment: The psalmist feels a sense of being forsaken and distant from God.
  - Cries of anguish: The psalmist expresses their deep emotional pain and distress.
  - Lack of response: God's apparent silence further intensifies the psalmist's feelings of despair.

## Scene 2: God's Faithfulness Throughout Generations

> "Yet you are enthroned as the Holy One; you are the one Israel praises. In you our ancestors put their trust; they trusted and you delivered them. To you they cried out and were saved; in you they trusted and were not put to shame." - Psalm 22:3-5 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges God's faithfulness throughout generations.
  - They recall how the ancestors trusted in God and were delivered.

- Imagery:
  - Enthronement: God is depicted as the Holy One, reigning with authority and power.
  - Trust and deliverance: The ancestors trusted in God and were saved from their troubles.
  - Not being put to shame: The psalmist affirms that those who trust in God will not be disappointed.

## Scene 3: Humiliation and Mockery

> "But I am a worm and not a man, scorned by everyone, despised by the people. All who see me mock me; they hurl insults, shaking their heads. 'He trusts in the LORD,' they say, 'let the LORD rescue him. Let him deliver him, since he delights in him.'" - Psalm 22:6-8 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist describes their experience of humiliation and mockery.
  - They are scorned and despised by the people.
  - Those who see them mock and insult them, questioning their trust in the Lord.

- Imagery:
  - Worm and not a man: The psalmist compares their lowly state to that of a despised creature.
  - Scorn and insults: The psalmist is subjected to ridicule and verbal abuse.
  - Questioning trust in the Lord: The mockers challenge the authenticity of the psalmist's reliance on God's deliverance.

## Scene 4: Unique Divine Connection

> "Yet you brought me out of the womb; you made me trust in you, even at my mother's breast. From birth I was cast on you; from my mother's womb you have been my God. Do not be far from me, for trouble is near and there is no one to help." - Psalm 22:9-11 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist reflects on their unique divine connection since birth.
  - They emphasize their early trust in God.

- Imagery:
  - Divine connection from birth: God's presence and protection have been with the psalmist from the earliest stages of life.
  - Trust from infancy: The psalmist's attachment to God began even while nursing at their mother's breast.
  - Plea for nearness: The psalmist implores God to be close, recognizing the impending trouble and the absence of human assistance.

## Scene 5: Attackers and Physical Weakness

> "Many bulls surround me; strong bulls of Bashan encircle me. Roaring lions that tear their prey open their mouths wide against me." - Psalm 22:12-13 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist describes their assailants and their own physical weakness.
  - They use vivid imagery of powerful and aggressive creatures to portray the danger they face.

- Imagery:
  - Bulls and lions: The psalmist likens their attackers to fierce and powerful animals, symbolizing the aggressiveness and strength of their enemies.
  - Surrounding and encircling: The psalmist is depicted as being surrounded and trapped by their adversaries.
  - Vulnerability: The psalmist acknowledges their own physical weakness in contrast to the strength of their assailants.

## Scene 6: Plea for Rescue and Deliverance

> "I am poured out like water, and all my bones are out of joint. My heart has turned to wax; it has melted within me." - Psalm 22:14 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist expresses their extreme physical and emotional distress.
  - They feel drained and weakened, with their entire being in disarray.

- Imagery:
  - Poured out like water: The psalmist feels emptied of strength and vitality.
  - Bones out of joint: The psalmist experiences a sense of dislocation and physical suffering.
  - Heart melting like wax: The psalmist's emotional state is depicted as deteriorating rapidly.

## Scene 7: Relentless Enemies and Desperate Plea

> "My mouth is dried up like a potsherd, and my tongue sticks to the roof of my mouth; you lay me in the dust of death. Dogs surround me, a pack of villains encircles me; they pierce my hands and my feet. All my bones are on display; people stare and gloat over me." - Psalm 22:15-17 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist portrays the intensity of their suffering and the cruelty of their enemies.
  - Their mouth is dry, and they are in a state of near-death.
  - The psalmist's hands and feet are pierced, and their bones are visible for all to see.

- Imagery:
  - Dry mouth and tongue sticking: The psalmist experiences extreme thirst and physical weakness.
  - Dust of death: The psalmist feels as if they are close to death and lying in the dust.
  - Dogs and villains: The enemies are likened to vicious dogs and evil individuals who perpetrate violence.
  - Pierced hands and feet: The psalmist uses imagery that foreshadows crucifixion, symbolizing great suffering.
  - Visible bones and people's gaze: The psalmist's torment is made public, with people gawking and finding satisfaction in their pain.

##Scene 8: Cries for Help and Divine Rescue

> "They divide my clothes among them and cast lots for my garment. But you, LORD, do not be far from me. You are my strength; come quickly to help me. Deliver me from the sword, my precious life from the power of the dogs. Rescue me from the mouth of the lions; save me from the horns of the wild oxen." - Psalm 22:18-21 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist continues to depict the cruelty of their enemies.
  - They cry out to the Lord for help and deliverance from impending danger.

- Imagery:
  - Division of clothes and casting lots: The psalmist's enemies are portrayed as callously dividing their belongings.
  - Plea for divine nearness and strength: The psalmist appeals to God's presence and sustaining power.
  - Rescue from the sword, dogs, lions, and wild oxen: The psalmist implores God to save them from various forms of imminent threat, using vivid imagery of dangerous adversaries.

##Scene 9: Praise and Proclamation of God's Deliverance

> "I will declare your name to my people; in the assembly I will praise you. You who fear the LORD, praise him! All you descendants of Jacob, honor him! Revere him, all you descendants of Israel! For he has not despised or scorned the suffering of the afflicted one; he has not hidden his face from him but has listened to his cry for help." - Psalm 22:22-24 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist expresses their intention to declare God's name and praise Him.
  - They call upon all who fear the Lord to join in honoring and revering Him.
  - The psalmist acknowledges God's compassion and attentive listening to their cry for help.

- Imagery:
  - Declaration and praise: The psalmist plans to publicly proclaim God's name and sing His praises.
  - Fear, honor, and reverence: The psalmist urges others to hold God in high regard and show deep respect for Him.
  - Compassion and attentive listening: The psalmist acknowledges God's empathy for those who suffer and God's responsiveness to their pleas for assistance.

##Scene 10: Assurance of Divine Help and Worship

> "From you comes the theme of my praise in the great assembly; before those who fear you I will fulfill my vows. The poor will eat and be satisfied; those who seek the LORD will praise him—may your hearts live forever!" - Psalm 22:25-26 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist affirms that their praise and worship will be centered on God.
  - They express their commitment to fulfill vows made to God.
  - The psalmist foresees the satisfaction and praise of the poor and those who seek the Lord.

- Imagery:
  - Theme of praise: The psalmist declares God as the inspiration behind their songs of praise.
  - Fulfillment of vows: The psalmist promises to honor their commitments to God.
  - Satisfied poor and praising seekers: The psalmist envisions a future where those in need are satisfied and those who seek God are filled with praise and thanksgiving.

##Scene 11: Universal Worship and Dominion

> "All the ends of the earth will remember and turn to the LORD, and all the families of the nations will bow down before him, for dominion belongs to the LORD and he rules over the nations." - Psalm 22:27-28 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist envisions a future where people from all over the world turn to the Lord and worship Him.
  - They acknowledge God's dominion and rule over the nations.

- Imagery:
  - Universal remembrance and turning to the Lord: The psalmist portrays a global awakening to the recognition of God's sovereignty, resulting in widespread worship and devotion.
  - Bowing down before Him: People from all nations are depicted as humbly submitting themselves to God.
  - God's dominion and rule: The psalmist acknowledges God's authority and governance over all nations.

##Scene 12: Prosperity and Worship of Future Generations

> "All the rich of the earth will feast and worship; all who go down to the dust will kneel before him—those who cannot keep themselves alive. Posterity will serve him; future generations will be told about the Lord. They will proclaim his righteousness, declaring to a people yet unborn: He has done it!" - Psalm 22:29-31 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist envisions a time of abundance and worship for the wealthy.
  - They emphasize the universal act of kneeling before God, even by those who have died.
  - The psalmist foresees the worship and proclamation of God's righteousness by future generations.

- Imagery:
  - Feasting and worship of the rich: The psalmist portrays a scene of prosperity where wealthier individuals engage in worship and celebration.
  - Kneeling before God: Even those who have died are envisioned as humbly bowing down before God.
  - Posterity and future generations: The psalmist anticipates the faithfulness of future generations to serve God and share His story.
  - Proclamation of God's righteousness: The psalmist envisions future generations declaring God's justice and goodness to those not yet born.

##Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict the psalmist's journey from despair and abandonment to a place of hope and trust in the faithfulness of God. They express intense emotions of anguish, humiliation, and physical suffering while also proclaiming God's sovereignty, mercy, and deliverance. Through rich imagery, the psalmist taps into deep human experiences and emotions, ultimately leading to a vision of universal worship and the flourishing of future generations in the knowledge of God's righteousness. The Psalm reminds the reader of the eudaimonic significance of seeking and trusting in God, even in the midst of adversity, and the assurance of ultimate victory and praise.
