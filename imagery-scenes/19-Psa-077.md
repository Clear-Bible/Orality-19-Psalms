# Psalm 77
1: לַמְנַצֵּ֥חַ עַֽל־ לְאָסָ֥ף מִזְמֽוֹר׃
2: קוֹלִ֣י אֶל־ אֱלֹהִ֣ים וְאֶצְעָ֑קָה קוֹלִ֥י אֶל־ אֱ֝לֹהִ֗ים וְהַאֲזִ֥ין אֵלָֽי׃
3: בְּי֥וֹם צָרָתִי֮ אֲדֹנָ֪י דָּ֫רָ֥שְׁתִּי יָדִ֤י ׀ לַ֣יְלָה נִ֭גְּרָה וְלֹ֣א תָפ֑וּג מֵאֲנָ֖ה הִנָּחֵ֣ם נַפְשִֽׁי׃
4: אֶזְכְּרָ֣ה אֱלֹהִ֣ים וְאֶֽהֱמָיָ֑ה אָשִׂ֓יחָה ׀ וְתִתְעַטֵּ֖ף רוּחִ֣י סֶֽלָה׃
5: אָ֭חַזְתָּ שְׁמֻר֣וֹת עֵינָ֑י נִ֝פְעַ֗מְתִּי וְלֹ֣א אֲדַבֵּֽר׃
6: חִשַּׁ֣בְתִּי יָמִ֣ים מִקֶּ֑דֶם שְׁ֝נ֗וֹת עוֹלָמִֽים׃
7: אֶֽזְכְּרָ֥ה נְגִינָתִ֗י בַּ֫לָּ֥יְלָה עִם־ לְבָבִ֥י אָשִׂ֑יחָה וַיְחַפֵּ֥שׂ רוּחִֽי׃
8: הַֽ֭לְעוֹלָמִים יִזְנַ֥ח ׀ אֲדֹנָ֑י וְלֹֽא־ יֹסִ֖יף לִרְצ֣וֹת עֽוֹד׃
9: הֶאָפֵ֣ס לָנֶ֣צַח חַסְדּ֑וֹ גָּ֥מַר אֹ֝֗מֶר לְדֹ֣ר וָדֹֽר׃
10: הֲשָׁכַ֣ח חַנּ֣וֹת אֵ֑ל אִם־ קָפַ֥ץ בְּ֝אַ֗ף רַחֲמָ֥יו סֶֽלָה׃
11: וָ֭אֹמַר חַלּ֣וֹתִי הִ֑יא שְׁ֝נ֗וֹת יְמִ֣ין עֶלְיֽוֹן׃
12: מַֽעַלְלֵי־ יָ֑הּ כִּֽי־ אֶזְכְּרָ֖ה מִקֶּ֣דֶם פִּלְאֶֽךָ׃
13: וְהָגִ֥יתִי בְכָל־ פָּעֳלֶ֑ךָ וּֽבַעֲלִ֖ילוֹתֶ֣יךָ אָשִֽׂיחָה׃
14: אֱ֭לֹהִים בַּקֹּ֣דֶשׁ דַּרְכֶּ֑ךָ מִי־ אֵ֥ל גָּ֝ד֗וֹל כֵּֽאלֹהִֽים׃
15: אַתָּ֣ה הָ֭אֵל עֹ֣שֵׂה פֶ֑לֶא הוֹדַ֖עְתָּ בָעַמִּ֣ים עֻזֶּֽךָ׃
16: גָּאַ֣לְתָּ בִּזְר֣וֹעַ עַמֶּ֑ךָ בְּנֵי־ יַעֲקֹ֖ב וְיוֹסֵ֣ף סֶֽלָה׃
17: רָ֘א֤וּךָ מַּ֨יִם ׀ אֱֽלֹהִ֗ים רָא֣וּךָ מַּ֣יִם יָחִ֑ילוּ אַ֝֗ף יִרְגְּז֥וּ תְהֹמֽוֹת׃
18: זֹ֤רְמוּ מַ֨יִם ׀ עָב֗וֹת ק֭וֹל נָתְנ֣וּ שְׁחָקִ֑ים אַף־ חֲ֝צָצֶ֗יךָ יִתְהַלָּֽכוּ׃
19: ק֤וֹל רַעַמְךָ֨ ׀ בַּגַּלְגַּ֗ל הֵאִ֣ירוּ בְרָקִ֣ים תֵּבֵ֑ל רָגְזָ֖ה וַתִּרְעַ֣שׁ הָאָֽרֶץ׃
20: בַּיָּ֤ם דַּרְכֶּ֗ךָ בְּמַ֣יִם רַבִּ֑ים וְ֝עִקְּבוֹתֶ֗יךָ לֹ֣א נֹדָֽעוּ׃
21: נָחִ֣יתָ כַצֹּ֣אן עַמֶּ֑ךָ בְּֽיַד־ מֹשֶׁ֥ה וְאַהֲרֹֽן׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 77

## Scene 1: Asaph's Cry for Help

> "I cried out to God for help; I cried out to God to hear me." - Psalm 77:1 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Asaph, the psalmist, is in a state of distress and desperation.
  - He raises his voice in prayer, calling out to God for help.
- Imagery:
  - Asaph's cry for help represents his deep longing and reliance on God.
  - It portrays his vulnerability and humility, acknowledging that he cannot overcome his troubles on his own.

## Scene 2: Wrestling with Doubts and Distress

> "When I was in distress, I sought the Lord; at night, I stretched out untiring hands, and I would not be comforted." - Psalm 77:2 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Asaph recounts a period of intense distress and anguish.
  - He actively seeks the Lord, extending his hands in prayer.
- Imagery:
  - Asaph's distress represents the trials and challenges he faces in life.
  - His outstretched hands symbolize his desperate yearning for comfort and reassurance.
  - The night serves as a metaphor for the darkness and uncertainty that envelops his situation.

## Scene 3: Remembering God's Faithfulness

> "I remembered you, God, and I groaned; I meditated, and my spirit grew faint." - Psalm 77:3 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Asaph reflects on his past experiences with God.
  - He engages in deep contemplation and meditation on God's faithfulness.
- Imagery:
  - Asaph's groaning represents his longing for God and the burdens he carries.
  - His faint spirit portrays his weariness and emotional exhaustion.
  - Remembering God's faithfulness highlights the eudaimonic significance of recalling God's past acts of deliverance and provision.

## Scene 4: Sleepless Nights and Reflection

> "You kept my eyes from closing; I was too troubled to speak." - Psalm 77:4 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Asaph describes sleepless nights filled with troubled thoughts.
  - He is unable to find the words to express his turmoil.
- Imagery:
  - Asaph's inability to sleep reflects his state of anxiety and restlessness.
  - The silence of his speech signifies the magnitude of his distress and the overwhelming nature of his thoughts.

## Scene 5: Pondering God's Deeds and Wonders

> "I thought about the former days, the years of long ago; I remembered my songs in the night. My heart meditated and my spirit asked: 'Will the Lord reject forever? Will he never show his favor again?'" - Psalm 77:5-7 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Asaph engages in deep reflection on the past, recalling the deeds and wonders of God.
  - He questions whether God will continue to show favor and if he will reject forever.
- Imagery:
  - Asaph's contemplation of the former days represents his quest for understanding and perspective.
  - His songs in the night symbolize his previous expressions of praise and worship to God.
  - The questions he poses express his longing for God's continued favor and reassurance.

## Scene 6: God's Wonders and Deliverance

> "Has his unfailing love vanished forever? Has his promise failed for all time? Has God forgotten to be merciful? Has he in anger withheld his compassion?'" - Psalm 77:8-9 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Asaph continues to question God's faithfulness and wonders if his promises have failed.
  - He ponders whether God has forgotten to be merciful and compassionate.
- Imagery:
  - The doubts expressed by Asaph reflect the internal struggle between faith and doubt.
  - The questions he poses convey his plea for God's deliverance and for a renewal of God's unfailing love.

## Scene 7: Remembering God's Mighty Deeds

> "Then I thought, 'To this I will appeal: the years when the Most High stretched out his right hand. I will remember the deeds of the LORD; yes, I will remember your miracles of long ago. I will consider all your works and meditate on all your mighty deeds.'" - Psalm 77:10-12 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Asaph resolves to focus on God's mighty deeds and miracles of the past.
  - He chooses to remember and meditate on God's works.
- Imagery:
  - Asaph's decision to appeal to God's past acts represents his choice to trust in God's faithfulness.
  - Remembering God's miracles and mighty deeds inspires hope and confidence in Asaph.
  - The act of meditation portrays Asaph's deep contemplation and engagement with God's works.

## Scene 8: God's Ways in the Sanctuary

> "Your ways, God, are holy. What god is as great as our God? You are the God who performs miracles; you display your power among the peoples." - Psalm 77:13-14 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Asaph acknowledges God's holiness and greatness in his ways.
  - He acknowledges God's power and miracles displayed before the people.
- Imagery:
  - Asaph's recognition of God's holiness highlights the eudaimonic significance of revering and worshiping God.
  - The mention of God's miracles and power emphasizes God's sovereignty and ability to intervene in the lives of his people.

## Scene 9: God's Redeeming Guidance

> "With your mighty arm you redeemed your people, the descendants of Jacob and Joseph. The waters saw you, God, the waters saw you and writhed; the very depths were convulsed." - Psalm 77:15-16 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Asaph recalls God's redeeming acts and guidance of the descendants of Jacob and Joseph.
  - He vividly describes the dramatic response of the waters to God's presence.
- Imagery:
  - God's mighty arm symbolizes his power and strength in redeeming his people.
  - The response of the waters portrays the awe-inspiring nature of God's intervention and the complete subjugation of creation to his command.

## Scene 10: God's Leading Through the Sea

> "The clouds poured down water, the heavens resounded with thunder; your arrows flashed back and forth. Your thunder was heard in the whirlwind, your lightning lit up the world; the earth trembled and quaked." - Psalm 77:17-18 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Asaph continues to recount the supernatural events of God's leading through the sea.
  - He vividly depicts the thunder, lightning, and trembling of the earth.
- Imagery:
  - The pouring down of water and resounding thunder represent the awesome power of God's presence.
  - God's arrows symbolize his judgments and divine intervention.
  - The trembling and quaking of the earth portray the profound impact of God's leading.

## Scene 11: God's Providence in Nature

> "Your path led through the sea, your way through the mighty waters, though your footprints were not seen. You led your people like a flock by the hand of Moses and Aaron." - Psalm 77:19-20 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - Asaph affirms God's providential guidance of his people through the sea.
  - He acknowledges that God's footprints were not visible, but his leading was undeniable.
- Imagery:
  - God's unseen footprints symbolize his hidden providence and care for his people.
  - The metaphor of leading his people like a flock portrays God's nurturing and protective guidance through Moses and Aaron.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict the psalmist's cry for help, wrestling with doubts and distress, remembering God's faithfulness, pondering God's deeds and wonders, questioning God's faithfulness, and resolving to remember and meditate on God's mighty deeds. The imagery in each scene provides vivid depictions of Asaph's emotional state and his journey towards finding hope and reassurance in God's faithfulness. Overall, the psalm highlights the eudaimonic significance of seeking and trusting in God amidst trials and difficulties, as well as the transformative power of remembering and meditating on God's mighty acts in the past.
