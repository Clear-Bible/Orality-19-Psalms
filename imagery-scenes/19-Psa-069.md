# Psalm 69
1: לַמְנַצֵּ֬חַ עַֽל־ שׁוֹשַׁנִּ֬ים לְדָוִֽד׃
2: הוֹשִׁיעֵ֥נִי אֱלֹהִ֑ים כִּ֤י בָ֖אוּ מַ֣יִם עַד־ נָֽפֶשׁ׃
3: טָבַ֤עְתִּי ׀ בִּיוֵ֣ן מְ֭צוּלָה וְאֵ֣ין מָעֳמָ֑ד בָּ֥אתִי בְמַעֲמַקֵּי־ מַ֝֗יִם וְשִׁבֹּ֥לֶת שְׁטָפָֽתְנִי׃
4: יָגַ֣עְתִּי בְקָרְאִי֮ נִחַ֪ר גְּר֫וֹנִ֥י כָּל֥וּ עֵינַ֑י מְ֝יַחֵ֗ל לֵאלֹהָֽי׃
5: רַבּ֤וּ ׀ מִשַּׂעֲר֣וֹת רֹאשִׁי֮ שֹׂנְאַ֪י חִ֫נָּ֥ם עָצְמ֣וּ מַ֭צְמִיתַי אֹיְבַ֣י שֶׁ֑קֶר אֲשֶׁ֥ר לֹא־ גָ֝זַ֗לְתִּי אָ֣ז אָשִֽׁיב׃
6: אֱֽלֹהִ֗ים אַתָּ֣ה יָ֭דַעְתָּ לְאִוַּלְתִּ֑י וְ֝אַשְׁמוֹתַ֗י מִמְּךָ֥ לֹא־ נִכְחָֽדוּ׃
7: אַל־ יֵ֘בֹ֤שׁוּ בִ֨י ׀ קֹוֶיךָ֮ אֲדֹנָ֥י יְהוִ֗ה צְבָ֫א֥וֹת אַל־ יִכָּ֣לְמוּ בִ֣י מְבַקְשֶׁ֑יךָ אֱ֝לֹהֵ֗י יִשְׂרָאֵֽל׃
8: כִּֽי־ עָ֭לֶיךָ נָשָׂ֣אתִי חֶרְפָּ֑ה כִּסְּתָ֖ה כְלִמָּ֣ה פָנָֽי׃
9: מ֭וּזָר הָיִ֣יתִי לְאֶחָ֑י וְ֝נָכְרִ֗י לִבְנֵ֥י אִמִּֽי׃
10: כִּֽי־ קִנְאַ֣ת בֵּיתְךָ֣ אֲכָלָ֑תְנִי וְחֶרְפּ֥וֹת ח֝וֹרְפֶ֗יךָ נָפְל֥וּ עָלָֽי׃
11: וָאֶבְכֶּ֣ה בַצּ֣וֹם נַפְשִׁ֑י וַתְּהִ֖י לַחֲרָפ֣וֹת לִֽי׃
12: וָאֶתְּנָ֣ה לְבוּשִׁ֣י שָׂ֑ק וָאֱהִ֖י לָהֶ֣ם לְמָשָֽׁל׃
13: יָשִׂ֣יחוּ בִ֭י יֹ֣שְׁבֵי שָׁ֑עַר וּ֝נְגִינ֗וֹת שׁוֹתֵ֥י שֵׁכָֽר׃
14: וַאֲנִ֤י תְפִלָּתִֽי־ לְךָ֨ ׀ יְהוָ֡ה עֵ֤ת רָצ֗וֹן אֱלֹהִ֥ים בְּרָב־ חַסְדֶּ֑ךָ עֲ֝נֵ֗נִי בֶּאֱמֶ֥ת יִשְׁעֶֽךָ׃
15: הַצִּילֵ֣נִי מִ֭טִּיט וְאַל־ אֶטְבָּ֑עָה אִנָּצְלָ֥ה מִ֝שֹּֽׂנְאַ֗י וּמִמַּֽעֲמַקֵּי־ מָֽיִם׃
16: אַל־ תִּשְׁטְפֵ֤נִי ׀ שִׁבֹּ֣לֶת מַ֭יִם וְאַל־ תִּבְלָעֵ֣נִי מְצוּלָ֑ה וְאַל־ תֶּאְטַר־ עָלַ֖י בְּאֵ֣ר פִּֽיהָ׃
17: עֲנֵ֣נִי יְ֭הוָה כִּי־ ט֣וֹב חַסְדֶּ֑ךָ כְּרֹ֥ב רַ֝חֲמֶ֗יךָ פְּנֵ֣ה אֵלָֽי׃
18: וְאַל־ תַּסְתֵּ֣ר פָּ֭נֶיךָ מֵֽעַבְדֶּ֑ךָ כִּֽי־ צַר־ לִ֝֗י מַהֵ֥ר עֲנֵֽנִי׃
19: קָרְבָ֣ה אֶל־ נַפְשִׁ֣י גְאָלָ֑הּ לְמַ֖עַן אֹיְבַ֣י פְּדֵֽנִי׃
20: אַתָּ֤ה יָדַ֗עְתָּ חֶרְפָּתִ֣י וּ֭בָשְׁתִּי וּכְלִמָּתִ֑י נֶ֝גְדְּךָ֗ כָּל־ צוֹרְרָֽי׃
21: חֶרְפָּ֤ה ׀ שָֽׁבְרָ֥ה לִבִּ֗י וָֽאָ֫נ֥וּשָׁה וָאֲקַוֶּ֣ה לָנ֣וּד וָאַ֑יִן וְ֝לַמְנַחֲמִ֗ים וְלֹ֣א מָצָֽאתִי׃
22: וַיִּתְּנ֣וּ בְּבָרוּתִ֣י רֹ֑אשׁ וְ֝לִצְמָאִ֗י יַשְׁק֥וּנִי חֹֽמֶץ׃
23: יְהִֽי־ שֻׁלְחָנָ֣ם לִפְנֵיהֶ֣ם לְפָ֑ח וְלִשְׁלוֹמִ֥ים לְמוֹקֵֽשׁ׃
24: תֶּחְשַׁ֣כְנָה עֵ֭ינֵיהֶם מֵרְא֑וֹת וּ֝מָתְנֵ֗יהֶם תָּמִ֥יד הַמְעַֽד׃
25: שְׁפָךְ־ עֲלֵיהֶ֥ם זַעְמֶ֑ךָ וַחֲר֥וֹן אַ֝פְּךָ֗ יַשִּׂיגֵֽם׃
26: תְּהִי־ טִֽירָתָ֥ם נְשַׁמָּ֑ה בְּ֝אָהֳלֵיהֶ֗ם אַל־ יְהִ֥י יֹשֵֽׁב׃
27: כִּֽי־ אַתָּ֣ה אֲשֶׁר־ הִכִּ֣יתָ רָדָ֑פוּ וְאֶל־ מַכְא֖וֹב חֲלָלֶ֣יךָ יְסַפֵּֽרוּ׃
28: תְּֽנָה־ עָ֭וֺן עַל־ עֲוֺנָ֑ם וְאַל־ יָ֝בֹ֗אוּ בְּצִדְקָתֶֽךָ׃
29: יִ֭מָּחֽוּ מִסֵּ֣פֶר חַיִּ֑ים וְעִ֥ם צַ֝דִּיקִ֗ים אַל־ יִכָּתֵֽבוּ׃
30: וַ֭אֲנִי עָנִ֣י וְכוֹאֵ֑ב יְשׁוּעָתְךָ֖ אֱלֹהִ֣ים תְּשַׂגְּבֵֽנִי׃
31: אֲהַֽלְלָ֣ה שֵׁם־ אֱלֹהִ֣ים בְּשִׁ֑יר וַאֲגַדְּלֶ֥נּוּ בְתוֹדָֽה׃
32: וְתִיטַ֣ב לַֽ֭יהוָה מִשּׁ֥וֹר פָּ֗ר מַקְרִ֥ן מַפְרִֽיס׃
33: רָא֣וּ עֲנָוִ֣ים יִשְׂמָ֑חוּ דֹּרְשֵׁ֥י אֱ֝לֹהִ֗ים וִיחִ֥י לְבַבְכֶֽם׃
34: כִּֽי־ שֹׁמֵ֣עַ אֶל־ אֶבְיוֹנִ֣ים יְהוָ֑ה וְאֶת־ אֲ֝סִירָ֗יו לֹ֣א בָזָֽה׃
35: יְֽ֭הַלְלוּהוּ שָׁמַ֣יִם וָאָ֑רֶץ יַ֝מִּ֗ים וְֽכָל־ רֹמֵ֥שׂ בָּֽם׃
36: כִּ֤י אֱלֹהִ֨ים ׀ י֘וֹשִׁ֤יעַ צִיּ֗וֹן וְ֭יִבְנֶה עָרֵ֣י יְהוּדָ֑ה וְיָ֥שְׁבוּ שָׁ֝֗ם וִירֵשֽׁוּהָ׃
37: וְזֶ֣רַע עֲ֭בָדָיו יִנְחָל֑וּהָ וְאֹהֲבֵ֥י שְׁ֝מ֗וֹ יִשְׁכְּנוּ־ בָֽהּ׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 69

## Scene 1: Cry for Help

> Save me, O God, for the waters have come up to my neck. I sink in the miry depths, where there is no foothold. I have come into the deep waters; the floods engulf me. I am worn out calling for help; my throat is parched. My eyes fail, looking for my God. - Psalm 69:1-3 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist is in distress, surrounded by deep waters and floods. They are crying out for help, feeling exhausted and parched.
- Imagery:
  - Deep waters and floods symbolize overwhelming trials and challenges.
  - Throat parched and eyes failing show the psalmist's desperation and exhaustion.

## Scene 2: Persecution and Scorn

> Those who hate me without reason outnumber the hairs of my head; many are my enemies without cause, those who seek to destroy me. I am forced to restore what I did not steal. You, God, know my folly; my guilt is not hidden from you. - Psalm 69:4-5 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist is being persecuted and scorned by numerous enemies for no apparent reason. They are falsely accused and unjustly burdened.
- Imagery:
  - Enemies outnumbering the hairs of the head represents the multitude of adversaries and their relentless attacks.
  - Being forced to restore what was not stolen portrays the unjust burden placed upon the psalmist.
  - God knowing the psalmist's folly and guilt highlights the psalmist's honesty and transparency before God.

## Scene 3: Defending God's Name

> May those who hope in you not be disgraced because of me, Lord, the Lord Almighty; may those who seek you not be put to shame because of me, God of Israel. - Psalm 69:6 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist appeals to God, asking that those who trust in Him would not experience disgrace or shame due to the psalmist's circumstances.
- Imagery:
  - The psalmist's concern for the reputation of God's name reflects their desire for righteousness and vindication.

## Scene 4: Isolation and Rejection

> For I endure scorn for your sake, and shame covers my face. I am a foreigner to my own family, a stranger to my own mother’s children; for zeal for your house consumes me, and the insults of those who insult you fall on me. - Psalm 69:7-9 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist experiences shame and isolation due to their loyalty to God. They are rejected by their own family and treated as a stranger.
- Imagery:
  - Scorn and shame covering the psalmist's face signifies the social humiliation they endure.
  - Being a foreigner to their own family and a stranger to their own mother's children highlights the psalmist's profound sense of isolation and rejection.
  - Zeal for God's house and bearing insults meant for God depict the psalmist's wholehearted devotion to God.

## Scene 5: Lamentation for Deliverance

> When I weep and fast, I must endure scorn; when I put on sackcloth, people make sport of me. Those who sit at the gate mock me, and I am the song of the drunkards. - Psalm 69:10-12 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist laments the mockery and scorn they face when expressing their sorrow and seeking God's deliverance.
- Imagery:
  - Weeping, fasting, and wearing sackcloth represent the psalmist's deep sorrow and repentance.
  - Mockery from those at the gate and being ridiculed as the song of drunkards underline the psalmist's humiliation and public shame.

## Scene 6: Personal Confession of Sin

> But I pray to you, Lord, in the time of your favor; in your great love, O God, answer me with your sure salvation. Rescue me from the mire, do not let me sink; deliver me from those who hate me, from the deep waters. Do not let the floodwaters engulf me or the depths swallow me up or the pit close its mouth over me. Answer me, Lord, out of the goodness of your love; in your great mercy turn to me. Do not hide your face from your servant; answer me quickly, for I am in trouble. Come near and rescue me; deliver me because of my foes. - Psalm 69:13-18 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist prays to God, expressing their trust in His love and salvation. They cry out for deliverance from their enemies, the deep waters, and the pit of despair.
- Imagery:
  - Praying to God in the time of His favor and His great love highlights the psalmist's reliance on God's mercy and timing.
  - Mire, deep waters, and the pit symbolize the psalmist's desperate situation and the need for rescue.
  - The plea for God not to hide His face and to answer quickly shows the psalmist's longing for God's presence and intervention.

## Scene 7: Cry for Vengeance

> You know how I am scorned, disgraced and shamed; all my enemies are before you. Scorn has broken my heart and has left me helpless; I looked for sympathy, but there was none, for comforters, but I found none. They put gall in my food and gave me vinegar for my thirst. May the table set before them become a snare; may it become retribution and a trap. - Psalm 69:19-22 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges their state of scorn, disgrace, and shame. They express their sense of brokenness and helplessness. The psalmist longs for justice and vengeance against their enemies.
- Imagery:
  - Scorn, disgrace, and shame depict the psalmist's emotional and social distress.
  - The lack of sympathy and comforters intensify the psalmist's feelings of loneliness and abandonment.
  - The reference to gall and vinegar symbolizes the cruelty and bitterness the psalmist endures.
  - The plea for the enemies' table to become a snare and retribution represents the psalmist's desire for divine justice upon their adversaries.

## Scene 8: Call for Deliverance

> May their eyes be darkened so they cannot see, and their backs be bent forever. Pour out your wrath on them; let your fierce anger overtake them. May their place be deserted; let there be no one to dwell in their tents. For they persecute those you wound and talk about the pain of those you hurt. Charge them with crime upon crime; do not let them share in your salvation. - Psalm 69:23-27 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist calls for divine judgment upon their enemies, desiring their spiritual blindness and lasting consequences for their actions.
- Imagery:
  - Eyes being darkened and backs being bent symbolize the psalmist's plea for the enemies to experience the consequences of their wicked actions.
  - Pouring out wrath, fierce anger, and deserted dwellings reflect the psalmist's desire for divine justice and abandonment of their adversaries.
  - The reference to persecuting those wounded by God and talking about the pain of those hurt portrays the malicious intent of the enemies.
  - The plea for the enemies not to share in God's salvation underlines the psalmist's desire to see them excluded from God's blessings.

## Scene 9: Praise and Hope in God's Salvation

> May they be blotted out of the book of life and not be listed with the righteous. But as for me, afflicted and in pain— may your salvation, God, protect me. I will praise God’s name in song and glorify him with thanksgiving. This will please the Lord more than an ox, more than a bull with its horns and hooves. The poor will see and be glad— you who seek God, may your hearts live! The Lord hears the needy and does not despise his captive people. - Psalm 69:28-33 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist expresses their hope in God's salvation and offers praise and thanksgiving. They long for the vindication of the righteous and the joy of the poor and needy.
- Imagery:
  - Being blotted out of the book of life signifies the psalmist's plea for the enemies to be removed from the community of the righteous.
  - Affliction and pain highlight the psalmist's current state, while God's salvation is seen as their protection and deliverance.
  - Praising God's name, singing songs, and offering thanksgiving demonstrate the psalmist's gratefulness and devotion.
  - The reference to an ox and a bull with its horns and hooves represents the psalmist's understanding that heartfelt praise and thanksgiving are more valuable to God than ritual sacrifices.
  - The mention of the poor being glad and those who seek God having their hearts live shows the psalmist's desire for others to experience the joy and life that comes from a relationship with God.
  - The psalmist's confidence in God's character is expressed through the belief that the Lord hears the needy and does not despise His captive people.

## Scene 10: Assurance of God's Deliverance

> Let heaven and earth praise him, the seas and all that move in them, for God will save Zion and rebuild the cities of Judah. Then people will settle there and possess it; the children of his servants will inherit it, and those who love his name will dwell there. - Psalm 69:34-36 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist declares that heaven, earth, and all living creatures should praise God because of His salvation and restoration of Zion and the cities of Judah. Those who love God's name will inherit and dwell in these places.
- Imagery:
  - The call for praise from heaven, earth, seas, and all creatures emphasizes the psalmist's desire for universal acknowledgment of God's deliverance and goodness.
  - The reference to saving Zion and rebuilding the cities of Judah conveys the psalmist's hope for the restoration of their people and their homeland.
  - Inheriting and dwelling in these places symbolize the blessings and security experienced by those who love God and remain faithful to His name.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict the psalmist's deep distress, persecution, and isolation. The vivid imagery of deep waters, floods, shame, and ridicule helps portray the psalmist's overwhelming trials and challenges. However, amidst the lamentation, the psalmist expresses their trust in God's love, salvation, and justice. They long for deliverance, offer praise and thanksgiving, and affirm their hope in God's restoration and ultimate victory. The eudaimonic significance lies in the psalmist's unwavering faith and reliance on God, despite their difficult circumstances. They exemplify the virtues of trust, perseverance, and devotion, finding solace and hope in their relationship with the Divine.
