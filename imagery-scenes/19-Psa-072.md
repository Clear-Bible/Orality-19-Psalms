# Psalm 72
1: לִשְׁלֹמֹ֨ה ׀ אֱֽלֹהִ֗ים מִ֭שְׁפָּטֶיךָ לְמֶ֣לֶךְ תֵּ֑ן וְצִדְקָתְךָ֥ לְבֶן־ מֶֽלֶךְ׃
2: יָדִ֣ין עַמְּךָ֣ בְצֶ֑דֶק וַעֲנִיֶּ֥יךָ בְמִשְׁפָּֽט׃
3: יִשְׂא֤וּ הָרִ֓ים שָׁ֘ל֥וֹם לָעָ֑ם וּ֝גְבָע֗וֹת בִּצְדָקָֽה׃
4: יִשְׁפֹּ֤ט ׀ עֲֽנִיֵּי־ עָ֗ם י֭וֹשִׁיעַ לִבְנֵ֣י אֶבְי֑וֹן וִֽידַכֵּ֣א עוֹשֵֽׁק׃
5: יִֽירָא֥וּךָ עִם־ שָׁ֑מֶשׁ וְלִפְנֵ֥י יָ֝רֵ֗חַ דּ֣וֹר דּוֹרִֽים׃
6: יֵ֭רֵד כְּמָטָ֣ר עַל־ גֵּ֑ז כִּ֝רְבִיבִ֗ים זַרְזִ֥יף אָֽרֶץ׃
7: יִֽפְרַח־ בְּיָמָ֥יו צַדִּ֑יק וְרֹ֥ב שָׁ֝ל֗וֹם עַד־ בְּלִ֥י יָרֵֽחַ׃
8: וְ֭יֵרְדְּ מִיָּ֣ם עַד־ יָ֑ם וּ֝מִנָּהָ֗ר עַד־ אַפְסֵי־ אָֽרֶץ׃
9: לְ֭פָנָיו יִכְרְע֣וּ צִיִּ֑ים וְ֝אֹיְבָ֗יו עָפָ֥ר יְלַחֵֽכוּ׃
10: מַלְכֵ֬י תַרְשִׁ֣ישׁ וְ֭אִיִּים מִנְחָ֣ה יָשִׁ֑יבוּ מַלְכֵ֥י שְׁבָ֥א וּ֝סְבָ֗א אֶשְׁכָּ֥ר יַקְרִֽיבוּ׃
11: וְיִשְׁתַּחֲווּ־ ל֥וֹ כָל־ מְלָכִ֑ים כָּל־ גּוֹיִ֥ם יַֽעַבְדֽוּהוּ׃
12: כִּֽי־ יַ֭צִּיל אֶבְי֣וֹן מְשַׁוֵּ֑עַ וְ֝עָנִ֗י וְֽאֵין־ עֹזֵ֥ר לֽוֹ׃
13: יָ֭חֹס עַל־ דַּ֣ל וְאֶבְי֑וֹן וְנַפְשׁ֖וֹת אֶבְיוֹנִ֣ים יוֹשִֽׁיעַ׃
14: מִתּ֣וֹךְ וּ֭מֵחָמָס יִגְאַ֣ל נַפְשָׁ֑ם וְיֵיקַ֖ר דָּמָ֣ם בְּעֵינָֽיו׃
15: וִיחִ֗י וְיִתֶּן־ לוֹ֮ מִזְּהַ֪ב שְׁ֫בָ֥א וְיִתְפַּלֵּ֣ל בַּעֲד֣וֹ תָמִ֑יד כָּל־ הַ֝יּ֗וֹם יְבָרֲכֶֽנְהֽוּ׃
16: יְהִ֤י פִסַּת־ בַּ֨ר ׀ בָּאָרֶץ֮ בְּרֹ֪אשׁ הָ֫רִ֥ים יִרְעַ֣שׁ כַּלְּבָנ֣וֹן פִּרְי֑וֹ וְיָצִ֥יצוּ מֵ֝עִ֗יר כְּעֵ֣שֶׂב הָאָֽרֶץ׃
17: יְהִ֤י שְׁמ֨וֹ לְֽעוֹלָ֗ם לִפְנֵי־ שֶׁמֶשׁ֮ שְׁ֫מ֥וֹ וְיִתְבָּ֥רְכוּ ב֑וֹ כָּל־ גּוֹיִ֥ם יְאַשְּׁרֽוּהוּ׃
18: בָּר֤וּךְ ׀ יְהוָ֣ה אֱ֭לֹהִים אֱלֹהֵ֣י יִשְׂרָאֵ֑ל עֹשֵׂ֖ה נִפְלָא֣וֹת לְבַדּֽוֹ׃
19: וּבָר֤וּךְ ׀ שֵׁ֥ם כְּבוֹד֗וֹ לְע֫וֹלָ֥ם וְיִמָּלֵ֣א כְ֭בוֹדוֹ אֶת־ כֹּ֥ל הָאָ֗רֶץ אָ֘מֵ֥ן ׀ וְאָמֵֽן׃
20: כָּלּ֥וּ תְפִלּ֑וֹת דָּ֝וִ֗ד בֶּן־ יִשָֽׁי׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 72

## Scene 1: The King's Rule over the Earth

> "Endow the king with your justice, O God, the royal son with your righteousness. May he judge your people in righteousness, your afflicted ones with justice." - Psalm 72:1-2 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The king is portrayed as ruling with justice and righteousness.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the king being endowed with God's justice and righteousness, symbolizing his authority and the divine source of his rule.
  - The image of the king judging the people with righteousness, indicating his responsibility to ensure fairness and equity for the afflicted.

## Scene 2: The Blessings of the King's Rule

> "May the mountains bring prosperity to the people, the hills the fruit of righteousness. May he defend the afflicted among the people and save the children of the needy; may he crush the oppressor." - Psalm 72:3-4 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The king's rule brings blessings and prosperity to the people, along with protection and deliverance from oppression.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the mountains bringing forth prosperity, symbolizing abundance and fertility.
  - The image of the hills producing the fruit of righteousness, representing the evidence of a just society under the king's rule.
  - The image of the king defending the afflicted and saving the children of the needy, portraying his role as a protector and savior.
  - The image of the king crushing the oppressor, symbolizing his power to overcome injustice and tyranny.

## Scene 3: The Extent of the King's Rule

> "May he endure as long as the sun, as long as the moon, through all generations. May he be like rain falling on a mown field, like showers watering the earth." - Psalm 72:5-6 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The king's rule is depicted as enduring and far-reaching, comparable to the sun, moon, rain, and showers.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the king enduring as long as the sun and moon, signifying his lasting and continuous reign.
  - The image of the king being like rain falling on a mown field, representing his refreshing and revitalizing effect on the people.
  - The image of the king being like showers watering the earth, symbolizing his ability to bring growth and abundance to the land.

## Scene 4: The King's Rule and the Righteousness of the People

> "In his days may the righteous flourish and prosperity abound till the moon is no more. May he rule from sea to sea and from the River to the ends of the earth." - Psalm 72:7-8 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The king's rule leads to the flourishing of the righteous and widespread prosperity.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the righteous flourishing under the king's rule, indicating their growth and well-being.
  - The image of prosperity abounding until the moon is no more, emphasizing the abundance and enduring nature of the blessings.
  - The image of the king ruling from sea to sea and from the River to the ends of the earth, highlighting the extent of his dominion and influence.

## Scene 5: The Kings and Rulers Pay Tribute to the King

> "The desert tribes will bow before him and his enemies will lick the dust. The kings of Tarshish and of distant shores will bring tribute to him; the kings of Sheba and Seba will present him gifts." - Psalm 72:9-10 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The kings and rulers of distant lands acknowledge the king's authority and honor him with tribute and gifts.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the desert tribes bowing before the king, signifying their submission and recognition of his power.
  - The image of the king's enemies licking the dust, symbolizing their defeat and humiliation before him.
  - The image of the kings of Tarshish and distant shores bringing tribute, representing the recognition and homage paid by powerful and distant rulers.
  - The image of the kings of Sheba and Seba presenting gifts, portraying the acknowledgment and reverence of wealthy and influential rulers.

## Scene 6: The King's Care for the Needy and Oppressed

> "All kings will bow down to him and all nations will serve him. For he will deliver the needy who cry out, the afflicted who have no one to help." - Psalm 72:11-12 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The king's rule is characterized by the universal recognition of his authority and his compassion for the needy and oppressed.
- Imagery:
  - The image of all kings bowing down to the king, representing their submission and acknowledgment of his supremacy.
  - The image of all nations serving him, signifying their obedience and allegiance to his rule.
  - The image of the king delivering the needy who cry out and the afflicted who have no one to help, symbolizing his care and support for the vulnerable and marginalized.

## Scene 7: The Blessings of the King's Rule on the Land

> "He will take pity on the weak and the needy and save the needy from death. He will rescue them from oppression and violence, for precious is their blood in his sight." - Psalm 72:13-14 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The king's rule brings compassion, protection, and deliverance to the weak, needy, and oppressed.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the king taking pity on the weak and needy, indicating his merciful and compassionate nature.
  - The image of the king saving the needy from death, symbolizing his role as a savior and protector.
  - The image of the king rescuing them from oppression and violence, portraying his ability to bring justice and peace.
  - The image of the preciousness of the blood of the weak and needy in the king's sight, emphasizing their value and worth in his eyes.

## Scene 8: The Prosperity and Abundance Under the King's Rule

> "Long may he live! May gold from Sheba be given him. May people ever pray for him and bless him all day long. May grain abound throughout the land; on the tops of the hills may it sway." - Psalm 72:15-16 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The king's reign is associated with longevity, abundance, and continuous blessing.
- Imagery:
  - The image of long life for the king, signifying stability and the desire for his reign to endure.
  - The image of gold from Sheba being given to the king, representing the prominence and wealth attributed to his rule.
  - The image of people praying for and blessing the king all day long, symbolizing their support and favor.
  - The image of grain abounding throughout the land and swaying on the tops of the hills, illustrating the abundance and prosperity under the king's rule.

## Scene 9: The Widespread Influence of the King's Rule

> "May the crops of the city flourish like Lebanon and thrive like the grass of the field. May his name endure forever; may it continue as long as the sun." - Psalm 72:16-17a (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The king's rule has a far-reaching influence, impacting the prosperity of the city and the enduring recognition of his name.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the crops of the city flourishing like Lebanon, symbolizing the fertility and abundance brought by the king's rule.
  - The image of the crops thriving like the grass of the field, indicating the growth and prosperity enjoyed under the king's reign.
  - The image of the king's name enduring forever and continuing as long as the sun, underscoring the lasting impact and recognition of his rule.

## Scene 10: Final Praise and Benediction

> "Then all nations will be blessed through him, and they will call him blessed. Praise be to the LORD God, the God of Israel, who alone does marvelous deeds." - Psalm 72:17b-18 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - All nations are blessed through the king, eliciting praise and recognition of God's marvelous acts.
- Imagery:
  - The image of all nations being blessed through the king, signifying the positive impact of his rule on a global scale.
  - The image of people calling the king blessed, indicating their acknowledgment of his divinely-bestowed favor and blessings.
  - The image of praising the LORD God, the God of Israel, who alone does marvelous deeds, emphasizing the recognition of God's sovereignty and the source of the king's authority.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict the idealized image of a righteous and just king ruling over the earth. The king's rule brings blessings, prosperity, and deliverance to the people. His authority is recognized by kings and rulers from distant lands, who pay tribute and bring gifts. The king demonstrates care and compassion for the weak, needy, and oppressed. Under his rule, there is widespread prosperity and abundance. The influence of his reign extends far and wide, and all nations are blessed through him. This Psalm portrays the eudaimonic meaning of a just and righteous ruler who brings harmony, peace, and justice to his people, reflecting God's own character and divine plan for humanity.
