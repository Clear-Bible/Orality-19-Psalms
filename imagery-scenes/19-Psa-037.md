# Psalm 37
1: לְדָוִ֨ד ׀ אַל־ תִּתְחַ֥ר בַּמְּרֵעִ֑ים אַל־ תְּ֝קַנֵּ֗א בְּעֹשֵׂ֥י עַוְלָֽה׃
2: כִּ֣י כֶ֭חָצִיר מְהֵרָ֣ה יִמָּ֑לוּ וּכְיֶ֥רֶק דֶּ֝֗שֶׁא יִבּוֹלֽוּן׃
3: בְּטַ֣ח בַּֽ֭יהוָה וַעֲשֵׂה־ ט֑וֹב שְׁכָן־ אֶ֝֗רֶץ וּרְעֵ֥ה אֱמוּנָֽה׃
4: וְהִתְעַנַּ֥ג עַל־ יְהוָ֑ה וְיִֽתֶּן־ לְ֝ךָ֗ מִשְׁאֲלֹ֥ת לִבֶּֽךָ׃
5: גּ֣וֹל עַל־ יְהוָ֣ה דַּרְכֶּ֑ךָ וּבְטַ֥ח עָ֝לָ֗יו וְה֣וּא יַעֲשֶֽׂה׃
6: וְהוֹצִ֣יא כָא֣וֹר צִדְקֶ֑ךָ וּ֝מִשְׁפָּטֶ֗ךָ כַּֽצָּהֳרָֽיִם׃
7: דּ֤וֹם ׀ לַיהוָה֮ וְהִתְח֪וֹלֵ֫ל ל֥וֹ אַל־ תִּ֭תְחַר בְּמַצְלִ֣יחַ דַּרְכּ֑וֹ בְּ֝אִ֗ישׁ עֹשֶׂ֥ה מְזִמּֽוֹת׃
8: הֶ֣רֶף מֵ֭אַף וַעֲזֹ֣ב חֵמָ֑ה אַל־ תִּ֝תְחַ֗ר אַךְ־ לְהָרֵֽעַ׃
9: כִּֽי־ מְ֭רֵעִים יִכָּרֵת֑וּן וְקֹוֵ֥י יְ֝הוָ֗ה הֵ֣מָּה יִֽירְשׁוּ־ אָֽרֶץ׃
10: וְע֣וֹד מְ֭עַט וְאֵ֣ין רָשָׁ֑ע וְהִתְבּוֹנַ֖נְתָּ עַל־ מְקוֹמ֣וֹ וְאֵינֶֽנּוּ׃
11: וַעֲנָוִ֥ים יִֽירְשׁוּ־ אָ֑רֶץ וְ֝הִתְעַנְּג֗וּ עַל־ רֹ֥ב שָׁלֽוֹם׃
12: זֹמֵ֣ם רָ֭שָׁע לַצַּדִּ֑יק וְחֹרֵ֖ק עָלָ֣יו שִׁנָּֽיו׃
13: אֲדֹנָ֥י יִשְׂחַק־ ל֑וֹ כִּֽי־ רָ֝אָ֗ה כִּֽי־ יָבֹ֥א יוֹמֽוֹ׃
14: חֶ֤רֶב ׀ פָּֽתְח֣וּ רְשָׁעִים֮ וְדָרְכ֪וּ קַ֫שְׁתָּ֥ם לְ֭הַפִּיל עָנִ֣י וְאֶבְי֑וֹן לִ֝טְב֗וֹחַ יִשְׁרֵי־ דָֽרֶךְ׃
15: חַ֭רְבָּם תָּב֣וֹא בְלִבָּ֑ם וְ֝קַשְּׁתוֹתָ֗ם תִּשָּׁבַֽרְנָה׃
16: טוֹב־ מְ֭עַט לַצַּדִּ֑יק מֵ֝הֲמ֗וֹן רְשָׁעִ֥ים רַבִּֽים׃
17: כִּ֤י זְרוֹע֣וֹת רְ֭שָׁעִים תִּשָּׁבַ֑רְנָה וְסוֹמֵ֖ךְ צַדִּיקִ֣ים יְהוָֽה׃
18: יוֹדֵ֣עַ יְ֭הוָה יְמֵ֣י תְמִימִ֑ם וְ֝נַחֲלָתָ֗ם לְעוֹלָ֥ם תִּהְיֶֽה׃
19: לֹֽא־ יֵ֭בֹשׁוּ בְּעֵ֣ת רָעָ֑ה וּבִימֵ֖י רְעָב֣וֹן יִשְׂבָּֽעוּ׃
20: כִּ֤י רְשָׁעִ֨ים ׀ יֹאבֵ֗דוּ וְאֹיְבֵ֣י יְ֭הוָה כִּיקַ֣ר כָּרִ֑ים כָּל֖וּ בֶעָשָׁ֣ן כָּֽלוּ׃
21: לֹוֶ֣ה רָ֭שָׁע וְלֹ֣א יְשַׁלֵּ֑ם וְ֝צַדִּ֗יק חוֹנֵ֥ן וְנוֹתֵֽן׃
22: כִּ֣י מְ֭בֹרָכָיו יִ֣ירְשׁוּ אָ֑רֶץ וּ֝מְקֻלָּלָ֗יו יִכָּרֵֽתוּ׃
23: מֵ֭יְהוָה מִֽצְעֲדֵי־ גֶ֥בֶר כּוֹנָ֗נוּ וְדַרְכּ֥וֹ יֶחְפָּֽץ׃
24: כִּֽי־ יִפֹּ֥ל לֹֽא־ יוּטָ֑ל כִּֽי־ יְ֝הוָ֗ה סוֹמֵ֥ךְ יָדֽוֹ׃
25: נַ֤עַר ׀ הָיִ֗יתִי גַּם־ זָ֫קַ֥נְתִּי וְֽלֹא־ רָ֭אִיתִי צַדִּ֣יק נֶעֱזָ֑ב וְ֝זַרְע֗וֹ מְבַקֶּשׁ־ לָֽחֶם׃
26: כָּל־ הַ֭יּוֹם חוֹנֵ֣ן וּמַלְוֶ֑ה וְ֝זַרְע֗וֹ לִבְרָכָֽה׃
27: ס֣וּר מֵ֭רָע וַעֲשֵׂה־ ט֗וֹב וּשְׁכֹ֥ן לְעוֹלָֽם׃
28: כִּ֤י יְהוָ֨ה ׀ אֹ֘הֵ֤ב מִשְׁפָּ֗ט וְלֹא־ יַעֲזֹ֣ב אֶת־ חֲ֭סִידָיו לְעוֹלָ֣ם נִשְׁמָ֑רוּ וְזֶ֖רַע רְשָׁעִ֣ים נִכְרָֽת׃
29: צַדִּיקִ֥ים יִֽירְשׁוּ־ אָ֑רֶץ וְיִשְׁכְּנ֖וּ לָעַ֣ד עָלֶֽיהָ׃
30: פִּֽי־ צַ֭דִּיק יֶהְגֶּ֣ה חָכְמָ֑ה וּ֝לְשׁוֹנ֗וֹ תְּדַבֵּ֥ר מִשְׁפָּֽט׃
31: תּוֹרַ֣ת אֱלֹהָ֣יו בְּלִבּ֑וֹ לֹ֖א תִמְעַ֣ד אֲשֻׁרָיו׃
32: צוֹפֶ֣ה רָ֭שָׁע לַצַּדִּ֑יק וּ֝מְבַקֵּ֗שׁ לַהֲמִיתוֹ׃
33: יְ֭הוָה לֹא־ יַעַזְבֶ֣נּוּ בְיָד֑וֹ וְלֹ֥א יַ֝רְשִׁיעֶ֗נּוּ בְּהִשָּׁפְטֽוֹ׃
34: קַוֵּ֤ה אֶל־ יְהוָ֨ה ׀ וּשְׁמֹ֬ר דַּרְכּ֗וֹ וִֽ֭ירוֹמִמְךָ לָרֶ֣שֶׁת אָ֑רֶץ בְּהִכָּרֵ֖ת רְשָׁעִ֣ים תִּרְאֶֽה׃
35: רָ֭אִיתִי רָשָׁ֣ע עָרִ֑יץ וּ֝מִתְעָרֶ֗ה כְּאֶזְרָ֥ח רַעֲנָֽן׃
36: וַ֭יּֽ͏ַעֲבֹר וְהִנֵּ֣ה אֵינֶ֑נּוּ וָֽ֝אֲבַקְשֵׁ֗הוּ וְלֹ֣א נִמְצָֽא׃
37: שְׁמָר־ תָּ֭ם וּרְאֵ֣ה יָשָׁ֑ר כִּֽי־ אַחֲרִ֖ית לְאִ֣ישׁ שָׁלֽוֹם׃
38: וּֽ֭פֹשְׁעִים נִשְׁמְד֣וּ יַחְדָּ֑ו אַחֲרִ֖ית רְשָׁעִ֣ים נִכְרָֽתָה׃
39: וּתְשׁוּעַ֣ת צַ֭דִּיקִים מֵיְהוָ֑ה מָֽ֝עוּזָּ֗ם בְּעֵ֣ת צָרָֽה׃
40: וַֽיַּעְזְרֵ֥ם יְהוָ֗ה וַֽיְפַ֫לְּטֵ֥ם יְפַלְּטֵ֣ם מֵ֭רְשָׁעִים וְיוֹשִׁיעֵ֑ם כִּי־ חָ֥סוּ בֽוֹ׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 37

## Scene 1: The Injustice of the Wicked

> 1 אַ֥ל תִּתְחַ֫רְ֥ץ בַּמְּרֵעִ֑ים אַל־תְּקַנֵּ֥א בְּעֹשֵׂ֗י עַוְּלָֽה׃
Do not fret because of those who are evil or be envious of those who do wrong.

- Scene:
  - A believer observing evil actions and their consequences.
- Imagery:
  - The image of evil actions being committed.
  - The image of the believer's response - not being agitated or envious.

## Scene 2: The Eudaimonia of the Righteous

> 2 כִּֽי־כֶ֣חִל עֵ֭שֶׁב מְהֵרָ֣ה יִמָּלֵ֑א וּ֝צִ֗פּוֹר עַל־כֵּ֥ן יְעַףְּפוּ׃
For like the grass they will soon wither, like green plants they will soon die away.

- Scene:
  - Observing the transient nature of the wicked's prosperity.
- Imagery:
  - The image of grass quickly withering and green plants dying away.
  - The image of the temporary nature of the wicked's success.

## Scene 3: Trusting in the Divine Justice

> 3 בְּֽטַח־בַּ֭יהוָה וַעֲשֵׂ֣ה טֽוֹב׃ שְֽׁׁכָן־אֶ֖רֶץ וּרְעֵ֣ה אֱמוּנָֽה׃
Trust in the Lord and do good; dwell in the land and enjoy safe pasture.

- Scene:
  - Encouraging believers to trust in the Lord and continue to do good.
- Imagery:
  - The image of dwelling in the land and enjoying safe pasture.
  - The image of the divine justice and provision.

## Scene 4: Delighting in the Divine Guidance

> 4 וְ֭הִתְעַנַּ֣ג עַל־יְהוָ֑ה וְיִֽתֶּן־לְךָ֥ מִשְׁאֲלֹֽתֵי־לִֽבֶּ֝ךָ׃
Take delight in the Lord, and he will give you the desires of your heart.

- Scene:
  - Encouraging believers to find joy and satisfaction in their relationship with the Lord.
- Imagery:
  - The image of delighting in the Lord's presence and favor.
  - The image of the desires of the heart being fulfilled by the Lord.

## Scene 5: Committing One's Way to the Lord

> 5 גֹּ֣ול אֶ֭ת־יְהוָה דַּרְכֶּ֑ךָ וּבְטַ֥ח עָלָֽיוּ׃ וְֽה֗וּא יַֽעֲשֶׂ֥ה
Commit your way to the Lord; trust in him and he will do this:
- Scene:
  - Encouraging believers to entrust their way and plans to the Lord's guidance.
- Imagery:
  - The image of rolling one's way onto the Lord, entrusting it to His care.
  - The image of relying on the Lord's faithfulness and action.

## Scene 6: Waiting Patiently for the Divine Justice

> 6 וְהֹוצִ֥יא כָאֹ֗ורֶה צִדְקֶ֥ךָ וּדְינֶֽךָ׃
He will make your righteous reward shine like the dawn, your vindication like the noonday sun.

- Scene:
  - Encouraging believers to patiently wait for the divine justice and reward.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the righteous reward shining like the dawn.
  - The image of vindication shining brightly like the noonday sun.

## Scene 7: The Inheritance of the Righteous

> 7 הַ֭חֲרֵשׁ מִ֣לִּפְנֵ֣י יְהוָ֑ה וְ֝הֹוחִ֗ילָה לֹ֣ו אַל־תִּֽתְחָרֵֽשׁ׃
Be still before the Lord and wait patiently for him; do not fret when people succeed in their ways when they carry out their wicked schemes.

- Scene:
  - Encouraging believers to be still before the Lord, waiting for His deliverance.
- Imagery:
  - The image of being silent and patient before the Lord's presence.
  - The image of not being agitated or anxious when the wicked appear to succeed.

## Scene 8: The Destruction of the Wicked

> 8 חֲ֭רֵשׁ מֵֽאַף־וַֽ֝עֲזֹ֗ב חֵ֥מָּה אַל־תִּֽתְחָרֵֽשׁ׃
Refrain from anger and turn from wrath; do not fret—it leads only to evil.

- Scene:
  - Encouraging believers to refrain from anger and avoid acts of evil.
- Imagery:
  - The image of refraining from anger and turning away from wrath.
  - The image of not being agitated or fretful, as it leads to evil.

## Scene 9: The Inheritance of the Meek

> 9 כִּֽי־הָ֭רְשָׁעִים יִכָּרֵ֣תוּ וְכִתְיֻשִּׁ֑ים וְקָוִ֥ים יְ֝חֵ֗ילוּ אַךְ־עֹ֥וד מְעָֽט׃
For those who are evil will be destroyed, but those who hope in the Lord will inherit the land.

- Scene:
  - The contrast between the fate of the wicked and the inheritance of the righteous.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the wicked being cut off and the righteous inheriting the land.
  - The image of hope in the Lord as the foundation of the righteous' future.

## Scene 10: The Sufficiency of the Righteous' Inheritance

> 10 וְעָ֤וד מְֽ֝עַֽט־וְאֵין־רָשָׁ֗ע וּֽבִנְתֵי־רֶ֥שַׁע אֵינָֽם׃
A little while, and the wicked will be no more; though you look for them, they will not be found.

- Scene:
  - The temporary existence of the wicked and their ultimate disappearance.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the wicked soon ceasing to exist.
  - The image of the futile search for the wicked's presence.

## Scene 11: The Steps of the Faithful

> 11 וְעָנָ֤ו עַנְוִ֪ים יִ֫ירְשׁ֣וּ־אָּ֭רֶץ וְֽהִתְעַנְּג֥וּ עַל־רֹ֑וב שָׁלֹ֥ום׀ רֹבֵ֗ים׃
But the meek will inherit the land and enjoy peace and prosperity.

- Scene:
  - The inheritance and abundant blessings of the meek.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the meek inheriting the land.
  - The image of enjoying peace and prosperity in abundance.

## Scene 12: The Protection of the Righteous

> 12 ׳מְֽרַשְּׁעִ֣ים׀ יֹחִ֣יתּוּ חֶ֑רֶב וְכִ֖שְׁתוּ קַ֣שְׁתֹותָֽם׃
The wicked plot against the righteous and gnash their teeth at them;

- Scene:
  - The hostility and plotting of the wicked against the righteous.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the wicked sharpening their swords and preparing their bows.
  - The image of their hostile reaction - gnashing their teeth.

## Scene 13: The Insufficiency of the Wicked

> 13 אֲדֹנָ֗י יִשְׂחָֽק־לָ֥מֹו כִּֽי־רָאָ֑ה כִּ֡י־יָ֘בֹ֤א יֹ֘ומֹ֗ו {כ} like that of the wicked.

- Scene:
  - The insignificance and transience of the wicked's plans.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the Lord's amusement and mocking of the wicked's plans.
  - The image of the wicked's plans being ultimately futile and coming to nothing.

## Scene 14: The Help of the Righteous

> 14 חֶ֭רֶב פָּתוּחַ֣ה וְקֹ֣שֶׁת נְטוּיָ֑ה וּ֝מָ֗תוּ לְרַשָּׁ֥ע רִבְעָֽה׃
But the Lord laughs at the wicked, for he knows their day is coming.

- Scene:
  - Depicting the Lord's response to the wicked's hostility.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the Lord's laughter in response to the wicked's threats.
  - The image of the wicked's eventual downfall and judgment.

## Scene 15: The Wicked's Weapons Turned Against Them

> 15 ׳חֶ֭רֶב פָּֽנָתָה תִרְאֶ֣ה, מְתוּ־לָ֑חֳלָלִ֖ים
But their swords will pierce their own hearts, and their bows will be broken.

- Scene:
  - The irony and self-destructiveness of the wicked's weapons.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the wicked's swords turning against themselves, causing harm.
  - The image of their bows breaking, rendering them useless.

## Scene 16: The Sustenance of the Righteous

> 16 מֵֽעַֽ֝ט וְֽ־טײר עֹ֗וד וְאֵ֣ין רָשָֽׁע ׃
Better the little that the righteous have than the wealth of many wicked;

- Scene:
  - The contentment and sufficiency of the righteous.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the righteous having little yet being satisfied.
  - The image of the wealth of the wicked being inferior to the righteous' contentment.

## Scene 17: The Steps of the Righteous are Established by the Lord

> 17 כִּ֖י זְר֣וֹעַ רְשָׁעִ֣ים תִּשָּׁבֵ֑ר וְסֹומֵ֥ךְ צַדִּיקִֽים׃
for the power of the wicked will be broken, but the LORD upholds the righteous.

- Scene:
  - The contrast between the fate of the wicked and the support of the righteous by the Lord.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the power of the wicked being shattered.
  - The image of the Lord upholding and supporting the righteous.

## Scene 18: The Blessing of the Righteous' Inheritance

> 18 יָ֭דֹועַ יְהוָ֣ה יְמֵ֣י תְמִימִ֑ים וְנַחֲלָתָ֖ם לְעֹולָ֣ם תִּהְיֶֽה׃
The blameless spend their days under the LORD's care, and their inheritance will endure forever.

- Scene:
  - The security and everlasting nature of the righteous' inheritance.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the blameless being under the Lord's care and protection.
  - The image of the righteous' inheritance lasting forever.

## Scene 19: The Steps of the Righteous Ordered by the Lord

> 19 לֹֽא־יֵבֹ֥ושׁוּ בְ֗עֵת רָעֹ֥ות וּבִ֥ימֵי רָעָֽב יִשְׂבָּֽעוּ׃
In times of disaster they will not wither; in days of famine they will enjoy plenty.

- Scene:
  - The provision and sustenance of the righteous during times of adversity.
- Imagery:
  - The image of the righteous not withering during times of disaster.
  - The image of the righteous having an abundance during times of famine.

## Scene 20: The Favor and Blessing of the Lord

> 20 כִּ֣י חֵ֭רֶם רְֽשָׁעִ֣ים יֹאבֵ֑דוּ וְֽ־בֹטֵ
