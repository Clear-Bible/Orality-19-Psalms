# Psalm 122
1: שִׁ֥יר הַֽמַּעֲל֗וֹת לְדָ֫וִ֥ד שָׂ֭מַחְתִּי בְּאֹמְרִ֣ים לִ֑י בֵּ֖ית יְהוָ֣ה נֵלֵֽךְ׃
2: עֹ֭מְדוֹת הָי֣וּ רַגְלֵ֑ינוּ בִּ֝שְׁעָרַ֗יִךְ יְרוּשָׁלָֽ͏ִם׃
3: יְרוּשָׁלַ֥͏ִם הַבְּנוּיָ֑ה כְּ֝עִ֗יר שֶׁחֻבְּרָה־ לָּ֥הּ יַחְדָּֽו׃
4: שֶׁשָּׁ֨ם עָל֪וּ שְׁבָטִ֡ים שִׁבְטֵי־ יָ֭הּ עֵד֣וּת לְיִשְׂרָאֵ֑ל לְ֝הֹד֗וֹת לְשֵׁ֣ם יְהוָֽה׃
5: כִּ֤י שָׁ֨מָּה ׀ יָשְׁב֣וּ כִסְא֣וֹת לְמִשְׁפָּ֑ט כִּ֝סְא֗וֹת לְבֵ֣ית דָּוִֽיד׃
6: שַׁ֭אֲלוּ שְׁל֣וֹם יְרוּשָׁלָ֑͏ִם יִ֝שְׁלָ֗יוּ אֹהֲבָֽיִךְ׃
7: יְהִֽי־ שָׁל֥וֹם בְּחֵילֵ֑ךְ שַׁ֝לְוָ֗ה בְּאַרְמְנוֹתָֽיִךְ׃
8: לְ֭מַעַן אַחַ֣י וְרֵעָ֑י אֲדַבְּרָה־ נָּ֖א שָׁל֣וֹם בָּֽךְ׃
9: לְ֭מַעַן בֵּית־ יְהוָ֣ה אֱלֹהֵ֑ינוּ אֲבַקְשָׁ֖ה ט֣וֹב לָֽךְ׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 122

## Scene 1: Joyful pilgrimage to Jerusalem

> I rejoiced with those who said to me, "Let us go to the house of the LORD." - Psalm 122:1 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist expresses his joy upon hearing the invitation to go to the house of the LORD in Jerusalem.

- Imagery:
  - Rejoicing and happiness: The psalmist's heart is filled with joy at the prospect of going to the house of the LORD.

## Scene 2: Standing within Jerusalem's gates

> Our feet are standing in your gates, Jerusalem. - Psalm 122:2 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges their physical presence standing within the gates of Jerusalem.

- Imagery:
  - Security and protection: The gates of Jerusalem symbolize a place of safety and security for the psalmist, where they can experience God's protection.

## Scene 3: Jerusalem as a unified city

> Jerusalem is built like a city that is closely compacted together. - Psalm 122:3 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist describes the unity and solidarity within the city of Jerusalem.

- Imagery:
  - Unity and cohesion: The image of a city closely compacted together suggests a community that is united, harmonious, and bound together in purpose.

## Scene 4: The tribes of Israel gathering in Jerusalem

> That is where the tribes go up— the tribes of the LORD— to praise the name of the LORD according to the statute given to Israel. - Psalm 122:4 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist observes the tribes of Israel coming together in Jerusalem to worship and praise God.

- Imagery:
  - Gathering and pilgrimage: The tribes of Israel represent the people coming together as a unified community, gathering in Jerusalem to fulfill their sacred duty of worshiping God.

## Scene 5: David's throne in Jerusalem

> There stand the thrones for judgment, the thrones of the house of David. - Psalm 122:5 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist recognizes the presence of the thrones of the house of David, symbolizing the righteous rule and authority in Jerusalem.

- Imagery:
  - Justice and righteousness: The thrones for judgment represent the establishment of just and righteous governance in Jerusalem, rooted in the lineage of David.

## Scene 6: Praying for Jerusalem's peace

> Pray for the peace of Jerusalem: "May those who love you be secure." - Psalm 122:6 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist urges the readers to pray for the peace and security of Jerusalem.

- Imagery:
  - Peace and security: Jerusalem symbolizes a place of peace and security, and the psalmist calls for prayers to ensure the ongoing well-being and stability of the city.

## Scene 7: Blessings upon Jerusalem

> May there be peace within your walls and security within your citadels. - Psalm 122:7 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist expresses a desire for peace and security within the walls and citadels of Jerusalem.

- Imagery:
  - Peace and protection: The walls and citadels of Jerusalem represent the physical boundaries and fortifications that provide safety and protection to its inhabitants, and the psalmist invokes blessings of peace and security within these walls.

## Scene 8: For the sake of the house of the LORD

> For the sake of my family and friends, I will say, "Peace be within you." - Psalm 122:8 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist affirms their commitment to pray for peace and security in Jerusalem for the sake of their loved ones.

- Imagery:
  - Love and unity: The mention of family and friends highlights the psalmist's deep care and concern for their loved ones, and their desire for peace in Jerusalem extends beyond personal motives, embracing the well-being of the entire community.

## Scene 9: The prosperity of God's dwelling place

> For the sake of the house of the LORD our God, I will seek your prosperity. - Psalm 122:9 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist expresses their commitment to seek the prosperity and well-being of the house of the LORD.

- Imagery:
  - Divine presence and blessings: The house of the LORD represents a sacred dwelling place, and the psalmist desires to seek the prosperity and blessings that come from being in the presence of God.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict the joy and anticipation of a pilgrimage to Jerusalem, the unity and solidarity within the city, the gathering of the tribes of Israel for worship, the righteous rule of David's throne, and a fervent prayer for the peace and prosperity of Jerusalem. The imagery of gathering, unity, peace, and divine blessings convey the eudaimonic significance of the psalm, emphasizing the importance of community, worship, justice, and seeking God's presence for the well-being and flourishing of believers.
