# Psalm 116
1: אָ֭הַבְתִּי כִּֽי־ יִשְׁמַ֥ע ׀ יְהוָ֑ה אֶת־ ק֝וֹלִ֗י תַּחֲנוּנָֽי׃
2: כִּֽי־ הִטָּ֣ה אָזְנ֣וֹ לִ֑י וּבְיָמַ֥י אֶקְרָֽא׃
3: אֲפָפ֤וּנִי ׀ חֶבְלֵי־ מָ֗וֶת וּמְצָרֵ֣י שְׁא֣וֹל מְצָא֑וּנִי צָרָ֖ה וְיָג֣וֹן אֶמְצָֽא׃
4: וּבְשֵֽׁם־ יְהוָ֥ה אֶקְרָ֑א אָנָּ֥ה יְ֝הוָ֗ה מַלְּטָ֥ה נַפְשִֽׁי׃
5: חַנּ֣וּן יְהֹוָ֣ה וְצַדִּ֑יק וֵ֖אלֹהֵ֣ינוּ מְרַחֵֽם׃
6: שֹׁמֵ֣ר פְּתָאיִ֣ם יְהֹוָ֑ה דַּ֝לּוֹתִ֗י וְלִ֣י יְהוֹשִֽׁיעַ׃
7: שׁוּבִ֣י נַ֭פְשִׁי לִמְנוּחָ֑יְכִי כִּֽי־ יְ֝הוָ֗ה גָּמַ֥ל עָלָֽיְכִי׃
8: כִּ֤י חִלַּ֥צְתָּ נַפְשִׁ֗י מִ֫מָּ֥וֶת אֶת־ עֵינִ֥י מִן־ דִּמְעָ֑ה אֶת־ רַגְלִ֥י מִדֶּֽחִי׃
9: אֶ֭תְהַלֵּךְ לִפְנֵ֣י יְהוָ֑ה בְּ֝אַרְצ֗וֹת הַֽחַיִּֽים׃
10: הֶ֭אֱמַנְתִּי כִּ֣י אֲדַבֵּ֑ר אֲ֝נִ֗י עָנִ֥יתִי מְאֹֽד׃
11: אֲ֭נִי אָמַ֣רְתִּי בְחָפְזִ֑י כָּֽל־ הָאָדָ֥ם כֹּזֵֽב׃
12: מָֽה־ אָשִׁ֥יב לַיהוָ֑ה כָּֽל־ תַּגְמוּל֥וֹהִי עָלָֽי׃
13: כּוֹס־ יְשׁוּע֥וֹת אֶשָּׂ֑א וּבְשֵׁ֖ם יְהוָ֣ה אֶקְרָֽא׃
14: נְ֭דָרַי לַיהוָ֣ה אֲשַׁלֵּ֑ם נֶגְדָה־ נָּ֝֗א לְכָל־ עַמּֽוֹ׃
15: יָ֭קָר בְּעֵינֵ֣י יְהוָ֑ה הַ֝מָּ֗וְתָה לַחֲסִידָֽיו׃
16: אֽ͏ָנָּ֣ה יְהוָה֮ כִּֽי־ אֲנִ֪י עַ֫בְדֶּ֥ךָ אֲֽנִי־ עַ֭בְדְּךָ בֶּן־ אֲמָתֶ֑ךָ פִּ֝תַּ֗חְתָּ לְמוֹסֵרָֽי׃
17: לְֽךָ־ אֶ֭זְבַּח זֶ֣בַח תּוֹדָ֑ה וּבְשֵׁ֖ם יְהוָ֣ה אֶקְרָֽא׃
18: נְ֭דָרַי לַיהוָ֣ה אֲשַׁלֵּ֑ם נֶגְדָה־ נָּ֝֗א לְכָל־ עַמּֽוֹ׃
19: בְּחַצְר֤וֹת ׀ בֵּ֤ית יְהוָ֗ה בְּֽת֘וֹכֵ֤כִי יְֽרוּשָׁלִָ֗ם הַֽלְלוּ־ יָֽהּ׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 116

## Scene 1: The Cry for Help

> "I love the Lord, for he heard my voice; he heard my cry for mercy." - Psalm 116:1 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist expresses their love for the Lord.
  - The psalmist acknowledges that the Lord heard their voice and cry for mercy.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of the psalmist's voice and cry represents their desperate appeal to the Lord for help.
  - The act of the Lord hearing the psalmist's cry for mercy conveys his compassion and willingness to provide assistance.

## Scene 2: Deliverance from Death

> "Because he turned his ear to me, I will call on him as long as I live. The cords of death entangled me, the anguish of the grave came over me; I was overcome by distress and sorrow." - Psalm 116:2-3 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist vows to call on the Lord as long as they live, as a result of the Lord's attentive response to their plea.
  - The psalmist describes their experience of being entangled by the cords of death and overwhelmed by distress and sorrow.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of the psalmist calling on the Lord as long as they live signifies their commitment to ongoing faith and reliance on God.
  - The imagery of the cords of death symbolizes the psalmist's perilous state, emphasizing the severity of the situation.
  - The imagery of distress and sorrow conveys the psalmist's deep emotional anguish and the magnitude of their need to be delivered.

## Scene 3: The Lord's Compassion and Salvation

> "Then I called on the name of the Lord: 'Lord, save me!'" - Psalm 116:4 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist reaffirms their act of calling on the name of the Lord, specifically pleading for salvation.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of the psalmist calling on the name of the Lord signifies their recognition of God's power and authority to save.
  - The act of pleading for salvation reflects the psalmist's earnest desire for deliverance.

## Scene 4: The Graciousness of the Lord

> "The Lord is gracious and righteous; our God is full of compassion." - Psalm 116:5 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges the graciousness, righteousness, and compassion of the Lord.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of the Lord being gracious and righteous highlights His kindness and fairness in dealing with His people.
  - The imagery of the Lord being full of compassion conveys His deep empathy and care for His children.

## Scene 5: The Lord's Protection

> "The Lord protects the unwary; when I was brought low, he saved me." - Psalm 116:6 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist proclaims the protective nature of the Lord.
  - The psalmist recalls a personal experience of being brought low and then saved by the Lord.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of the Lord protecting the unwary emphasizes His watchful care over those who may be vulnerable or unaware of impending danger.
  - The imagery of being brought low signifies a state of weakness or distress, while being saved conveys the Lord's intervention and deliverance.

## Scene 6: Return to Rest

> "Return to your rest, my soul, for the Lord has been good to you." - Psalm 116:7 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist exhorts their own soul, instructing it to return to rest.
  - The psalmist acknowledges the goodness of the Lord towards them.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of returning to rest suggests a state of calmness, contentment, and trust in the Lord's benevolence.
  - The imagery of the Lord being good underscores His positive character and the psalmist's recognition of His kindness and generosity.

## Scene 7: The Lord's Rescue

> "For you, Lord, have delivered me from death, my eyes from tears, my feet from stumbling." - Psalm 116:8 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist declares that the Lord has delivered them from death, saved them from tears, and prevented their feet from stumbling.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of the Lord delivering from death signifies His power to rescue from mortal danger.
  - The imagery of saving from tears represents the Lord's ability to bring comfort and alleviate sorrow.
  - The imagery of preventing feet from stumbling conveys the Lord's guidance and protection, ensuring a steady and secure path.

## Scene 8: An Offering of Gratitude

> "I will walk before the Lord in the land of the living." - Psalm 116:9 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist declares their commitment to walk before the Lord in the land of the living, expressing gratitude for His deliverance.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of walking before the Lord signifies a life lived in obedience and faithfulness to Him.
  - The phrase "in the land of the living" represents the psalmist's appreciation for being alive and present in the world, acknowledging God's grace and continual provision.

## Scene 9: Faith in the Midst of Affliction

> "I trusted in the Lord when I said, 'I am greatly afflicted.'" - Psalm 116:10 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist affirms their trust in the Lord, even in the midst of great affliction.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of trusting in the Lord conveys a deep reliance and dependence on His strength and faithfulness.
  - The phrase "I am greatly afflicted" represents the psalmist's acknowledgment of their challenging circumstances and their choice to place their trust in God regardless.

## Scene 10: Declaration of Faith

> "In my alarm I said, 'Everyone is a liar.'" - Psalm 116:11 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist expresses their alarm and makes a declaration that everyone is a liar.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of alarm conveys the psalmist's sense of distress and unease.
  - The phrase "everyone is a liar" may serve as hyperbole, expressing the psalmist's disappointment and disillusionment with the deceptive and unreliable nature of people.

## Scene 11: The Response of Faith

> "What shall I return to the Lord for all his goodness to me?" - Psalm 116:12 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist ponders what they should offer in response to the Lord's goodness to them.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of returning to the Lord conveys the psalmist's desire to reciprocate God's goodness with their own actions.
  - The phrase "all his goodness to me" highlights the abundant blessings and favor the psalmist has received from the Lord.

## Scene 12: Offering of Thanksgiving

> "I will lift up the cup of salvation and call on the name of the Lord." - Psalm 116:13 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist declares their intention to lift up the cup of salvation and call on the name of the Lord as an offering of thanksgiving.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of lifting up the cup of salvation symbolizes the psalmist's act of gratitude and celebration for God's deliverance.
  - The imagery of calling on the name of the Lord represents the psalmist's acknowledgement of His authority and their desire for a continued relationship with Him.

## Scene 13: Fulfilling Vows

> "I will fulfill my vows to the Lord in the presence of all his people." - Psalm 116:14 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist pledges to fulfill their vows to the Lord in the presence of all His people.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of fulfilling vows signifies the psalmist's commitment to honoring their promises and dedicating themselves fully to God.
  - The phrase "in the presence of all his people" suggests a public declaration and demonstration of faithfulness to the Lord.

## Scene 14: Precious in the Sight of the Lord

> "Precious in the sight of the Lord is the death of his faithful servants." - Psalm 116:15 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist reflects on the value and significance of the death of the Lord's faithful servants.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of "precious" conveys the idea of something being treasured and highly regarded.
  - The phrase "the death of his faithful servants" emphasizes the Lord's recognition and special regard for the lives and ultimate sacrifice of His devoted followers.

## Scene 15: A Life of Thanksgiving

> "Truly I am your servant, Lord; I serve you just as my mother did; you have freed me from my chains." - Psalm 116:16 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist declares their identity as a servant of the Lord, comparing themselves to their mother's faithful service.
  - The psalmist acknowledges their liberation from chains by the Lord.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of being a servant of the Lord reflects the psalmist's commitment to a life of obedience and service.
  - The phrase "just as my mother did" highlights the psalmist's desire to follow in the footsteps of their mother's devotion to God.
  - The imagery of being freed from chains represents the psalmist's deliverance from bondage, whether literal or metaphorical.

## Scene 16: Offering Sacrifices of Praise

> "I will sacrifice a thank offering to you and call on the name of the Lord." - Psalm 116:17 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist declares their intention to offer a thank offering to the Lord and to call on His name.

- Imagery:
  - The imagery of sacrificing a thank offering signifies the psalmist's act of worship and gratitude towards God.
  - The imagery of calling on the name of the Lord represents the psalmist's ongoing reliance and trust in His power and provision.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict the psalmist's cry for help, deliverance from death, the Lord's compassion and salvation, His graciousness and protection, gratitude and faithfulness, and the psalmist's commitment to offering thanksgiving and fulfilling vows. The imagery in each scene portrays the psalmist's deep trust in the Lord, His willingness to hear their cry, and their sincere gratitude for His deliverance and faithfulness. It reminds believers of the goodness and compassion of God and inspires them to respond with thanksgiving, obedience, and a life dedicated to serving Him.
