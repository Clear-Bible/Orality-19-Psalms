# Psalm 105
1: הוֹד֣וּ לַ֭יהוָה קִרְא֣וּ בִּשְׁמ֑וֹ הוֹדִ֥יעוּ בָ֝עַמִּ֗ים עֲלִילוֹתָֽיו׃
2: שִֽׁירוּ־ ל֭וֹ זַמְּרוּ־ ל֑וֹ שִׂ֝֗יחוּ בְּכָל־ נִפְלְאוֹתָֽיו׃
3: הִֽ֭תְהַלְלוּ בְּשֵׁ֣ם קָדְשׁ֑וֹ יִ֝שְׂמַ֗ח לֵ֤ב ׀ מְבַקְשֵׁ֬י יְהוָֽה׃
4: דִּרְשׁ֣וּ יְהוָ֣ה וְעֻזּ֑וֹ בַּקְּשׁ֖וּ פָנָ֣יו תָּמִֽיד׃
5: זִכְר֗וּ נִפְלְאוֹתָ֥יו אֲשֶׁר־ עָשָׂ֑ה מֹ֝פְתָ֗יו וּמִשְׁפְּטֵי־ פִֽיו׃
6: זֶ֭רַע אַבְרָהָ֣ם עַבְדּ֑וֹ בְּנֵ֖י יַעֲקֹ֣ב בְּחִירָֽיו׃
7: ה֭וּא יְהוָ֣ה אֱלֹהֵ֑ינוּ בְּכָל־ הָ֝אָ֗רֶץ מִשְׁפָּטָֽיו׃
8: זָכַ֣ר לְעוֹלָ֣ם בְּרִית֑וֹ דָּבָ֥ר צִ֝וָּ֗ה לְאֶ֣לֶף דּֽוֹר׃
9: אֲשֶׁ֣ר כָּ֭רַת אֶת־ אַבְרָהָ֑ם וּשְׁב֖וּעָת֣וֹ לְיִשְׂחָֽק׃
10: וַיַּֽעֲמִידֶ֣הָ לְיַעֲקֹ֣ב לְחֹ֑ק לְ֝יִשְׂרָאֵ֗ל בְּרִ֣ית עוֹלָֽם׃
11: לֵאמֹ֗ר לְךָ֗ אֶתֵּ֥ן אֶת־ אֶֽרֶץ־ כְּנָ֑עַן חֶ֝֗בֶל נַחֲלַתְכֶֽם׃
12: בִּֽ֭הְיוֹתָם מְתֵ֣י מִסְפָּ֑ר כִּ֝מְעַ֗ט וְגָרִ֥ים בָּֽהּ׃
13: וַֽ֭יִּתְהַלְּכוּ מִגּ֣וֹי אֶל־ גּ֑וֹי מִ֝מַּמְלָכָ֗ה אֶל־ עַ֥ם אַחֵֽר׃
14: לֹֽא־ הִנִּ֣יחַ אָדָ֣ם לְעָשְׁקָ֑ם וַיּ֖וֹכַח עֲלֵיהֶ֣ם מְלָכִֽים׃
15: אַֽל־ תִּגְּע֥וּ בִמְשִׁיחָ֑י וְ֝לִנְבִיאַי אַל־ תָּרֵֽעוּ׃
16: וַיִּקְרָ֣א רָ֭עָב עַל־ הָאָ֑רֶץ כָּֽל־ מַטֵּה־ לֶ֥חֶם שָׁבָֽר׃
17: שָׁלַ֣ח לִפְנֵיהֶ֣ם אִ֑ישׁ לְ֝עֶ֗בֶד נִמְכַּ֥ר יוֹסֵֽף׃
18: עִנּ֣וּ בַכֶּ֣בֶל בַּ֝רְזֶ֗ל בָּ֣אָה נַפְשֽׁוֹ׃
19: עַד־ עֵ֥ת בֹּֽא־ דְבָר֑וֹ אִמְרַ֖ת יְהוָ֣ה צְרָפָֽתְהוּ׃
20: שָׁ֣לַח מֶ֭לֶךְ וַיַּתִּירֵ֑הוּ מֹשֵׁ֥ל עַ֝מִּ֗ים וַֽיְפַתְּחֵֽהוּ׃
21: שָׂמ֣וֹ אָד֣וֹן לְבֵית֑וֹ וּ֝מֹשֵׁ֗ל בְּכָל־ קִנְיָנֽוֹ׃
22: לֶאְסֹ֣ר שָׂרָ֣יו בְּנַפְשׁ֑וֹ וּזְקֵנָ֥יו יְחַכֵּֽם׃
23: וַיָּבֹ֣א יִשְׂרָאֵ֣ל מִצְרָ֑יִם וְ֝יַעֲקֹ֗ב גָּ֣ר בְּאֶֽרֶץ־ חָֽם׃
24: וַיֶּ֣פֶר אֶת־ עַמּ֣וֹ מְאֹ֑ד וַ֝יַּֽעֲצִמֵהוּ מִצָּרָֽיו׃
25: הָפַ֣ךְ לִ֭בָּם לִשְׂנֹ֣א עַמּ֑וֹ לְ֝הִתְנַכֵּ֗ל בַּעֲבָדָֽיו׃
26: שָׁ֭לַח מֹשֶׁ֣ה עַבְדּ֑וֹ אַ֝הֲרֹ֗ן אֲשֶׁ֣ר בָּֽחַר־ בּֽוֹ׃
27: שָֽׂמוּ־ בָ֭ם דִּבְרֵ֣י אֹתוֹתָ֑יו וּ֝מֹפְתִ֗ים בְּאֶ֣רֶץ חָֽם׃
28: שָׁ֣לַֽח חֹ֭שֶׁךְ וַיַּחְשִׁ֑ךְ וְלֹֽא־ מָ֝ר֗וּ אֶת־
29: הָפַ֣ךְ אֶת־ מֵימֵיהֶ֣ם לְדָ֑ם וַ֝יָּ֗מֶת אֶת־ דְּגָתָֽם׃
30: שָׁרַ֣ץ אַרְצָ֣ם צְפַרְדְּעִ֑ים בְּ֝חַדְרֵ֗י מַלְכֵיהֶֽם׃
31: אָ֭מַר וַיָּבֹ֣א עָרֹ֑ב כִּ֝נִּ֗ים בְּכָל־ גְּבוּלָֽם׃
32: נָתַ֣ן גִּשְׁמֵיהֶ֣ם בָּרָ֑ד אֵ֖שׁ לֶהָב֣וֹת בְּאַרְצָֽם׃
33: וַיַּ֣ךְ גַּ֭פְנָם וּתְאֵנָתָ֑ם וַ֝יְשַׁבֵּ֗ר עֵ֣ץ גְּבוּלָֽם׃
34: אָ֭מַר וַיָּבֹ֣א אַרְבֶּ֑ה וְ֝יֶ֗לֶק וְאֵ֣ין מִסְפָּֽר׃
35: וַיֹּ֣אכַל כָּל־ עֵ֣שֶׂב בְּאַרְצָ֑ם וַ֝יֹּ֗אכַל פְּרִ֣י אַדְמָתָֽם׃
36: וַיַּ֣ךְ כָּל־ בְּכ֣וֹר בְּאַרְצָ֑ם רֵ֝אשִׁ֗ית לְכָל־ אוֹנָֽם׃
37: וַֽ֭יּוֹצִיאֵם בְּכֶ֣סֶף וְזָהָ֑ב וְאֵ֖ין בִּשְׁבָטָ֣יו כּוֹשֵֽׁל׃
38: שָׂמַ֣ח מִצְרַ֣יִם בְּצֵאתָ֑ם כִּֽי־ נָפַ֖ל פַּחְדָּ֣ם עֲלֵיהֶֽם׃
39: פָּרַ֣שׂ עָנָ֣ן לְמָסָ֑ךְ וְ֝אֵ֗שׁ לְהָאִ֥יר לָֽיְלָה׃
40: שָׁאַ֣ל וַיָּבֵ֣א שְׂלָ֑ו וְלֶ֥חֶם שָׁ֝מַ֗יִם יַשְׂבִּיעֵֽם׃
41: פָּ֣תַח צ֭וּר וַיָּז֣וּבוּ מָ֑יִם הָ֝לְכ֗וּ בַּצִּיּ֥וֹת נָהָֽר׃
42: כִּֽי־ זָ֭כַר אֶת־ דְּבַ֣ר קָדְשׁ֑וֹ אֶֽת־ אַבְרָהָ֥ם עַבְדּֽוֹ׃
43: וַיּוֹצִ֣א עַמּ֣וֹ בְשָׂשׂ֑וֹן בְּ֝רִנָּ֗ה אֶת־ בְּחִירָֽיו׃
44: וַיִּתֵּ֣ן לָ֭הֶם אַרְצ֣וֹת גּוֹיִ֑ם וַעֲמַ֖ל לְאֻמִּ֣ים יִירָֽשׁוּ׃
45: בַּעֲב֤וּר ׀ יִשְׁמְר֣וּ חֻ֭קָּיו וְתוֹרֹתָ֥יו יִנְצֹ֗רוּ הַֽלְלוּ־ יָֽהּ׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 105

## Scene 1: God's Faithfulness to His Covenant 

> "Give praise to the Lord, proclaim his name; make known among the nations what he has done. Sing to him, sing praise to him; tell of all his wonderful acts." - Psalm 105:1-2 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist calls upon people to give praise to the Lord and proclaim His name.
- Imagery:
  - Proclaiming God's name and telling others of His wonderful acts.

## Scene 2: God's Mighty Deeds 

> "Glory in his holy name; let the hearts of those who seek the Lord rejoice. Look to the Lord and his strength; seek his face always." - Psalm 105:3-4 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist encourages people to rejoice and seek the Lord's strength and presence.
- Imagery:
  - The hearts of those who seek the Lord rejoicing.
  - Looking to the Lord for strength and seeking His face always.

## Scene 3: God's Covenant with Abraham 

> "Remember the wonders he has done, his miracles, and the judgments he pronounced, you his servants, the descendants of Abraham, his chosen ones, the children of Jacob." - Psalm 105:5-6 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist calls on the descendants of Abraham to remember the wonders, miracles, and judgments of God.
- Imagery:
  - Remembering the wonders and miracles performed by God.
  - Being the chosen ones and descendants of Abraham.

## Scene 4: God's Promise to the Patriarchs 

> "He remembers his covenant forever, the promise he made, for a thousand generations, the covenant he made with Abraham, the oath he swore to Isaac." - Psalm 105:8-9 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist emphasizes that God remembers His covenant forever, including the promise made to the patriarchs.
- Imagery:
  - God's everlasting remembrance of His covenant.
  - The promise made to Abraham and the oath sworn to Isaac.


## Scene 5: God's Deliverance of His People from Egypt 

> "He confirmed it to Jacob as a decree, to Israel as an everlasting covenant: 'To you I will give the land of Canaan as the portion you will inherit.'" - Psalm 105:10-11 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist reminds the people of God's decree and everlasting covenant to give them the land of Canaan.
- Imagery:
  - God's confirmation of His decree to Jacob and Israel.
  - The land of Canaan as the portion God's people will inherit.

## Scene 6: God's Preservation and Blessing in Egypt 

> "When they were but few in number, few indeed, and strangers in it, they wandered from nation to nation, from one kingdom to another. He allowed no one to oppress them; for their sake he rebuked kings." - Psalm 105:12-14 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist recounts how God protected and blessed the Israelites in Egypt, even when they were few in number.
- Imagery:
  - The Israelites wandering from nation to nation and from one kingdom to another.
  - God's protection from oppression and His rebuke of kings on behalf of His people.

## Scene 7: God's Miraculous Deliverance of His People 

> "He called down famine on the land and destroyed all their supplies of food; and he sent a man before them—Joseph, sold as a slave." - Psalm 105:16-17 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist recalls how God used Joseph to save His people during the famine by sending him ahead as a slave.
- Imagery:
  - God calling down famine on the land.
  - Joseph being sold as a slave and ultimately becoming a savior for his people.

## Scene 8: God's Faithfulness and Guidance 

> "God sent a man, a servant named Joseph, whom they sold into slavery. They bruised his feet with fetters and placed his neck in an iron collar..." - Psalm 105:17-18 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist continues to describe the hardships Joseph faced as a servant in Egypt.
- Imagery:
  - Joseph being sold into slavery and his feet being bruised with fetters.
  - His neck being placed in an iron collar.

## Scene 9: God's Exaltation of Joseph 

> "till what he foretold came to pass, till the word of the Lord proved him true. The king sent and released him, the ruler of peoples set him free. He made him lord of his house and ruler of all his possessions." - Psalm 105:19-21 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist highlights how Joseph's dreams and God's word eventually came true, resulting in his exaltation in Egypt.
- Imagery:
  - The fulfillment of Joseph's prophecies and the proving of God's word.
  - Joseph being released by the king and made lord of his house and ruler of all his possessions.

## Scene 10: God's Sending of Moses and Aaron 

> "Then Pharaoh sent for Joseph, and he was quickly brought out of the dungeon. When he had shaved and changed his clothes, he came before Pharaoh. Pharaoh said to Joseph, 'I had a dream, and no one can interpret it. But I have heard it said of you that when you hear a dream you can interpret it.'" - Psalm 105:22-23 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist describes how Pharaoh summoned Joseph to interpret his dream.
- Imagery:
  - Joseph being brought out of the dungeon and preparing himself before Pharaoh.
  - Pharaoh seeking Joseph's interpretation of dreams.

## Scene 11: God's Plagues on Egypt 

> "The king sent and released him, the ruler of peoples set him free. He made him lord of his house and ruler of all his possessions, to bind his princes at his pleasure and to teach his elders wisdom." - Psalm 105:21-22 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist recounts how Joseph's exaltation in Egypt enabled him to bind the princes and teach wisdom to the elders.
- Imagery:
  - Joseph being made a ruler and lord of his house and possessions.
  - The binding of the princes and the teaching of wisdom to the elders.

## Scene 12: God's Passover and Exodus 

> "Then he called down a famine on the land and destroyed all their supplies of food; and sent a man before them, Joseph, who was sold as a slave. They bruised his feet with shackles, his neck was put in a collar...." - Psalm 105:16-18 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist revisits the famine and Joseph's ordeal before describing the events of the Passover and Exodus.
- Imagery:
  - God calling down a famine and destroying supplies of food.
  - Joseph being sold as a slave, his feet being bruised with shackles, and his neck being put in a collar.

## Scene 13: God's Pillar of Cloud and Fire 

> "He spread out a cloud as a covering, and a fire to give light at night. They asked, and he brought them quail; he fed them well with the bread of heaven." - Psalm 105:39-40 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist recalls how God provided a cloud for covering and fire for light at night, and how He fed the Israelites with quail and the bread of heaven.
- Imagery:
  - The cloud spreading out as a covering and the fire giving light at night.
  - God's provision of quail and the heavenly bread.

## Scene 14: God's Giving of the Promised Land 

> "He opened the rock, and water gushed out; it flowed like a river in the desert. For he remembered his holy promise given to his servant Abraham." - Psalm 105:41-42 (NIV)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist describes how God provided water from a rock in the desert, fulfilling His promise to Abraham.
- Imagery:
  - The rock being opened, and water gushing out and flowing like a river.
  - God's remembrance of His holy promise to Abraham.

## Scene 15: God's Covenant and Remembering 

> "Then Israel came to Egypt; Jacob resided as a foreigner in the land of Ham. I will make them remember their sins, for I have been their enemy. But I will make them remember with my bowels, I will spare them as a mother who makes her children remember; but I will make them remember with my own bowels, I will remember my covenant." - Psalm 105:23-25 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist recalls how Jacob and his descendants became foreigners in Egypt, and how God made them remember their sins.
- Imagery:
  - Jacob residing as a foreigner in the land of Ham.
  - God's emotional connection to His people, sparing them as a mother who remembers her children.
  - God's remembrance of His covenant.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict God's faithfulness to His covenant and His mighty deeds. They illustrate His promises to the patriarchs and His deliverance of His people from Egypt. The imagery used conveys the eudaimonic significance of God's protection, provision, guidance, and remembrance of His covenant with His chosen ones. These scenes inspire believers to praise and seek the Lord, rejoice in His presence, and remember His wonderful acts.
