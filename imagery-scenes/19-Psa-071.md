# Psalm 71
1: בְּךָֽ־ יְהוָ֥ה חָסִ֑יתִי אַל־ אֵב֥וֹשָׁה לְעוֹלָֽם׃
2: בְּצִדְקָתְךָ֗ תַּצִּילֵ֥נִי וּֽתְפַלְּטֵ֑נִי הַטֵּֽה־ אֵלַ֥י אָ֝זְנְךָ֗ וְהוֹשִׁיעֵֽנִי׃
3: הֱיֵ֤ה לִ֨י ׀ לְצ֥וּר מָע֡וֹן לָב֗וֹא תָּמִ֗יד צִוִּ֥יתָ לְהוֹשִׁיעֵ֑נִי כִּֽי־ סַלְעִ֖י וּמְצוּדָתִ֣י אָֽתָּה׃
4: אֱֽלֹהַ֗י פַּ֭לְּטֵנִי מִיַּ֣ד רָשָׁ֑ע מִכַּ֖ף מְעַוֵּ֣ל וְחוֹמֵץ׃
5: כִּֽי־ אַתָּ֥ה תִקְוָתִ֑י אֲדֹנָ֥י יְ֝הוִ֗ה מִבְטַחִ֥י מִנְּעוּרָֽי׃
6: עָלֶ֤יךָ ׀ נִסְמַ֬כְתִּי מִבֶּ֗טֶן מִמְּעֵ֣י אִ֭מִּי אַתָּ֣ה גוֹזִ֑י בְּךָ֖ תְהִלָּתִ֣י תָמִֽיד׃
7: כְּ֭מוֹפֵת הָיִ֣יתִי לְרַבִּ֑ים וְ֝אַתָּ֗ה מַֽחֲסִי־ עֹֽז׃
8: יִמָּ֣לֵא פִ֭י תְּהִלָּתֶ֑ךָ כָּל־ הַ֝יּ֗וֹם תִּפְאַרְתֶּֽךָ׃
9: אַֽל־ תַּ֭שְׁלִיכֵנִי לְעֵ֣ת זִקְנָ֑ה כִּכְל֥וֹת כֹּ֝חִ֗י אֽ͏ַל־ תַּעַזְבֵֽנִי׃
10: כִּֽי־ אָמְר֣וּ אוֹיְבַ֣י לִ֑י וְשֹׁמְרֵ֥י נַ֝פְשִׁ֗י נוֹעֲצ֥וּ יַחְדָּֽו׃
11: לֵ֭אמֹר אֱלֹהִ֣ים עֲזָב֑וֹ רִֽדְפ֥וּ וְ֝תִפְשׂ֗וּהוּ כִּי־ אֵ֥ין מַצִּֽיל׃
12: אֱ֭לֹהִים אַל־ תִּרְחַ֣ק מִמֶּ֑נִּי אֱ֝לֹהַ֗י לְעֶזְרָ֥תִי
13: יֵבֹ֣שׁוּ יִכְלוּ֮ שֹׂטְנֵ֪י נַ֫פְשִׁ֥י יַֽעֲט֣וּ חֶ֭רְפָּה וּכְלִמָּ֑ה מְ֝בַקְשֵׁ֗י רָעָתִֽי׃
14: וַ֭אֲנִי תָּמִ֣יד אֲיַחֵ֑ל וְ֝הוֹסַפְתִּ֗י עַל־ כָּל־ תְּהִלָּתֶֽךָ׃
15: פִּ֤י ׀ יְסַפֵּ֬ר צִדְקָתֶ֗ךָ כָּל־ הַיּ֥וֹם תְּשׁוּעָתֶ֑ךָ כִּ֤י לֹ֖א יָדַ֣עְתִּי סְפֹרֽוֹת׃
16: אָב֗וֹא בִּ֭גְבֻרוֹת אֲדֹנָ֣י יְהוִ֑ה אַזְכִּ֖יר צִדְקָתְךָ֣ לְבַדֶּֽךָ׃
17: אֱֽלֹהִ֗ים לִמַּדְתַּ֥נִי מִנְּעוּרָ֑י וְעַד־ הֵ֝֗נָּה אַגִּ֥יד נִפְלְאוֹתֶֽיךָ׃
18: וְגַ֤ם עַד־ זִקְנָ֨ה ׀ וְשֵׂיבָה֮ אֱלֹהִ֪ים אַֽל־ תַּעַ֫זְבֵ֥נִי עַד־ אַגִּ֣יד זְרוֹעֲךָ֣ לְד֑וֹר לְכָל־ יָ֝ב֗וֹא גְּבוּרָתֶֽךָ׃
19: וְצִדְקָתְךָ֥ אֱלֹהִ֗ים עַד־ מָ֫ר֥וֹם אֲשֶׁר־ עָשִׂ֥יתָ גְדֹל֑וֹת אֱ֝לֹהִ֗ים מִ֣י כָמֽוֹךָ׃
20: אֲשֶׁ֤ר צָר֥וֹת רַבּ֗וֹת וְרָ֫ע֥וֹת תָּשׁ֥וּב וּֽמִתְּהֹמ֥וֹת הָ֝אָ֗רֶץ תָּשׁ֥וּב תַּעֲלֵֽנִי׃
21: תֶּ֤רֶב ׀ גְּֽדֻלָּתִ֗י וְתִסֹּ֥ב תְּֽנַחֲמֵֽנִי׃
22: גַּם־ אֲנִ֤י ׀ אוֹדְךָ֣ בִכְלִי־ נֶבֶל֮ אֲמִתְּךָ֪ אֱלֹ֫הָ֥י אֲזַמְּרָ֣ה לְךָ֣ בְכִנּ֑וֹר קְ֝ד֗וֹשׁ יִשְׂרָאֵֽל׃
23: תְּרַנֵּ֣נָּ֣ה שְׂ֭פָתַי כִּ֣י אֲזַמְּרָה־ לָּ֑ךְ וְ֝נַפְשִׁ֗י אֲשֶׁ֣ר פָּדִֽיתָ׃
24: גַּם־ לְשׁוֹנִ֗י כָּל־ הַ֭יּוֹם תֶּהְגֶּ֣ה צִדְקָתֶ֑ךָ כִּי־ בֹ֥שׁוּ כִֽי־ חָ֝פְר֗וּ מְבַקְשֵׁ֥י רָעָתִֽי׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 71

## Scene 1: Cry for Deliverance and Protection

> "In you, Lord, I have taken refuge; let me never be put to shame. In your righteousness, rescue me and deliver me; turn your ear to me and save me." - Psalm 71:1-2 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist fervently cries out to the Lord for deliverance and protection.
- Imagery:
  - Taking refuge in the Lord: The psalmist seeks shelter and safety in the Lord, finding solace in His presence.
  - Righteousness of God: The psalmist appeals to the righteousness of God, trusting that He will act justly on their behalf.
  - Turning the ear: Symbolizes God's attentiveness and willingness to listen to the psalmist's plea.
  - Salvation: The psalmist longs to be saved from their troubles, looking to God as the ultimate source of rescue.

## Scene 2: Divine Shield and Rock of Refuge

> "Be a sheltering rock for me always; You resolved to save me, for You are my rock and my stronghold." - Psalm 71:3 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist asks the Lord to be a constant source of protection and salvation, likening Him to a sheltering rock.
- Imagery:
  - Sheltering rock: God is portrayed as a solid and unmovable rock, providing a secure foundation and refuge for the psalmist.
  - Constant presence: The psalmist desires God's protection and salvation to be ongoing, never-ending.
  - Rock and stronghold: These terms emphasize the strength, stability, and immovability of God as a place of safety.

## Scene 3: Confidence in God from Youth

> "You were my hope, O Lord God, my trust since I was a youth." - Psalm 71:5 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist recalls their unwavering trust and hope in the Lord from their youth.
- Imagery:
  - Hope and trust: The psalmist acknowledges their reliance on God, finding hope and trust in Him throughout their life.
  - Youth: Symbolizes a time of innocence and vulnerability, highlighting the psalmist's deep-rooted faith in God from a young age.

## Scene 4: God's Protection Throughout Life

> "For You are my hope, Lord God, my trust from my youth. I have leaned on You from birth; You drew me from my mother's womb; my praise is always for You." - Psalm 71:5-6 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist praises God for His continuous protection and acknowledges their dependence on Him from birth.
- Imagery:
  - Leaning on God: The psalmist relies on God's support and strength, leaning on Him for guidance and protection.
  - Drawing from the womb: Symbolizes God's intimate involvement in the psalmist's life, from the very beginning.
  - Praise: The psalmist expresses their unwavering adoration and gratitude towards God.

## Scene 5: A Sign to Many

> "I have become a sign to many, for you are my strong refuge." - Psalm 71:7 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges that their life has become a testimony and example of God's faithfulness and protection.
- Imagery:
  - Sign: The psalmist's life serves as a visible representation of God's strength and refuge to others.
  - Strong refuge: God's unwavering support and protection are emphasized, highlighting His ability to provide safety to those who seek Him.

## Scene 6: Abandoned by Others, but Not Forsaken by God

> "Do not cast me away when I am old; do not forsake me when my strength is gone." - Psalm 71:9 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist appeals to God, expressing their fear of abandonment and the need for His continued presence.
- Imagery:
  - Casting away: The psalmist fears being rejected or discarded in their old age.
  - Forsakenness: The psalmist pleads for God's unfailing presence and support, even in their weakened state.

## Scene 7: Enemies Plotting Against the Psalmist

> "For my enemies speak against me, those who keep watch for my life conspire together, saying, 'God has forsaken them; pursue and seize them, for no one will rescue them.'" - Psalm 71:10-11 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The enemies of the psalmist are actively plotting against them, spreading false accusations and doubting God's faithfulness.
- Imagery:
  - Enemies speaking: Symbolizes the verbal attacks and slander directed towards the psalmist.
  - Watching and conspiring: Portrays the enemies actively working together to devise plans against the psalmist's life.
  - God's forsakenness: The enemies question the psalmist's relationship with God, doubting His presence and deliverance.
  - No rescue: The enemies express their belief that no one will come to the psalmist's aid, implying hopelessness and isolation.

## Scene 8: Sustained by God's Strength

> "But as for me, I will hope continually and will praise You yet more and more. My mouth shall tell of Your righteousness and of Your salvation all the day, for their number is past my knowledge." - Psalm 71:14-15 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist declares their unwavering hope, praise, and commitment to proclaiming God's righteousness and salvation.
- Imagery:
  - Continual hope and praise: The psalmist's devotion and trust in God are depicted as lasting and unending.
  - Mouth declaring: Symbolizes the psalmist's resolve to constantly proclaim the goodness of God and His saving acts.
  - Abundance: Highlights the overwhelming magnitude of God's righteousness and salvation, surpassing the psalmist's comprehension.

## Scene 9: God's Faithfulness from Old Age

> "Even when I am old and gray, do not forsake me, my God, till I declare your power to the next generation, your mighty acts to all who are to come." - Psalm 71:18 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist expresses their desire for God's continual presence, emphasizing their role in passing on God's power and mighty acts to future generations.
- Imagery:
  - Old and gray: Symbolizes the psalmist's advanced age and vulnerability, invoking God's presence and support in their later years.
  - Power and mighty acts: Relies on God's strength and the demonstration of His miraculous deeds as a means to inspire and teach future generations.

## Scene 10: Awaiting God's Justice on the Wicked

> "With the lyre I will give thanks to You, O Lord, my God, in the secret place. Why should I fear in the days of evil, when iniquity dogs my heel round about? They that are hostile to me stumble and fall." - Psalm 71:22-24 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist expresses gratitude to the Lord and eagerly awaits His righteous judgment on the wicked.
- Imagery:
  - Lyre and thanksgiving: Represents the psalmist's worshipful response and gratitude towards God.
  - Secret place: Symbolizes a personal and intimate setting where the psalmist offers praise to God.
  - Days of evil: Refers to times of distress and injustice, during which the psalmist trusts in God's protection.
  - Iniquity dogs: Depicts the relentless pursuit and opposition of the wicked towards the psalmist.
  - Hostile stumbling: The psalmist prophesies the downfall and defeat of their adversaries.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict the deep faith and dependence of the psalmist on the Lord, who is portrayed as a refuge, rock, and stronghold. The psalmist seeks God's protection, especially in times of trouble, and relies on His righteousness and salvation. The imagery of youth, mother's womb, and old age highlights the psalmist's lifelong trust in God. Despite facing enemies and doubts, the psalmist remains steadfast in their hope, continually praising God and declaring His power. There is a strong emphasis on passing on the knowledge and experience of God's faithfulness to future generations. The imagery in this Psalm encourages believers to seek refuge in God, trust in His deliverance, and declare His mighty acts throughout their lives.
