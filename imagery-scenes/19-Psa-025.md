# Psalm 25
1: לְדָוִ֡ד אֵלֶ֥יךָ יְ֝הוָ֗ה נַפְשִׁ֥י אֶשָּֽׂא׃
2: אֱֽלֹהַ֗י בְּךָ֣ בָ֭טַחְתִּי אַל־ אֵב֑וֹשָׁה אַל־ יַֽעַלְצ֖וּ אֹיְבַ֣י לִֽי׃
3: גַּ֣ם כָּל־ קֹ֭וֶיךָ לֹ֣א יֵבֹ֑שׁוּ יֵ֝בֹ֗שׁוּ הַבּוֹגְדִ֥ים רֵיקָֽם׃
4: דְּרָכֶ֣יךָ יְ֭הוָה הוֹדִיעֵ֑נִי אֹ֖רְחוֹתֶ֣יךָ לַמְּדֵֽנִי׃
5: הַדְרִ֘יכֵ֤נִי בַאֲמִתֶּ֨ךָ ׀ וְֽלַמְּדֵ֗נִי כִּֽי־ אַ֭תָּה אֱלֹהֵ֣י יִשְׁעִ֑י אוֹתְךָ֥ קִ֝וִּ֗יתִי כָּל־ הַיּֽוֹם׃
6: זְכֹר־ רַחֲמֶ֣יךָ יְ֭הוָה וַחֲסָדֶ֑יךָ כִּ֖י מֵעוֹלָ֣ם הֵֽמָּה׃
7: חַטֹּ֤אות נְעוּרַ֨י ׀ וּפְשָׁעַ֗י אַל־ תִּ֫זְכֹּ֥ר כְּחַסְדְּךָ֥ זְכָר־ לִי־ אַ֑תָּה לְמַ֖עַן טוּבְךָ֣ יְהוָֽה׃
8: טוֹב־ וְיָשָׁ֥ר יְהוָ֑ה עַל־ כֵּ֤ן יוֹרֶ֖ה חַטָּאִ֣ים בַּדָּֽרֶךְ׃
9: יַדְרֵ֣ךְ עֲ֭נָוִים בַּמִּשְׁפָּ֑ט וִֽילַמֵּ֖ד עֲנָוִ֣ים דַּרְכּֽוֹ׃
10: כָּל־ אָרְח֣וֹת יְ֭הוָה חֶ֣סֶד וֶאֱמֶ֑ת לְנֹצְרֵ֥י בְ֝רִית֗וֹ וְעֵדֹתָֽיו׃
11: לְמַֽעַן־ שִׁמְךָ֥ יְהוָ֑ה וְֽסָלַחְתָּ֥ לַ֝עֲוֺנִ֗י כִּ֣י רַב־ הֽוּא׃
12: מִי־ זֶ֣ה הָ֭אִישׁ יְרֵ֣א יְהוָ֑ה י֝וֹרֶ֗נּוּ בְּדֶ֣רֶךְ יִבְחָֽר׃
13: נַ֭פְשׁוֹ בְּט֣וֹב תָּלִ֑ין וְ֝זַרְע֗וֹ יִ֣ירַשׁ אָֽרֶץ׃
14: ס֣וֹד יְ֭הוָה לִירֵאָ֑יו וּ֝בְרִית֗וֹ לְהוֹדִיעָֽם׃
15: עֵינַ֣י תָּ֭מִיד אֶל־ יְהוָ֑ה כִּ֤י הֽוּא־ יוֹצִ֖יא מֵרֶ֣שֶׁת רַגְלָֽי׃
16: פְּנֵה־ אֵלַ֥י וְחָנֵּ֑נִי כִּֽי־ יָחִ֖יד וְעָנִ֣י אָֽנִי׃
17: צָר֣וֹת לְבָבִ֣י הִרְחִ֑יבוּ מִ֝מְּצֽוּקוֹתַ֗י הוֹצִיאֵֽנִי׃
18: רְאֵ֣ה עָ֭נְיִי וַעֲמָלִ֑י וְ֝שָׂ֗א לְכָל־ חַטֹּאותָֽי׃
19: רְאֵֽה־ אוֹיְבַ֥י כִּי־ רָ֑בּוּ וְשִׂנְאַ֖ת חָמָ֣ס שְׂנֵאֽוּנִי׃
20: שָׁמְרָ֣ה נַ֭פְשִׁי וְהַצִּילֵ֑נִי אַל־ אֵ֝ב֗וֹשׁ כִּֽי־ חָסִ֥יתִי בָֽךְ׃
21: תֹּם־ וָיֹ֥שֶׁר יִצְּר֑וּנִי כִּ֝֗י קִוִּיתִֽיךָ׃
22: פְּדֵ֣ה אֱ֭לֹהִים אֶת־ יִשְׂרָאֵ֑ל מִ֝כֹּ֗ל צָֽרוֹתָיו׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 25

## Scene 1: Plea for Guidance and Deliverance

> To You, O Lord, I lift up my soul. O my God, I trust in You; let me not be ashamed; let not my enemies triumph over me. - Psalm 25:1-2 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist approaches the Lord, lifting up his soul in humble surrender.
- Imagery:
  - Lifting up of the soul symbolizes the psalmist's deep longing and surrender to the Lord.
  - Trusting in God reflects the psalmist's reliance on divine assistance.
  - The plea to not be ashamed and to not let enemies triumph over him indicates the psalmist's desire for protection and vindication.

## Scene 2: Teach Me Your Ways

> Show me Your ways, O Lord; teach me Your paths. Lead me in Your truth and teach me, for You are the God of my salvation; on You I wait all the day. - Psalm 25:4-5 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist seeks guidance and instruction from the Lord.
- Imagery:
  - Showing the ways and teaching the paths expresses the psalmist's desire to understand and follow God's ways.
  - The request to be led in truth and taught emphasizes the psalmist's longing for spiritual direction and wisdom.
  - Waiting on the Lord throughout the day signifies the psalmist's trust and dependence on God's provision and guidance.

## Scene 3: Remember Your Compassion and Love

> Remember, O Lord, Your compassion and Your lovingkindnesses, for they have been from old. Remember not the sins of my youth or my transgressions; according to Your lovingkindness remember me, for Your goodness' sake, O Lord. - Psalm 25:6-7 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist implores the Lord to remember His compassion and lovingkindness towards him.
- Imagery:
  - Remembering God's compassion and lovingkindness reflects the psalmist's appeal for God's grace and mercy.
  - The plea to not remember the sins and transgressions of the youth signifies the psalmist's desire for forgiveness.
  - The reference to God's goodness emphasizes the psalmist's confidence in God's character and faithfulness.

## Scene 4: God's Instructions and Covenant

> Good and upright is the Lord; therefore He gives instruction to sinners in the way. He guides the humble in justice, and He teaches the humble His way. - Psalm 25:8-9 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges God's goodness and righteousness and the resulting instructions given.
- Imagery:
  - God giving instructions to sinners symbolizes His willingness to guide and correct those who are humble and repentant.
  - God guiding the humble in justice signifies His desire to lead them towards righteousness and fairness.
  - Teaching the humble His way represents God's desire for His people to walk in His paths and follow His commands.

## Scene 5: Divine Guidance and Deliverance

> All the paths of the Lord are lovingkindness and truth to those who keep His covenant and His testimonies. - Psalm 25:10 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges the nature of God's paths for those who are faithful.
- Imagery:
  - The paths of the Lord being characterized by lovingkindness and truth signifies the divine guidance and protection for those who are loyal to God.
  - Keeping God's covenant and testimonies reflects the psalmist's commitment to following God's commands and remaining faithful to His covenant.

## Scene 6: Prayer for Mercy and Redemption

> For Your name's sake, O Lord, You will pardon my iniquity, for it is great. - Psalm 25:11 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist expresses trust in God's forgiveness and mercy.
- Imagery:
  - The mention of God's name emphasizes His reputation and character as a merciful and forgiving God.
  - The psalmist's acknowledgement of their great iniquity reflects humility and recognition of their need for redemption.

## Scene 7: The Fear and Friendship of the Lord

> Who is the man who fears the Lord? He will instruct him in the way he should choose. His soul will lodge in prosperity, and his seed will inherit the earth. - Psalm 25:12-13 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist ponders the benefits of fearing and being a friend of the Lord.
- Imagery:
  - Fearing the Lord signifies reverence and obedience, leading to divine instruction and guidance.
  - The soul lodging in prosperity indicates the blessings and well-being experienced by one who fears the Lord.
  - The inheritance of the earth by the seed (descendants) symbolizes the lasting legacy and blessings extended to the righteous.

## Scene 8: The Secret of the Lord

> The secret of the Lord is for those who fear Him, and He will make them know His covenant. - Psalm 25:14 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist acknowledges the privilege of knowing the secret (intimate knowledge) of the Lord.
- Imagery:
  - The secret of the Lord conveys the hidden wisdom and understanding reserved for those who fear and honor Him.
  - Making them know His covenant signifies God's revelation of His promises and divine plan to those who are faithful.

## Scene 9: Need for Deliverance and Redemption

> My eyes are always toward the Lord, For He will pluck my feet out of the net. - Psalm 25:15 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist expresses reliance on the Lord for deliverance and rescue.
- Imagery:
  - My eyes are always toward the Lord portrays the psalmist's unwavering focus and dependence on God for help.
  - The mention of being plucked out of the net represents divine intervention and liberation from the schemes and traps of the enemy.

## Scene 10: Plea for Divine Favor and Redemption

> Turn to me and be gracious to me, for I am lonely and afflicted. The troubles of my heart are enlarged; bring me out of my distresses. - Psalm 25:16-17 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist cries out for God's favor and redemption in the midst of loneliness and affliction.
- Imagery:
  - The plea for God to turn and be gracious emphasizes the psalmist's desperate need for divine intervention and mercy.
  - The troubles of the heart being enlarged signifies the depth and intensity of the psalmist's distress.
  - Bringing the psalmist out of distresses symbolizes the desired deliverance and restoration.

## Scene 11: Guarding the Covenant

> Look upon my affliction and my trouble, and forgive all my sins. - Psalm 25:18 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist calls upon God to see their affliction, trouble, and grant forgiveness.
- Imagery:
  - The plea for God to look upon the affliction and trouble signifies the psalmist's desire for divine attention and intervention in their distress.
  - Forgiving all sins portrays the psalmist's recognition of their own shortcomings and the need for divine forgiveness and reconciliation.

## Scene 12: Enemies and Deliverance

> Consider my enemies, for they are many; and they hate me with cruel hatred. Keep my soul, and deliver me; let me not be ashamed, for I take refuge in You. - Psalm 25:19-20 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist appeals to God to consider their numerous enemies and to provide protection and deliverance.
- Imagery:
  - Enemies being many and hating with cruel hatred reveal the psalmist's feeling of vulnerability and the intensity of the opposition they face.
  - Keeping the soul signifies divine preservation and safeguarding.
  - Taking refuge in God reflects the psalmist's trust and dependency on God's protection and deliverance.

## Scene 13: Integrity and Redemption

> Let integrity and uprightness preserve me, for I wait for You. - Psalm 25:21 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist seeks preservation through integrity and uprightness while waiting on the Lord.
- Imagery:
  - Integrity and uprightness as preservers depict the psalmist's commitment to righteous living and personal integrity.
  - Waiting for the Lord reflects the psalmist's patient trust and anticipation for God's intervention and redemption.

## Scene 14: Prayer for Redemption and Deliverance

> Redeem Israel, O God, out of all his troubles. - Psalm 25:22 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The psalmist concludes by praying for the redemption and deliverance of Israel from their troubles.
- Imagery:
  - Redeeming Israel signifies the psalmist's desire for God's salvation and restoration of His chosen people.
  - Troubles represent the challenges and hardships faced by Israel as a nation.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict the psalmist's plea for guidance, instruction, and divine intervention. The images convey a deep longing for God's ways, a reliance on His compassion and lovingkindness, and a humble recognition of one's own need for redemption. The psalmist acknowledges the benefits of fearing and being a friend of the Lord, and expresses trust in God's protection and deliverance. They also cry out for divine favor, forgiveness, and deliverance in the midst of affliction and trouble. The overall message is one of hope, trust, and dependence on God for guidance, redemption, and deliverance.
