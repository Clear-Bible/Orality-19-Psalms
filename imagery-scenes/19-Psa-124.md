# Psalm 124
1: שִׁ֥יר הַֽמַּעֲל֗וֹת לְדָ֫וִ֥ד לוּלֵ֣י יְ֭הוָה שֶׁהָ֣יָה לָ֑נוּ יֹֽאמַר־ נָ֝א יִשְׂרָאֵֽל׃
2: לוּלֵ֣י יְ֭הוָה שֶׁהָ֣יָה לָ֑נוּ בְּק֖וּם עָלֵ֣ינוּ אָדָֽם׃
3: אֲ֭זַי חַיִּ֣ים בְּלָע֑וּנוּ בַּחֲר֖וֹת אַפָּ֣ם בָּֽנוּ׃
4: אֲ֭זַי הַמַּ֣יִם שְׁטָפ֑וּנוּ נַ֗֜חְלָה עָבַ֥ר עַל־ נַפְשֵֽׁנוּ׃
5: אֲ֭זַי עָבַ֣ר עַל־ נַפְשֵׁ֑נוּ הַ֝מַּ֗יִם הַזֵּֽידוֹנִֽים׃
6: בָּר֥וּךְ יְהוָ֑ה שֶׁלֹּ֥א נְתָנָ֥נוּ טֶ֝֗רֶף לְשִׁנֵּיהֶֽם׃
7: נַפְשֵׁ֗נוּ כְּצִפּ֥וֹר נִמְלְטָה֮ מִפַּ֪ח י֫וֹקְשִׁ֥ים הַפַּ֥ח נִשְׁבָּ֗ר וַאֲנַ֥חְנוּ נִמְלָֽטְנוּ׃
8: עֶ֭זְרֵנוּ בְּשֵׁ֣ם יְהוָ֑ה עֹ֝שֵׂ֗ה שָׁמַ֥יִם וָאָֽרֶץ׃

# Imagery Outline of Psalm 124

## Scene 1: The Threat of Enemies

> If it had not been the Lord who was on our side— let Israel now say—if it had not been the Lord who was on our side when people rose up against us, then they would have swallowed us alive, when their anger was kindled against us; then the flood would have swept us away, the torrent would have gone over us; then over us would have gone the raging waters. - Psalm 124:1-5 (WLC) 

- Scene:
  - The people of Israel facing a threat of enemies rising up against them.
- Imagery:
  - Being swallowed alive by the enemies' anger.
  - The image of a flood sweeping them away.
  - The raging waters going over them.

## Scene 2: Rescued by the Lord

> Blessed be the Lord, who has not given us as prey to their teeth! We have escaped like a bird from the snare of the fowlers; the snare is broken, and we have escaped! Our help is in the name of the Lord, who made heaven and earth. - Psalm 124:6-8 (WLC)

- Scene:
  - The people of Israel praising and giving thanks to the Lord for their rescue.
- Imagery:
  - Being delivered from being prey for the enemies' teeth.
  - Escaping from a snare, like a bird being freed.
  - The snare being broken, symbolizing their complete deliverance.
  - Recognizing that their help comes from the Lord, the Creator of heaven and earth.

## Summary

The scenes in this Psalm depict the people of Israel facing a threat from enemies, but being rescued by the Lord. The imagery of being swallowed alive, the flood, and the raging waters represent the danger they were in. However, they express gratitude for being delivered, comparing it to escaping the snare of a bird and recognizing that their help comes from the Lord, who is the creator of all things. This Psalm highlights the eudaimonic meaning of trust and reliance on the Lord in times of trouble, knowing that He is their deliverer.
